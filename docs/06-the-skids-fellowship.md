# The SKIDS Fellowship

The credential pathway by which a SKIDS pediatrician advances from
delivering Tier 1 protocols to running credentialed Tier 2 specialty
clinics.

---

## Why this exists

The Indian pediatric workforce produces roughly 1,500 new pediatricians
a year. Most complete MBBS → MD Paeds → general practice. Subspecialty
fellowship requires another 1–3 years of training that is usually
self-funded; most cannot afford it. The result is a profession in which
generalists outnumber subspecialists by an order of magnitude, and the
average pediatrician spends a decade as a generalist before they can
afford to subspecialise.

The SKIDS Fellowship reverses the order. A young pediatrician joins
SKIDS with one subspecialty credential — or none — and earns
additional credentials over time, funded by SKIDS, at partner training
institutions. Each new credential unlocks an additional Tier 2
specialty clinic at their SKIDS clinic, which adds a revenue line that
pays back the fellowship cost in 12–18 months at typical patient flow.

This is not philanthropy. It is structural.

- For SKIDS, the Fellowship is how the platform's clinical capability
  scales without requiring the network to recruit already-credentialed
  subspecialists (who are scarce, expensive, and often committed to
  hospital systems).
- For the pediatrician, it is the difference between a 10-year wait for
  subspecialty practice and a 3-year ladder.
- For the patient, it is access to subspecialty pediatric care in a
  primary-care setting, at a primary-care price point.

## How it works

### 1. Eligibility

A SKIDS pediatrician is eligible for the Fellowship after:
- Completing 12 months of SKIDS Tier 1 practice
- Demonstrating consistent protocol adherence in §12 quarterly reviews
- Passing a clinical knowledge assessment in the chosen subspecialty
- Securing a sponsoring letter from the SKIDS clinical leadership

### 2. Subspecialty selection

The SKIDS Fellowship initially supports the three Tier 2 services:

```
Pediatric Endocrinology
Pediatric Neurology
Pediatric Rheumatology
```

The Fellowship may expand to support advanced credentials in Tier 1
services where this would meaningfully upgrade the protocol's complex
tier — for example, a Pediatric Sleep Medicine fellowship to upgrade
B1-07 Sleep Clinic from primary-tier-only to credentialed sleep medicine.

### 3. Partner institutions

The Fellowship is delivered through partnership with:
- Indian institutes offering recognised pediatric subspecialty fellowships
  (DM / DNB programmes at PGI Chandigarh, AIIMS Delhi, CMC Vellore, KEM
  Mumbai, etc.)
- International programmes via remote / hybrid pathways where appropriate
  (US ABP-recognised fellowships, Royal College pathways)
- Direct mentorship arrangements with named senior subspecialists in the
  SKIDS network

The exact partner institutions are listed in a separate operations
document maintained by SKIDS clinical leadership. They may change as
new partnerships are formed.

### 4. Funding

SKIDS funds the fellowship in full:
- Tuition / training fees at partner institution
- Stipend during fellowship period (so the pediatrician's family income
  is preserved)
- Continuing locum coverage at their SKIDS clinic during fellowship
  periods (so their patient panel is not lost)

In return, the pediatrician commits to:
- Completing the fellowship to certification
- Returning to SKIDS practice for a defined period (typically 3 years
  post-fellowship)
- Activating the credentialed Tier 2 service at their SKIDS clinic
- Mentoring at least one junior SKIDS pediatrician through their own
  Fellowship pathway in the same subspecialty over the following 5 years

The commercial terms are documented separately in the SKIDS Fellowship
agreement; this protocol library document covers the clinical structure
only.

### 5. Activation of the Tier 2 service

When the pediatrician completes the fellowship and returns to practice:
- Their SKIDS clinic activates the relevant Tier 2 protocol (B2-XX)
- They are named as the protocol owner for that protocol
- They begin running the specialty clinic; they continue to deliver
  Tier 1 services as before
- Their compensation increases to reflect the credentialed service line

### 6. Path to senior protocol owner

A pediatrician with multiple SKIDS Fellowship credentials and 5+ years
of post-fellowship practice may become a **named protocol owner across
the network** — taking quarterly governance responsibility not just for
their own clinic but for one or more protocols across all SKIDS clinics
in their region. This is a leadership track, with corresponding
compensation.

## How the Fellowship appears in protocols

Every Tier 1 protocol's §12 (Quality and audit) ends with a reference
to the Fellowship pathway, where applicable. Format:

```
### 12.4 The SKIDS Fellowship pathway

Pediatricians delivering this Tier 1 protocol who wish to advance to
credentialed practice in [subspecialty] may apply for The SKIDS
Fellowship in [subspecialty]. See `docs/06-the-skids-fellowship.md`
for eligibility, partner institutions, and funding terms.

The credentialed pediatrician would then deliver:
- This Tier 1 protocol's complex-tier cases (currently routing to §7
  hard referral) without external referral
- The associated Tier 2 specialty clinic (B2-XX [Specialty])
- Protocol governance for this Tier 1 protocol across the SKIDS network
```

For protocols where there is no clean subspecialty advancement
(e.g., B1-22 Vaccination), the Fellowship reference is omitted.

## Investor and recruitment framing

Two things to note in how the Fellowship gets described externally.

**For investors**, the Fellowship is the structural answer to the
question "how does SKIDS scale clinical capability without facing the
subspecialist recruitment crisis that constrains every healthcare
network in India?" The answer: SKIDS doesn't try to recruit existing
subspecialists in volume. SKIDS turns its general-practice pediatricians
into subspecialists, in-house, on a defined ladder, with structural
loyalty.

**For pediatricians**, the Fellowship is the answer to "how do I get
from MD Paeds to subspecialty practice without spending a decade
generalising and self-funding fellowship?" The answer: SKIDS provides
the platform that makes general-tier work commercially viable from day
one, and funds the fellowship that lets you specialise on a 3-year
timeline rather than a 10-year one.

Both framings are honest and structurally correct.

---

## What this means for the protocol library

The Fellowship pathway is referenced in every Tier 1 protocol's §12 and
embedded in the design of every Tier 2 protocol. It is the institutional
glue that connects the protocol library to the human capital strategy
of the SKIDS platform.

When authoring a protocol, treat the Fellowship reference as a real
feature, not a footnote. A SKIDS pediatrician reading the §12 of a
Tier 1 protocol should see a clear path to becoming the credentialed
subspecialist who governs that protocol. That visibility is part of
what makes joining SKIDS structurally attractive.
