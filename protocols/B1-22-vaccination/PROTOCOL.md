# SKIDS Vaccination Clinic

> The SKIDS Vaccination Clinic delivers IAP IIS 2024-25-aligned
> immunisation, catch-up scheduling, HPV programme delivery, travel
> vaccination counselling, AEFI documentation and management, and
> vaccine-hesitancy engagement for children 0–18 years — integrated
> with every A1 periodic visit and cross-referenced by every B1
> protocol that depends on immunisation status.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Administers all vaccines on the IAP Immunisation Schedule (IIS) 2024-25 at age-appropriate periodic visits, including birth doses, primary series, boosters, and adolescent doses [IAP-IIS-2024].
- Reconciles vaccination status against IAP IIS at every A1 periodic visit and generates catch-up schedules for children with missed or delayed doses per IAP catch-up guidance.
- Delivers the HPV vaccination programme (quadrivalent or nonavalent, 2-dose or 3-dose per age at initiation) for boys and girls, cross-referenced with B1-13 Adolescent Clinic [IAP-IIS-2024].
- Administers Tdap booster, MenACWY (where indicated), and annual influenza vaccine for adolescents [IAP-IIS-2024].
- Counsels on and administers travel vaccines (typhoid Vi polysaccharide or conjugate, hepatitis A if not in routine schedule, yellow fever, meningococcal, JE, rabies pre-exposure) per destination risk profile.
- Documents and manages adverse events following immunisation (AEFI) per WHO/IAP classification; reports serious AEFI to the district AEFI surveillance system.
- Engages vaccine-hesitant families using IAP motivational-interviewing framework without coercion; documents declinations with specific vaccine declined and next planned conversation.
- Manages vaccination in special populations: preterm infants (chronological age dosing), immunocompromised children (live-vaccine contraindications per IAP/CDC), children with egg allergy (influenza/MMR/yellow fever risk stratification), children on immunosuppressive therapy.
- Maintains cold-chain integrity for all vaccine stock in the SKIDS clinic cold-chain unit (2–8°C monitored).
- Issues digital and printable vaccination certificates after every visit.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Administer investigational or unapproved vaccines outside IAP IIS and recognised travel-vaccine indications.
- Manage severe AEFI requiring hospitalisation beyond initial stabilisation and adrenaline administration (§7.1 to ER).
- Override parental vaccine refusal — the protocol documents refusal and re-engages per §10.5; disenrolment from SKIDS does not follow.
- Administer BCG (birth dose at birthing facility; SKIDS does not stock BCG due to multi-dose vial wastage constraints) [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm SKIDS network position on BCG stocking].
- Provide immunology workup for suspected primary immunodeficiency (§7.3 to pediatric immunology).

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (with birth-dose reconciliation at first SKIDS visit if not given at birthing facility).

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — IAP IIS NIS vaccines (UIP-equivalent doses) administered at periodic visits; vaccination-status reconciliation at every A1 visit.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — full IAP IIS including non-NIS vaccines (rotavirus, PCV, typhoid conjugate, varicella, hepatitis A, influenza annual); catch-up scheduling included.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — full IAP IIS + HPV programme (2-dose or 3-dose) + MenACWY + travel-vaccine counselling and administration + priority AEFI follow-up.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1. Every SKIDS pediatrician administers vaccines under this protocol. Cold-chain management is a clinic-operations dependency (SKIDS nurse + clinic manager). Protocol governance with named immunisation programme lead or pediatric infectious disease specialist. §7 escalates to ER (anaphylaxis), pediatric immunology (suspected PID), or pediatric infectious disease (complex AEFI, immunocompromised-child vaccine decisions).

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Vaccination status reconciliation

At every visit (A1 periodic or dedicated B1-22), perform:

1. **Card reconciliation.** Compare the physical IAP IIS card (parent-held) with the Companion PHR vaccination record. Resolve discrepancies (doses given elsewhere not yet logged; doses logged but not on card).
2. **Age-appropriate schedule check.** Against IAP IIS 2024-25: identify doses due at this visit, doses overdue, and doses upcoming.
3. **Catch-up assessment.** For children with missed doses: apply IAP catch-up guidance — minimum intervals between doses, maximum ages for catch-up, accelerated schedules for children significantly behind.
4. **Contraindication screen.** For each vaccine due:
   - Acute moderate-severe illness with fever → defer (mild illness without fever is NOT a contraindication).
   - Previous anaphylaxis to that vaccine or a component → absolute contraindication; substitute where available.
   - Immunocompromised state → live-vaccine contraindications (MMR, varicella, OPV, BCG, LAIV, rotavirus in SCID, yellow fever).
   - Egg allergy → risk-stratify for influenza (administer in clinic with 30-min observation if mild egg allergy; severe egg allergy → §7.3 for allergy-specialist-supervised administration); MMR and varicella are safe regardless of egg allergy [IAP-IIS-2024].
   - Pregnancy (adolescent) → no live vaccines; Tdap recommended in 3rd trimester (route to obstetric provider); inactivated vaccines safe.
   - Age-specific contraindications: pertussis-containing vaccines contraindicated if prior encephalopathy within 7 days of pertussis dose.

### 4.2 AEFI history assessment

If parent reports a prior adverse event:

1. Classify the prior event per WHO-AEFI-2019:
   - **Minor / expected:** injection-site pain, low-grade fever, irritability → reassure; not a contraindication.
   - **Moderate:** persistent crying > 3h, fever > 40.5°C, HHE (hypotonic-hyporesponsive episode) → document; generally not a contraindication to subsequent doses but requires observation period extension to 60 minutes.
   - **Serious:** anaphylaxis, encephalopathy, vaccine-strain disease → absolute contraindication to that vaccine; consider substitute or §7.3 referral.
2. Document the AEFI in the PHR with WHO causality classification.

### 4.3 Special-population assessment

| Population | Assessment | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Preterm (< 37 weeks) | Dose by chronological age, not corrected age [IAP-IIS-2024] | Standard schedule; birth-dose hepatitis B within 12h regardless of weight |
| Immunocompromised (primary or secondary) | Identify specific immunodeficiency; assess live-vaccine eligibility | No live vaccines if severely immunocompromised; inactivated vaccines per schedule; §7.3 to pediatric immunology if PID suspected |
| HIV-exposed / HIV-infected | CD4 count and viral load; HAART status | If CD4 ≥ 15%: MMR and varicella can be given; if CD4 < 15%: no live vaccines; BCG per national programme guidance |
| Post-HSCT / organ transplant | Time since transplant; immunosuppression status | Re-vaccination per transplant protocol; §7.3 to transplant team |
| On immunosuppressive therapy | Drug, dose, duration | Live vaccines deferred until ≥ 3 months off therapy (≥ 6 months for rituximab); inactivated vaccines per schedule but may have reduced immunogenicity |
| Egg allergy | Severity of egg reaction | See §4.1 contraindication screen |

### 4.4 HPV programme eligibility

- Age 9–14 years: 2-dose schedule (0, 6–12 months). Quadrivalent or nonavalent per availability.
- Age ≥ 15 years: 3-dose schedule (0, 1–2 months, 6 months).
- Catch-up through age 26 (per WHO-HPV-2022; IAP recommends through adolescence; SKIDS extends per WHO for catch-up in young adults attending with adolescent siblings).
- Prior sexual activity is NOT a contraindication — counsel that vaccine remains beneficial.
- Cross-reference: B1-13 §6.1 HPV programme delivery at adolescent visits.

### 4.5 Travel-vaccine assessment

1. Identify destination, duration, activities (rural/urban), and departure date.
2. Cross-reference with CDC-Travel-2024 and IAP guidance:
   - **Yellow fever:** required for travel to endemic zones; administer ≥ 10 days before departure; live vaccine — contraindications per §4.1.
   - **Typhoid:** Vi polysaccharide (≥ 2 years) or conjugate (≥ 6 months) for travel to South Asia, SE Asia, Africa, Latin America.
   - **Hepatitis A:** if not in routine schedule or if not yet completed.
   - **Meningococcal (MenACWY):** for Hajj/Umrah, sub-Saharan Africa meningitis belt, college dormitory.
   - **JE (Japanese encephalitis):** for travel to endemic rural areas of South/Southeast Asia with ≥ 1 month stay.
   - **Rabies pre-exposure:** for travel to areas with high rabies risk and limited access to post-exposure prophylaxis.
   - **Cholera oral vaccine:** for travel to active cholera zones (limited indication; counsel on water/food hygiene).
3. Generate travel-vaccine plan with timeline to departure; administer at B1-22 visit or schedule series.

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

B1-22 does not use a traditional mild/moderate/severe severity grading like other B1 protocols. The "severity" dimension for vaccination is the degree of schedule deviation and the presence of AEFI risk or special-population complexity.

### 5.1 Schedule status grading

| Status | Definition | Intervention tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Up-to-date** | All IAP IIS doses given on time | §6.1 Light — administer age-due doses at periodic visit |
| **Behind (≤ 6 months)** | 1–3 doses missed within past 6 months | §6.2 Standard — catch-up schedule; dedicated B1-22 visit |
| **Severely behind (> 6 months or ≥ 4 doses)** | ≥ 4 doses missed OR > 6 months behind on primary series | §6.2 Standard — accelerated catch-up; multiple visits over 4–8 weeks |
| **Never vaccinated** | No vaccination history; child ≥ 12 months | §6.2 Standard — full catch-up per IAP accelerated schedule |
| **Outbreak context** | Measles / pertussis / diphtheria outbreak declared in region | §6.2 Standard — same-week catch-up of outbreak-relevant doses regardless of other schedule status |

### 5.2 AEFI risk grading

| Risk | Definition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard risk** | No prior AEFI or minor/expected AEFI only | Standard 30-min observation |
| **Elevated risk** | Prior moderate AEFI (HHE, fever > 40.5°C, persistent crying) | 60-min observation; pre-medication with paracetamol if prior febrile AEFI |
| **High risk** | Prior serious AEFI to a vaccine component; severe egg allergy requiring that vaccine | §7.3 to allergy specialist or pediatric immunology for supervised administration |

### 5.3 Special-population grading

| Complexity | Definition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Healthy, term, no immunodeficiency | §6.1 |
| **Modified schedule** | Preterm, mild immunocompromise, on low-dose immunosuppression | §6.5 modified schedule per §4.3 |
| **Complex** | Severe immunodeficiency, post-transplant, on high-dose immunosuppression, suspected PID | §7.3 to pediatric immunology |

### 5.4 Boundary rules

- A child who is behind on vaccines but otherwise healthy is §6.2, not §7 — catch-up is within Tier 1 scope.
- A child with a suspected primary immunodeficiency (recurrent severe infections, family history of PID, absent thymic shadow) is §7.3 to pediatric immunology before any live-vaccine administration.
- An outbreak-context flag overrides routine scheduling — same-week catch-up for outbreak-relevant doses.
- Vaccine refusal is not a severity grade; it is a parent-communication pathway (§10.5).

**Administration protocol:**
1. Confirm identity, age, vaccine due, contraindication screen (§4.1).
2. Prepare vaccine per manufacturer instructions; verify cold-chain integrity (2–8°C; check VVM if applicable).
3. Administer at correct site, route, and dose.
4. Document in Companion: vaccine name, manufacturer, lot number, expiry date, dose number, route, site, administering nurse/doctor, time.
5. Observe for 30 minutes post-administration (60 minutes if elevated AEFI risk per §5.2).
6. Issue updated vaccination certificate (digital + printable).
7. Schedule next dose(s) per IAP IIS.

**Dispensary stock:**
- All IAP IIS vaccines per current schedule (NIS and non-NIS).
- Adrenaline 1:1000 (1 mg/mL) auto-injector or ampoule + syringe — mandatory at every vaccination station.
- Paracetamol syrup and tablets for post-vaccination fever guidance.
- Ice packs (analgesia guidance for parents).
- Needle sizes: 25G 16mm (infants), 25G 25mm (children), 23G 25mm (adolescents/deltoid).

**Cold-chain:**
- Dedicated vaccine refrigerator (2–8°C) with continuous digital temperature monitoring and alarm.
- Temperature log reviewed daily by clinic nurse; excursion > 2h outside 2–8°C = quarantine affected stock + contact manufacturer for viability guidance.
- No vaccine stored in door compartments or freezer.
- Diluent stored at 2–8°C or room temperature per manufacturer; never frozen.
- Open multi-dose vials (where used): follow WHO open-vial policy — discard at end of session or per manufacturer guidance.

### 6.2 Standard tier (catch-up vaccination)

**Catch-up scheduling per IAP catch-up guidance:**
1. List all missed doses.
2. Apply minimum intervals between doses (IAP/CDC catch-up table).
3. Schedule accelerated catch-up: up to 4 injectable vaccines per visit (2 per limb, different sites) + oral vaccines.
4. Generate a catch-up calendar with visit dates; load into Companion for scheduling.
5. Administer at each catch-up visit; reconcile remaining doses.

**Multiple-injection visit guidance:**
- Maximum 4 injectable vaccines per visit (anatomically distinct sites).
- If > 4 injectable vaccines needed, split across 2 visits separated by minimum interval (usually 4 weeks).
- Counsel parent on expected increase in local reactions; provide paracetamol guidance.
- Live injectable vaccines (MMR, varicella) given on the same day OR separated by ≥ 28 days; do not give at 1–27 day intervals.

**Catch-up for specific vaccines:**
- **HepB:** 3-dose series; minimum interval dose 1→2: 4 weeks; dose 2→3: 8 weeks (and ≥ 16 weeks after dose 1). No maximum age.
- **DTaP/DTwP:** primary 3-dose + 2 boosters; minimum intervals per IAP. If ≥ 7 years and never received: use Tdap (not DTaP).
- **IPV:** primary 3-dose + 2 boosters; minimum intervals per IAP.
- **MMR:** 2 doses; minimum interval 4 weeks. No maximum age.
- **Varicella:** 2 doses; minimum interval 12 weeks (< 13 years) or 4 weeks (≥ 13 years).
- **PCV:** depends on age at first dose; follow IAP age-based catch-up table.
- **Rotavirus:** maximum age for first dose 15 weeks 0 days; maximum age for last dose 8 months 0 days. Do NOT catch up beyond maximum age.
- **HPV:** per §4.4.

### 6.3 Travel vaccination

Per §4.5 assessment. Administered at B1-22 visit with standard observation protocol. Travel vaccines not part of routine IAP IIS are charged per-dose regardless of subscription tier.

**Pre-travel timeline:**
- Ideally ≥ 4–6 weeks before departure.
- If < 2 weeks: administer what is possible; counsel on limitations.
- Yellow fever: must be ≥ 10 days before arrival in endemic zone (international requirement).

### 6.4 Contraindications and precautions

| Vaccine | Contraindication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Any vaccine | Anaphylaxis to prior dose or component | Absolute contraindication; substitute vaccine where available; if no substitute, §7.3 |
| Any vaccine | Acute moderate-severe febrile illness | Defer until recovery; mild illness is NOT a reason to defer |
| Live vaccines (MMR, varicella, OPV, BCG, LAIV, rotavirus, yellow fever) | Severe immunodeficiency (primary or secondary) | Contraindicated; §7.3 to immunology for vaccine plan |
| Live vaccines | Pregnancy | Contraindicated; Tdap in 3rd trimester is inactivated and recommended |
| Pertussis-containing (DTwP/DTaP/Tdap) | Encephalopathy within 7 days of prior pertussis dose | Omit pertussis component; give DT or Td only |
| Rotavirus | SCID; history of intussusception | Contraindicated |
| Influenza (egg-based) | Severe egg allergy with anaphylaxis | §7.3 to allergy specialist for supervised administration; egg-free formulation where available |
| Yellow fever | Age < 9 months; immunocompromised; severe egg allergy | Contraindicated; issue waiver letter for travel if needed |

### 6.5 Special-population modified schedules

- **Preterm:** chronological age dosing; no delay. HepB birth dose within 12h regardless of weight. If birth weight < 2 kg: HepB birth dose does NOT count as dose 1 — give 3 additional doses starting at 1 month [IAP-IIS-2024].
- **Immunocompromised (mild):** inactivated vaccines per schedule; defer live vaccines until immunology clearance.
- **Post-splenectomy / functional asplenia:** PCV-23 + MenACWY + annual influenza; Hib if not previously given.
- **Chronic kidney disease:** HepB higher-dose series; annual influenza; PCV.
- **On corticosteroids:** ≥ 2 mg/kg/day prednisone equivalent for ≥ 14 days = defer live vaccines until ≥ 1 month off therapy.

### 6.6 Anaphylaxis management at vaccination station

Every SKIDS vaccination station must have:
- Adrenaline 1:1000 (1 mg/mL) — weight-banded dosing: 0.01 mL/kg IM (max 0.3 mL for < 30 kg; max 0.5 mL for ≥ 30 kg) [WAO-Anaphylaxis-2020].
- Oxygen supply.
- Pulse oximeter.
- Suction.
- IV access kit.

If anaphylaxis occurs post-vaccination:
1. Administer adrenaline IM anterolateral thigh immediately.
2. Position supine with legs elevated (unless vomiting or dyspnoeic).
3. Call emergency services.
4. Repeat adrenaline at 5 minutes if no improvement.
5. Establish IV access; normal saline bolus 20 mL/kg if hypotensive.
6. Transfer to ER — §7.1.
7. Document as serious AEFI; report per §6.7.

### 6.7 AEFI documentation and reporting

**For every AEFI (minor or serious):**
1. Document in Companion PHR: vaccine(s) given, time of onset, symptoms, classification (minor / moderate / serious), outcome.
2. Update the child's vaccination record with AEFI flag for that vaccine.

**For serious AEFI (anaphylaxis, encephalopathy, vaccine-strain disease, hospitalisation, death):**
1. Complete the national AEFI reporting form (AEFI Form 1).
2. Submit to district immunisation officer within 24h.
3. Notify protocol owner.
4. §7.3 referral for investigation if cause unclear.

### 6.8 Tapering / stopping

Not applicable — vaccination is episodic, not chronic. Catch-up schedules have defined endpoints. Annual influenza continues annually.

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)

- [ ] **Anaphylaxis post-vaccination** (onset within minutes to 4h; urticaria + angioedema + respiratory compromise or hypotension). → Adrenaline IM immediately at vaccination station + ER transfer.
- [ ] **Severe anaphylaxis not responding to 2 doses of adrenaline.** → ER with ongoing resuscitation.
- [ ] **Vaccine-strain disease with systemic toxicity** (e.g., disseminated BCG in immunocompromised child identified at follow-up). → ER + pediatric infectious disease.
- [ ] **Intussusception within 21 days of rotavirus vaccine** (episodic inconsolable crying, currant-jelly stool, abdominal mass). → ER + pediatric surgery.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Encephalopathy within 7 days of pertussis-containing vaccine** (altered consciousness, seizures > 24h, focal neurological deficit). → Pediatric neurology / B2-02 (where available).
- [ ] **Hypotonic-hyporesponsive episode (HHE) with prolonged or recurrent episodes.** → Pediatric neurology for evaluation before subsequent doses.
- [ ] **Extensive limb swelling post-vaccination** (entire limb involvement). → Pediatric immunology / allergy for evaluation.
- [ ] **Suspected primary immunodeficiency identified at vaccination visit** (history of recurrent severe infections, family history of PID, absent thymic shadow on chest X-ray). → Pediatric immunology within 7 days; NO live vaccines until cleared.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)

- [ ] **Severe egg allergy requiring egg-based vaccine** (influenza, yellow fever) → allergy specialist for supervised administration.
- [ ] **Immunocompromised child requiring live-vaccine decision** → pediatric immunology for individualised vaccine plan.
- [ ] **Post-transplant child requiring re-vaccination** → transplant team for re-vaccination schedule.
- [ ] **Suspected primary immunodeficiency (not urgent presentation)** → pediatric immunology.
- [ ] **Complex AEFI requiring causality assessment** → district immunisation officer + pediatric infectious disease.
- [ ] **Confirmed anaphylaxis to vaccine component with no substitute available** → allergy specialist for desensitisation assessment or permanent exemption documentation.

### 7.4 Handoff communication

Standard packet:
1. Complete vaccination history with all doses, dates, lot numbers.
2. AEFI history with WHO classification.
3. Immunodeficiency status and relevant labs (CD4, immunoglobulins where applicable).
4. Specific clinical question for receiving specialist.
5. Companion-generated referral summary.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

| Scenario | First follow-up | Subsequent |
|---|---|---|
| Routine periodic vaccination | Next A1 periodic visit per age schedule | Per A1 cadence |
| Catch-up (behind ≤ 6 months) | 4 weeks (next catch-up doses) | Every 4 weeks until caught up |
| Catch-up (severely behind) | 2 weeks (accelerated) | Every 2–4 weeks until caught up |
| HPV programme (2-dose) | 6–12 months after dose 1 | Complete after dose 2 |
| HPV programme (3-dose) | 1–2 months after dose 1 | 6 months after dose 1 (dose 3); complete |
| Travel vaccination series | Per vaccine schedule | Per vaccine schedule |
| Post-AEFI (moderate) | 4 weeks (clinical review before next dose) | Resume schedule if cleared |
| Post-AEFI (serious, §7) | Per receiving specialist | Per receiving specialist |

§8.2 What to recheck: vaccination status reconciliation at every visit; AEFI monitoring at 30/60 minutes post-dose; cold-chain integrity at every session.

§8.3 Success: child is up-to-date on IAP IIS 2024-25 at every A1 periodic visit; AEFI rate within expected population range; HPV programme completion rate ≥ 85% for enrolled adolescents; catch-up completion within 12 weeks of initiation.

§8.7 Loss-to-follow-up: Companion alert to manager + parent (WhatsApp) if vaccination overdue beyond 60-day grace period per IAP IIS. Manager re-engagement per §11.

## §9 Companion software workflow

### 9.1 Doctor Module screens

**Screen 1: Pre-consult summary** — IAP IIS card with next-due doses highlighted; prior AEFI flags; special-population flags (preterm, immunocompromised); subscription tier and included vaccines.

**Screen 2: Vaccination reconciliation** — side-by-side display of IAP IIS expected-for-age vs. PHR record vs. parent-held card. Discrepancy resolution workflow (add missing records; flag undocumented doses). `vaccination_card_reconciled`: boolean.

**Screen 3: Contraindication screen** — structured checklist: acute illness? fever? prior anaphylaxis? immunocompromised? egg allergy? pregnancy? current medications? Hard-block on administration if contraindication not cleared.

**Screen 4: Vaccine selection** — auto-populated list of doses due today; doctor confirms or modifies. Each vaccine entry includes: name, manufacturer options, lot number (barcode scan from vial), dose number, route, site.

**Screen 5: Administration** — nurse confirms administration; timestamp logged; observation timer starts (30 or 60 minutes per AEFI risk). Timer must complete before visit can close.

**Screen 6: AEFI documentation** — if any AEFI during observation period: structured form for symptoms, onset time, classification, intervention, outcome. Serious AEFI triggers §7 workflow and AEFI reporting form generation.

**Screen 7: Certificate generation** — auto-generates updated vaccination certificate (digital + printable PDF) with all doses administered to date.

**Screen 8: Next-visit scheduling** — auto-suggests next dose date(s) per IAP IIS or catch-up calendar; loads into Companion scheduling.

### 9.4 Parent app rendering

Post-visit: updated vaccination card (visual, colour-coded: green = given, amber = due soon, red = overdue); next doses with dates; post-vaccination care guidance (fever management, injection-site care, when to call).

### 9.5 Auto-generated outputs

1. Vaccination certificate (digital + printable PDF) — updated at every visit.
2. Catch-up calendar (printable) for families with missed doses.
3. Travel-vaccine plan document for travel-vaccine visits.
4. AEFI reporting form (serious AEFI only).

### 9.6 Edge cases

- Vaccine out of stock: Companion flags; reschedules; manager notified for procurement.
- Cold-chain failure: all affected stock quarantined; affected children notified for potential re-vaccination per manufacturer guidance.
- Parent declines vaccine: Companion logs specific vaccine declined, date, and next planned conversation date; does NOT block other vaccinations or A1 visits.
- Duplicate dose detected (given elsewhere and now at SKIDS): Companion flags; extra doses of most vaccines are safe but documented; do not repeat if confirmed given.
- Offline: paper vaccination record; upload to Companion when connectivity restored; observation timer managed manually.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Routine vaccination:

> "[Arjun] is due for [these vaccines] today. They protect against [diseases]. The nurse will give [number] small injections in [his] thigh / arm. We watch [him] for 30 minutes afterwards — that is standard for every child. [He] might have a mild fever tonight or a sore spot where the injection went in. Paracetamol is fine if [he] is uncomfortable. Call us if the fever goes above 40°C or if [he] seems unusually unwell."

Catch-up:

> "[Arjun] has missed [number] vaccines. This is common — many children fall behind, especially after the pandemic. We will make a catch-up plan: [number] visits over the next [weeks]. Each visit may have up to 4 injections. It is safe to give multiple vaccines at one visit — the immune system handles it well. The goal is to get [him] fully protected within [timeline]."

HPV:

> "The HPV vaccine protects against cervical cancer, other HPV-related cancers, and genital warts. It works best when given before exposure to HPV, which is why we give it at age 9–14. [She/He] needs [2/3] doses. The second dose is in [6–12 months]. Side effects are usually a sore arm for a day or two."

### 10.2 Subscription tier explanation

> "Your Basic plan covers the vaccines that are part of the government immunisation programme. The Advanced plan adds the recommended vaccines that aren't in the government programme — rotavirus, pneumococcal, varicella, hepatitis A, influenza, and typhoid. The Premium plan adds HPV vaccination, travel vaccines, and meningococcal where needed."

### 10.3 Post-visit summary template

```
Vaccines given today: [list with dose numbers]
Next vaccines due: [list with dates]
Observation completed: [30 / 60] minutes, no adverse events
Post-vaccination care: Paracetamol [dose] if fever or discomfort.
  Call SKIDS if: fever > 40°C, rash, unusual drowsiness, persistent
  crying > 3 hours, swelling at injection site spreading beyond the
  joint above and below.
Next vaccination visit: [date]
```

|---|---|

| Catch-up initiated | "Catching up on missed vaccines — what to expect" |

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

- *Vaccine hesitancy.* Use the IAP motivational-interviewing approach. Listen to the specific concern (safety, ingredients, "too many too soon," religious, alternative-medicine preference). Address the specific concern with evidence, not with a lecture. If the parent declines, document the specific vaccine declined, the reason given, and the next planned conversation. Do not coerce. Do not disenrol. Re-engage at next visit. Parents who partially vaccinate are managed by administering accepted vaccines and continuing engagement on declined ones.
- *"Natural immunity" preference.* Acknowledge that natural infection produces immunity; explain that the vaccine achieves the same immunity without the disease risk. For measles specifically: quantify the complication rate (1 in 1,000 encephalitis; 1–2 in 1,000 death in developing countries) vs. vaccine adverse event rate.
- *Religious objections.* Some families have specific religious concerns about vaccine ingredients (gelatin, human cell-line origin). Where alternatives exist (gelatin-free formulations), offer them. Where no alternative exists, provide the relevant religious-authority statements (IAP has compiled these) and respect the decision.
- *"Too many too soon."* The child's immune system encounters thousands of antigens daily; vaccines add a negligible immunological load. This is the evidence; present it plainly without condescension.
- *Cost concern.* If the family cannot afford non-NIS vaccines, prioritise NIS (government-programme) vaccines at no cost through UIP linkage. SKIDS manager can arrange UIP-linkage visits or government-centre referrals for families on Basic tier.
- *Social-media misinformation.* Do not engage in point-by-point debunking. Acknowledge that the parent saw concerning information. Redirect to IAP resources and the child's specific clinical picture.

|---|---|

## §12 Quality and audit

§12.1 Metrics:
- ≥ 90% of enrolled children up-to-date on IAP IIS at each A1 periodic visit [IAP-IIS-2024].
- ≥ 85% HPV programme completion (both doses within 18 months of dose 1).
- Catch-up completion within 12 weeks for ≥ 80% of children initiated on catch-up.
- Cold-chain excursion rate: 0 events resulting in vaccine wastage per quarter (target).
- AEFI reporting: 100% of serious AEFI reported within 24h.
- Vaccine-refusal re-engagement: ≥ 70% of families who declined at visit N accept at a subsequent visit within 12 months.
- Anaphylaxis management: 100% of vaccination stations pass quarterly resuscitation-readiness audit.
- Loss-to-follow-up for catch-up: < 15%.

§12.2 Quarterly: protocol owner reviews cold-chain logs, all serious AEFI, all §7 referrals, random sample of 30 vaccination visits (reconciliation completeness, observation compliance, documentation quality), HPV programme uptake.

§12.3 Certification: every SKIDS pediatrician and nurse must complete IAP-accredited immunisation training (8 hours); annual update (2 hours); anaphylaxis management drill (quarterly); cold-chain management competency (annual).

§12.4 Fellowship: SKIDS Pediatric Infectious Disease Fellowship pathway (future; not yet established). Pediatricians with interest in immunisation programme leadership may pursue IAP Immunisation Academy certification as a recognised pathway.
