# SKIDS Endocrine Clinic

> The SKIDS Endocrine Clinic is a Tier 2 service requiring a
> Fellowship-credentialed pediatric endocrinologist. It manages
> type 1 and type 2 diabetes mellitus, thyroid disorders,
> growth-hormone deficiency, adrenal disorders, pubertal disorders
> (precocious and delayed), PCOS, and disorders of sexual
> development (DSD). Available only at SKIDS clinics where a
> credentialed endocrinologist is assigned.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Manages type 1 diabetes mellitus: insulin initiation, dose titration, SMBG/CGM monitoring, carbohydrate counting, HbA1c targets (< 7.0% per ISPAD), hypoglycaemia management, DKA prevention education, sick-day rules.
- Manages type 2 diabetes mellitus (from B1-10 §7.3 referral): metformin initiation, lifestyle intensification, insulin if needed, metabolic-syndrome management.
- Manages hypothyroidism (congenital and acquired): levothyroxine dosing, TSH monitoring, growth-velocity tracking.
- Manages hyperthyroidism (Graves disease): methimazole, monitoring for agranulocytosis, thyroid-storm recognition (→ ER).
- Evaluates and manages growth-hormone deficiency: GH-stimulation testing, IGF-1/IGFBP-3, bone age, GH-replacement therapy initiation and monitoring.
- Manages pubertal disorders: precocious puberty (central — GnRH agonist therapy; peripheral — treat underlying cause), delayed puberty (constitutional vs. pathological workup), PCOS (from B1-13/B1-10 referral — hormonal management + lifestyle cross B1-10).
- Evaluates disorders of sexual development (DSD): ambiguous genitalia assessment, karyotype, hormonal profiling, multidisciplinary coordination (genetics, urology, psychology).
- Manages adrenal disorders: congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH — cortisol + fludrocortisone replacement, 17-OHP monitoring), Addison disease, Cushing syndrome workup (from B1-10 §7.3).
- Manages calcium/phosphate disorders: hypoparathyroidism, pseudohypoparathyroidism, rickets refractory to vitamin D (from B1-09 §7.3).

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Operate without a Fellowship-credentialed pediatric endocrinologist (Tier 2 requirement).
- Manage DKA acutely (→ §7.1 ER; B2-01 manages post-DKA long-term care).
- Perform surgical procedures (thyroidectomy, adrenalectomy → §7.3 surgery).
- Manage paediatric oncology endocrine complications directly (→ coordinate with oncology).

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (with transition planning to adult endocrinology at 18).

**Tier 2 credential requirement:** Pediatric endocrinologist with recognised subspecialty credential (DM Pediatric Endocrinology, or equivalent Fellowship from recognised institution). SKIDS clinics activate B2-01 only when this credential is present.

**When B2-01 is NOT available at a SKIDS clinic:** A1 and B1 protocol §7 triggers route directly to the named external pediatric endocrinologist partner (Tier 3). The child does not remain at SKIDS for endocrine management.

## §3–§12 (Tier 2 structure)

B2-01 follows the standard 12-section structure but with the following Tier 2 adaptations:

**§3:** Receives referrals from A1 §7.2 (new-onset DM), B1-10 §7.3 (T2DM, pre-diabetes refractory), B1-13 §7.3 (PCOS, pubertal disorders), B1-09 §7.3 (refractory rickets, growth faltering), B1-23 §7.3 (suspected endocrine cause of developmental delay — hypothyroidism), and B1-17 §7.3 (adrenal HTN workup).

**§4:** Endocrine-specific diagnostic workup per condition. Insulin-tolerance test, GH-stimulation test (arginine, clonidine, glucagon), GnRH-stimulation test, ACTH-stimulation test, dexamethasone-suppression test — all performed under Fellowship-credentialed supervision.

**§5 [SACRED]:** Severity grading per condition-specific guidelines (ISPAD for DM, Tanner-based for pubertal disorders, bone-age-based for growth).

**§6:** Condition-specific therapeutics: insulin (basal-bolus, pump candidacy), metformin, levothyroxine, methimazole, GH-replacement, GnRH agonist (leuprolide), hydrocortisone + fludrocortisone (CAH), calcium + calcitriol (hypoparathyroidism).

**§7 [SACRED]:**
- §7.1: DKA (→ ER); thyroid storm (→ ER); adrenal crisis (→ ER + IV hydrocortisone).
- §7.2: New-onset T1DM without DKA → B2-01 within 7 days for insulin initiation.
- §7.3: GH-replacement candidacy → tertiary endocrine centre if stimulation-testing not available at SKIDS; DSD multidisciplinary → tertiary centre; CAH salt-wasting crisis → ER + neonatal unit.

### §8 Follow-up schedule

#### 8.1 Follow-up frequency

| Condition | First follow-up | Subsequent | Annual review |
|---|---|---|---|
| T1DM (newly diagnosed) | 1 week post-initiation | Every 2 weeks × 2 months; then every 3 months (HbA1c + self-care review) | Yes — comprehensive (retinal, renal, foot, lipid, thyroid) |
| T1DM (established) | Every 3 months | Every 3 months (HbA1c, SMBG/CGM review, dose adjustment) | Yes — comprehensive annual |
| T2DM | 4 weeks post-metformin | Every 3 months (HbA1c, weight, lifestyle review) | Yes — metabolic panel |
| Hypothyroidism (congenital) | 2 weeks post-initiation | Every 4–6 weeks until TSH stable; then every 3–6 months | Yes — growth velocity + TSH |
| Hypothyroidism (acquired) | 6 weeks post-levothyroxine | Every 3–6 months (TSH) | Yes |
| Hyperthyroidism (Graves) | 2 weeks (FBC for agranulocytosis) | Every 4–6 weeks × 3 months; then every 3 months | Yes — TFT + antibody titres |
| GH deficiency (on replacement) | 3 months | Every 3–6 months (growth velocity + IGF-1 + bone age annually) | Yes |
| Precocious puberty (on GnRH agonist) | 3 months | Every 3–6 months (Tanner staging + growth velocity + bone age) | Yes |
| CAH (on replacement) | 2 weeks post-dose adjustment | Every 3 months (17-OHP, electrolytes, growth velocity) | Yes |

#### 8.2 What to recheck at each follow-up

- *T1DM:* HbA1c, SMBG/CGM data download, insulin doses, hypoglycaemia episodes (frequency + severity), sick-day rule compliance, carb-counting accuracy, injection-site rotation, growth velocity, school participation.
- *Thyroid:* TSH (free T4 if dose change), growth velocity, school performance, constipation/energy screening.
- *GH:* height velocity (cm/year), IGF-1 level, bone age (annually), injection-site rotation, adherence.
- *Pubertal disorders:* Tanner stage, growth velocity, bone age (every 6–12 months), psychosocial adjustment.
- *CAH:* 17-OHP (target 2–10 nmol/L), electrolytes, growth velocity, blood pressure.

#### 8.3 Treatment success criteria

- *T1DM:* HbA1c < 7.0% [ISPAD-2022]; < 2 severe hypoglycaemia episodes/year; no DKA recurrence; time-in-range > 70% if CGM.
- *Hypothyroidism:* TSH normalised within 3 months of levothyroxine initiation; growth velocity age-appropriate.
- *Hyperthyroidism:* free T4 normalised within 6–8 weeks; no agranulocytosis; euthyroid maintenance.
- *GH deficiency:* growth velocity ≥ 8 cm/year in first year of replacement; IGF-1 in age-appropriate range.
- *Precocious puberty:* Tanner regression or stabilisation within 6 months; bone-age advancement decelerated.

### §10 Parent-facing communication

#### 10.1 Scripted explanations

When diagnosing T1DM:

> "This is a lifelong condition, but it is completely manageable. [Child]'s pancreas has stopped making insulin, so we will give insulin from outside — by injection or, eventually, possibly by a pump. [He/She] will learn to check blood sugar, give insulin, and count carbohydrates. Most children with type 1 diabetes live fully normal, active lives — they play sports, go to school, travel, and grow up to do everything their friends do. We will be with you every step. The first few weeks are the hardest; after that, it becomes routine."

When diagnosing hypothyroidism:

> "[Child] has an underactive thyroid gland. The thyroid makes a hormone that the body needs for energy, growth, and brain development. We're going to replace that hormone with a small tablet taken once a day, every day. Once the dose is right, [he/she] will feel completely normal. We check blood tests every few months to make sure the dose stays right as [he/she] grows."

When initiating GH replacement:

> "The growth hormone test confirms that [child]'s body isn't making enough growth hormone. The treatment is a daily injection — a very small needle, given at bedtime. Most children adjust to it within a week or two. We expect to see [him/her] grow significantly faster in the first year. We'll measure every three months and adjust the dose as [he/she] grows."

#### 10.2 Cultural sensitivities

- *DM stigma in Indian families.* Diabetes carries significant social stigma. Families may conceal the diagnosis from school, extended family, or marriage prospects. Counsel that concealment from school is unsafe (teacher must know for hypoglycaemia management). Address marriage/fertility concerns proactively for adolescents — T1DM does not prevent marriage or healthy pregnancy with good control.
- *Insulin injection fear.* Families may resist insulin, viewing it as "the last resort" or "addictive." Counsel that in T1DM, insulin is the only treatment — it is not optional and not a sign of severity but of the specific type of diabetes.
- *GH replacement and "height obsession."* Some families request GH for constitutional short stature without GH deficiency. B2-01 prescribes GH only for confirmed GH deficiency or approved indications — not for parental height expectations. Document the conversation.
- *Precocious puberty and family anxiety.* Early puberty in girls causes intense family anxiety in the Indian context. Counsel on the medical rationale for treatment (preserving adult height, psychosocial considerations) rather than cultural framing.

### §12 Quality and audit

#### 12.1 Outcome metrics

B2-01 is working well when:

- HbA1c < 7.0% in ≥ 50% of T1DM patients within 1 year of B2-01 management [ISPAD-2022].
- DKA recurrence rate < 5% per year among established T1DM patients.
- Severe hypoglycaemia rate < 2 episodes per patient per year.
- Hypothyroidism: TSH normalised within 3 months of levothyroxine in ≥ 90% of cases.
- GH replacement: growth velocity ≥ 8 cm/year in first year in ≥ 80% of GH-deficient patients.
- Precocious puberty: Tanner stabilisation within 6 months of GnRH agonist in ≥ 90% of cases.
- CAH: 17-OHP within target range (2–10 nmol/L) at ≥ 70% of quarterly reviews.
- 100% of §7.1 emergencies (DKA, thyroid storm, adrenal crisis) result in same-day ER handoff.
- CGM adoption offered to 100% of T1DM patients; uptake tracked.
- Annual comprehensive review completed for 100% of T1DM patients.

#### 12.2 Quarterly case review

The protocol owner (credentialed pediatric endocrinologist) reviews every quarter:
- All T1DM patients: HbA1c trajectory, DKA events, severe hypoglycaemia events, insulin regimen appropriateness.
- All new diagnoses: diagnostic workup completeness, time to treatment initiation.
- All §7 referrals (every tier).
- All GH-replacement patients: growth-velocity response, IGF-1 appropriateness, adherence.
- All dose adjustments: clinical rationale documented.
- All loss-to-follow-up cases (especially T1DM — DKA risk).

Output: 1-page report to SKIDS clinical operations.

#### 12.3 Credentialing and the SKIDS Fellowship

B2-01 is unique among SKIDS protocols: the SKIDS Pediatric Endocrinology Fellowship IS the credential pathway. Completing the Fellowship activates B2-01 at a SKIDS clinic. The Fellow-in-training cannot independently deliver B2-01 until credentialed.

Requirements to deliver B2-01:
- DM Pediatric Endocrinology or equivalent recognised subspecialty credential.
- Demonstrated proficiency in insulin initiation, dose titration, CGM interpretation, GH-stimulation testing, and GnRH-stimulation testing.
- Minimum case load of 50 B2-01 visits per quarter.
- Annual continuing education (8 hours covering ISPAD updates, IAP endocrine updates, new therapeutics review).
- Protocol governance responsibility: the B2-01 endocrinologist also governs the endocrine-related §7 triggers in B1-09, B1-10, B1-13, and B1-23.
