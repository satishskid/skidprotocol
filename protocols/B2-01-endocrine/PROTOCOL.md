# SKIDS Endocrine Clinic

> The SKIDS Endocrine Clinic is a Tier 2 service requiring a
> Fellowship-credentialed pediatric endocrinologist. It manages
> type 1 and type 2 diabetes mellitus, thyroid disorders,
> growth-hormone deficiency, adrenal disorders, pubertal disorders
> (precocious and delayed), PCOS, and disorders of sexual
> development (DSD). Available only at SKIDS clinics where a
> credentialed endocrinologist is assigned.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Manages type 1 diabetes mellitus: insulin initiation, dose titration, SMBG/CGM monitoring, carbohydrate counting, HbA1c targets (< 7.0% per ISPAD), hypoglycaemia management, DKA prevention education, sick-day rules.
- Manages type 2 diabetes mellitus (from B1-10 §7.3 referral): metformin initiation, lifestyle intensification, insulin if needed, metabolic-syndrome management.
- Manages hypothyroidism (congenital and acquired): levothyroxine dosing, TSH monitoring, growth-velocity tracking.
- Manages hyperthyroidism (Graves disease): methimazole, monitoring for agranulocytosis, thyroid-storm recognition (→ ER).
- Evaluates and manages growth-hormone deficiency: GH-stimulation testing, IGF-1/IGFBP-3, bone age, GH-replacement therapy initiation and monitoring.
- Manages pubertal disorders: precocious puberty (central — GnRH agonist therapy; peripheral — treat underlying cause), delayed puberty (constitutional vs. pathological workup), PCOS (from B1-13/B1-10 referral — hormonal management + lifestyle cross B1-10).
- Evaluates disorders of sexual development (DSD): ambiguous genitalia assessment, karyotype, hormonal profiling, multidisciplinary coordination (genetics, urology, psychology).
- Manages adrenal disorders: congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH — cortisol + fludrocortisone replacement, 17-OHP monitoring), Addison disease, Cushing syndrome workup (from B1-10 §7.3).
- Manages calcium/phosphate disorders: hypoparathyroidism, pseudohypoparathyroidism, rickets refractory to vitamin D (from B1-09 §7.3).

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Operate without a Fellowship-credentialed pediatric endocrinologist (Tier 2 requirement).
- Manage DKA acutely (→ §7.1 ER; B2-01 manages post-DKA long-term care).
- Perform surgical procedures (thyroidectomy, adrenalectomy → §7.3 surgery).
- Manage paediatric oncology endocrine complications directly (→ coordinate with oncology).

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (with transition planning to adult endocrinology at 18).

**Tier 2 credential requirement:** Pediatric endocrinologist with recognised subspecialty credential (DM Pediatric Endocrinology, or equivalent Fellowship from recognised institution). SKIDS clinics activate B2-01 only when this credential is present.

**When B2-01 is NOT available at a SKIDS clinic:** A1 and B1 protocol §7 triggers route directly to the named external pediatric endocrinologist partner (Tier 3). The child does not remain at SKIDS for endocrine management.

## §3–§12 (Tier 2 structure)

B2-01 follows the standard 12-section structure but with the following Tier 2 adaptations:

**§3:** Receives referrals from A1 §7.2 (new-onset DM), B1-10 §7.3 (T2DM, pre-diabetes refractory), B1-13 §7.3 (PCOS, pubertal disorders), B1-09 §7.3 (refractory rickets, growth faltering), B1-23 §7.3 (suspected endocrine cause of developmental delay — hypothyroidism), and B1-17 §7.3 (adrenal HTN workup).

**§4:** Endocrine-specific diagnostic workup per condition. Insulin-tolerance test, GH-stimulation test (arginine, clonidine, glucagon), GnRH-stimulation test, ACTH-stimulation test, dexamethasone-suppression test — all performed under Fellowship-credentialed supervision.

**§5 [SACRED]:** Severity grading per condition-specific guidelines (ISPAD for DM, Tanner-based for pubertal disorders, bone-age-based for growth).

**§6:** Condition-specific therapeutics: insulin (basal-bolus, pump candidacy), metformin, levothyroxine, methimazole, GH-replacement, GnRH agonist (leuprolide), hydrocortisone + fludrocortisone (CAH), calcium + calcitriol (hypoparathyroidism).

**§7 [SACRED]:**
- §7.1: DKA (→ ER); thyroid storm (→ ER); adrenal crisis (→ ER + IV hydrocortisone).
- §7.2: New-onset T1DM without DKA → B2-01 within 7 days for insulin initiation.
- §7.3: GH-replacement candidacy → tertiary endocrine centre if stimulation-testing not available at SKIDS; DSD multidisciplinary → tertiary centre; CAH salt-wasting crisis → ER + neonatal unit.

**§8:** DM: every 3 months (HbA1c + self-care review); thyroid: every 3–6 months (TSH); GH: every 3–6 months (growth velocity + IGF-1); pubertal: every 3–6 months (Tanner progression).

**§9:** Companion integration: DM module (SMBG/CGM data import, insulin-dose log, HbA1c trend, carb-counting log); growth-velocity chart with bone-age overlay; pubertal-staging timeline.

**§10:** T1DM diagnosis script: "This is a lifelong condition, but it is completely manageable. [He] will learn to check blood sugar, give insulin, and count carbohydrates. Most children with type 1 diabetes live fully normal, active lives. We will be with you every step." Cultural: DM stigma in Indian families; marriage/fertility concerns addressed proactively for adolescents.

**§11:** B2-01 is Premium tier only. Manager role: coordinate multi-visit scheduling, insulin/supply procurement, CGM device support.

**§12:** Metrics: HbA1c < 7.0% in ≥ 50% of T1DM patients within 1 year; DKA rate < 5% per year; hypothyroidism TSH normalised within 3 months of levothyroxine; GH-replacement growth-velocity response ≥ 8 cm/year in first year. Fellowship: the SKIDS Pediatric Endocrinology Fellowship IS the credential pathway — completing it activates B2-01 at a clinic.
