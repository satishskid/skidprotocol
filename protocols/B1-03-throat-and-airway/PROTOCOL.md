# SKIDS Throat & Airway Clinic

> The SKIDS Throat & Airway Clinic delivers structured pharyngitis
> management with RADT-guided streptococcal treatment, adenotonsillar
> assessment with Brodsky grading, recurrent tonsillitis evaluation
> for tonsillectomy candidacy, croup management, chronic/recurrent
> upper-airway infection workup, and ENT surgical-partner routing
> for tonsillectomy, adenoidectomy, and airway endoscopy. Shares
> the ENT surgeon Tier 3 partner with B1-02 Hearing & Ear.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Diagnoses and manages acute pharyngitis (viral vs. bacterial) using clinical criteria (McIsaac score) and rapid antigen detection test (RADT) for Group A Streptococcus (GAS); treats confirmed GAS pharyngitis with appropriate antibiotic therapy to prevent rheumatic fever [AAP-GAS-2012].
- Assesses adenotonsillar hypertrophy using Brodsky/Friedman grading (1–4); routes grade 3–4 with clinical indication to ENT surgeon partner for tonsillectomy / adenoidectomy consideration.
- Evaluates recurrent tonsillitis against Paradise criteria (≥ 7 episodes in 1 year, ≥ 5/year for 2 years, ≥ 3/year for 3 years — each documented with fever, adenopathy, exudate, or positive GAS test) for tonsillectomy referral [AAP-Tonsillectomy-2011].
- Manages croup (laryngotracheobronchitis) in clinic: Westley croup score assessment, single-dose dexamethasone, nebulised adrenaline for moderate-severe; routes severe/refractory to §7.1 ER.
- Manages peritonsillar cellulitis (early, non-abscess) with parenteral or high-dose oral antibiotics; routes peritonsillar abscess to §7.1/§7.2 ENT for drainage.
- Evaluates chronic / recurrent sore throat with structured differential: post-nasal drip (cross with B1-06 allergic rhinitis), GORD-related pharyngitis (cross with B1-15 GI when drafted), habitual mouth breathing.
- Cross-cares with B1-02 Hearing & Ear for adenotonsillar hypertrophy causing OME and with B1-07 Sleep for adenotonsillar hypertrophy causing OSA.
- Screens for obstructive airway concerns: stridor assessment (inspiratory = supraglottic/glottic; biphasic = subglottic/tracheal), stertor, and routes structural airway disease to §7.
- Manages acute epiglottitis as §7.1 emergency (rare in Hib-vaccinated populations but must be recognised).
- Counsels on tonsillectomy/adenoidectomy pre-operatively when ENT referral is made; manages post-operative follow-up in coordination with ENT partner.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform tonsillectomy, adenoidectomy, or any surgical procedure (§7.3 to ENT surgeon partner).
- Perform direct or indirect laryngoscopy, flexible nasendoscopy, or airway endoscopy (§7.3 to ENT).
- Manage deep neck space infection (parapharyngeal, retropharyngeal abscess) beyond initial recognition and §7.1 routing.
- Manage tracheostomy care.
- Manage subglottic stenosis, laryngomalacia requiring intervention, or congenital airway anomalies beyond recognition and §7.3 routing.
- Manage suspected malignancy (lymphoma presenting as tonsillar asymmetry) beyond recognition and §7.2 routing.

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 throat routing; acute pharyngitis management including RADT; croup management.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — recurrent tonsillitis monitoring programme; adenotonsillar assessment; ENT referral coordination.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — multi-protocol cross-care (B1-03 + B1-02 + B1-07 for adenotonsillar-driven OME + OSA management); priority ENT partner surgical scheduling.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1. Every SKIDS pediatrician manages pharyngitis, croup, and tonsillar assessment under this protocol. The ENT surgeon partner (Tier 3, shared with B1-02) provides tonsillectomy, adenoidectomy, peritonsillar abscess drainage, airway endoscopy, and post-operative management. Protocol governance with named pediatric ENT specialist.

**Departures from cited guidelines:**

1. *RADT-only approach (no backup throat culture).* IDSA 2012 recommends backup throat culture for RADT-negative children with high clinical suspicion. SKIDS follows RADT-only for children with McIsaac ≤ 2 but obtains backup throat culture for McIsaac ≥ 3 with negative RADT. This is a pragmatic departure — RADT sensitivity is 85–95%; in the Indian context where RF burden is significant, the backup culture for high-suspicion cases is justified [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm SKIDS RADT sensitivity data].

2. *Rheumatic fever secondary prophylaxis.* RF remains endemic in India. B1-03 initiates secondary prophylaxis (benzathine penicillin G 3-weekly or 4-weekly IM) for confirmed RF/RHD per IAP guidelines. Some protocols reserve this for cardiology; SKIDS initiates at B1-03 with B1-16 Cardiac cross-care for echocardiographic monitoring.

3. *Tonsillectomy threshold.* Protocol follows Paradise criteria strictly. Some Indian ENT practices perform tonsillectomy on lower thresholds. SKIDS routes at Paradise criteria OR at lower threshold if adenotonsillar OSA is documented (B1-07 cross-care).

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Acute pharyngitis assessment

**McIsaac score (modified Centor):**

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Temperature ≥ 38°C | +1 |
| Absence of cough | +1 |
| Tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy | +1 |
| Tonsillar swelling or exudates | +1 |
| Age 3–14 years | +1 |
| Age 15–44 years | 0 |
| Age ≥ 45 years | −1 |

**Decision rules:**
- McIsaac 0–1: viral likely; no RADT needed; symptomatic management.
- McIsaac 2–3: RADT indicated.
- McIsaac ≥ 4: RADT indicated; high GAS probability.
- RADT positive → treat as GAS pharyngitis (§6.2).
- RADT negative, McIsaac ≥ 3: send backup throat culture; treat if culture positive.
- RADT negative, McIsaac ≤ 2: viral pharyngitis; symptomatic management.

**RADT procedure:**
- Swab both tonsillar surfaces and posterior pharynx (NOT tongue, NOT buccal mucosa).
- Result in 5–10 minutes.
- Do NOT test children < 3 years for GAS routinely (GAS pharyngitis rare < 3; RF extremely rare < 3) unless household contact with confirmed GAS [AAP-GAS-2012].

### 4.2 Examination

- **Oropharynx.** Tonsillar size (Brodsky grade 1–4); exudates (white, yellow, grey); erythema; palatal petechiae (suggestive of GAS); uvula deviation (peritonsillar abscess); posterior pharyngeal wall (cobblestoning = post-nasal drip).
- **Neck.** Anterior cervical lymphadenopathy (tender, mobile = reactive; fixed, non-tender, > 2 cm → §7.2 malignancy workup). Midline swelling (thyroglossal cyst → §7.3). Lateral swelling (branchial cleft cyst, lymphoma → §7.2/§7.3).
- **Nasal.** Nasal obstruction, mouth breathing, adenoidal facies (long face, high-arched palate, dental crowding).
- **Ears.** Otoscopy — TM status (OME commonly coexists with adenotonsillar hypertrophy; cross B1-02).
- **Respiratory.** Stridor (inspiratory, expiratory, biphasic); stertor; voice quality (hot-potato voice = peritonsillar abscess; hoarse = laryngeal pathology).
- **General.** Temperature; hydration status; ability to swallow (drooling = epiglottitis → §7.1; trismus = peritonsillar abscess).

### 4.3 Adenotonsillar assessment

**Brodsky/Friedman tonsillar grading:**

| Grade | Description |
|---|---|
| 0 | Tonsils within tonsillar fossa |
| 1 | Tonsils < 25% of oropharyngeal width |
| 2 | Tonsils 25–50% |
| 3 | Tonsils 50–75% |
| 4 | Tonsils > 75% ("kissing tonsils") |

Grade 3–4 with clinical indication (OSA symptoms per B1-07, dysphagia, failure to thrive) → §7.3 ENT for tonsillectomy/adenoidectomy consideration.

**Adenoid assessment:**
- Clinical: mouth breathing, adenoidal facies, hyponasal speech, snoring.
- Lateral neck X-ray (adenoidal-nasopharyngeal ratio): B1-03 does not routinely order; the clinical picture drives the ENT referral. ENT performs nasendoscopy for definitive adenoid assessment.

### 4.4 Recurrent tonsillitis assessment

**Paradise criteria for tonsillectomy candidacy [AAP-Tonsillectomy-2011]:**
- ≥ 7 episodes in 1 year; OR
- ≥ 5 episodes/year for 2 consecutive years; OR
- ≥ 3 episodes/year for 3 consecutive years.

Each episode must be documented with ≥ 1 of: temperature ≥ 38.3°C, cervical lymphadenopathy (tender, > 2 cm), tonsillar exudate, positive GAS test.

**Documentation requirement:** Companion maintains the tonsillitis episode log (date, documented criteria met, treatment given, culture/RADT result). The log is included in the ENT referral packet.

### 4.5 Croup assessment

**Westley Croup Score** (sum all 5 domains; maximum 17):

| Feature | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 | Score 4 | Score 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Stridor** | None | With stethoscope at rest | Without stethoscope at rest | — | — | — |
| **Retractions** | None | Mild | Moderate | Severe | — | — |
| **Air entry** | Normal | Decreased | Markedly decreased | — | — | — |
| **Cyanosis** | None | — | — | — | With agitation | At rest |
| **Consciousness** | Normal | — | — | — | — | Disoriented |

**Classification:**
- Mild (Westley 0–2): barking cough, no stridor at rest.
- Moderate (Westley 3–7): stridor at rest, some retractions, no distress.
- Severe (Westley 8–11): severe stridor, significant retractions, agitation or lethargy.
- Impending respiratory failure (Westley ≥ 12): decreased consciousness, minimal air entry. → §7.1 immediate.

### 4.6 Peritonsillar assessment

- Unilateral tonsillar bulge with uvula deviation; trismus (limited mouth opening); "hot-potato" voice; drooling; odynophagia.
- Distinguish cellulitis (no fluctuance) from abscess (fluctuance present, point tenderness).
- Cellulitis → §6.4. Abscess → §7.1/§7.2 ENT for drainage.

### 4.7 Chronic pharyngitis workup

Differential for chronic/recurrent sore throat without meeting Paradise criteria:
1. Post-nasal drip (allergic rhinitis → B1-06 cross-care).
2. GORD-related pharyngitis (→ B1-15 GI cross-care when drafted).
3. Mouth breathing (adenotonsillar hypertrophy or nasal obstruction).
4. Environmental irritant (passive smoke, pollution, dry air).
5. Habitual throat clearing / globus pharyngeus (psychogenic → B1-08 cross-care if significant).
6. Viral reactivation (EBV chronic; rare).

### 4.8 Stridor assessment

- **Inspiratory stridor** → supraglottic or glottic (laryngomalacia most common in infants; epiglottitis in acute febrile child).
- **Biphasic stridor** → subglottic (croup in acute; subglottic stenosis if chronic/recurrent).
- **Expiratory stridor / wheeze** → tracheobronchial (→ B1-14 cross-care if wheezing component).
- Acute stridor with fever + toxicity + drooling → epiglottitis → §7.1 ER (do NOT examine throat; call emergency services).
- Chronic stridor from birth → laryngomalacia (self-limiting if feeding well and growing; §7.3 ENT if failure to thrive, feeding difficulty, or cyanotic episodes).

### 4.9 Decision tree

```
Child presents with throat / airway complaint
        │
        ▼
Acute sore throat → §4.1 McIsaac + RADT
        ├── GAS positive → §6.2 antibiotic
        ├── GAS negative → §6.1 symptomatic
        │
Recurrent tonsillitis → §4.4 Paradise log
        ├── Criteria met → §7.3 ENT tonsillectomy
        ├── Not yet met → monitor; continue documenting
        │
Adenotonsillar hypertrophy → §4.3 Brodsky grading
        ├── Grade 3–4 with indication → §7.3 ENT
        ├── Grade 1–2 → monitor; cross B1-07/B1-02 if symptoms
        │
Croup → §4.5 Westley score
        ├── Mild → §6.3 dexamethasone
        ├── Moderate → §6.3 dexamethasone + nebulised adrenaline
        ├── Severe / impending failure → §7.1 ER
        │
Peritonsillar → §4.6
        ├── Cellulitis → §6.4
        ├── Abscess → §7.1 / §7.2 ENT
        │

        ├── Acute + fever + drooling → epiglottitis → §7.1
        ├── Chronic from birth → laryngomalacia → monitor / §7.3

```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

### 5.1 Acute pharyngitis

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Viral pharyngitis** | McIsaac ≤ 1 OR RADT negative + McIsaac ≤ 2 | §6.1 symptomatic |
| **GAS pharyngitis (uncomplicated)** | RADT positive or culture positive | §6.2 amoxicillin |
| **GAS pharyngitis with peritonsillar cellulitis** | Unilateral swelling, no fluctuance, trismus mild | §6.4 high-dose antibiotic |
| **Peritonsillar abscess** | Fluctuance, trismus severe, uvula deviation | §7.1/§7.2 ENT for drainage |
| **Pharyngitis with airway compromise** | Stridor, drooling, inability to swallow | §7.1 ER |

### 5.2 Recurrent tonsillitis

| Frequency | Action |
|---|---|
| < 3 episodes/year (documented) | §6.1 manage each episode; monitor |
| ≥ 3/year for 1–2 years (approaching Paradise) | Continue monitoring; document meticulously |
| Paradise criteria met | §7.3 ENT for tonsillectomy |
| Recurrent tonsillitis + OSA (B1-07) or OME (B1-02) | §7.3 ENT even if Paradise not yet met (combined indication) |

### 5.3 Adenotonsillar hypertrophy

| Grade + Symptoms | Action |
|---|---|
| Grade 1–2, asymptomatic | Monitor; no intervention |
| Grade 2 with snoring, mild mouth breathing | Cross B1-07 for sleep assessment; monitor |
| Grade 3–4 with OSA symptoms (B1-07 PSQ positive) | §7.3 ENT for adenotonsillectomy |
| Grade 3–4 with OME + hearing loss (B1-02) | §7.3 ENT for adenoidectomy ± grommets |
| Grade 3–4 with dysphagia or FTT | §7.3 ENT for tonsillectomy |

### 5.4 Croup

| Westley score | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | Mild | §6.3 single-dose dexamethasone; discharge |
| 3–7 | Moderate | §6.3 dexamethasone + nebulised adrenaline; observe 2–4h |
| 8–11 | Severe | §6.3 + §7.1 if not responding |
| ≥ 12 | Impending respiratory failure | §7.1 immediate ER |

### 5.5 Boundary rules

- GAS pharyngitis in children < 3 years: do NOT test routinely; treat only if household GAS contact or clinical high-suspicion (scarlatiniform rash, palatal petechiae).
- Tonsillar asymmetry (one tonsil significantly larger than the other, especially if firm or non-tender) → §7.2 for lymphoma workup before assuming simple hypertrophy.
- Post-tonsillectomy bleeding → §7.1 ER regardless of volume (even "minor" post-tonsillectomy bleeding can escalate rapidly).
- A child with recurrent pharyngitis who does NOT meet Paradise criteria does NOT get referred for tonsillectomy; continue documenting episodes.
- Croup in children > 6 years or recurrent croup (≥ 3 episodes) → consider structural cause; §7.3 ENT for airway evaluation.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (viral pharyngitis / mild symptoms)

- Paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose PO QDS or ibuprofen 10 mg/kg/dose PO TDS for pain and fever.
- Warm salt-water gargles (≥ 6 years).
- Adequate fluids; soft diet.
- Honey (≥ 1 year) for sore-throat relief.
- No antibiotics for viral pharyngitis.
- Expected resolution: 3–5 days.

### 6.2 Standard tier (GAS pharyngitis treatment)

**First-line:** Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day (max 1 g) PO once daily × 10 days OR amoxicillin 25 mg/kg/dose (max 500 mg) PO BD × 10 days [AAP-GAS-2012].

**Alternative (penicillin allergy, non-anaphylactic):** Cephalexin 20 mg/kg/dose (max 500 mg) PO BD × 10 days.

**Alternative (penicillin allergy, anaphylactic):** Azithromycin 12 mg/kg (max 500 mg) PO day 1, then 6 mg/kg (max 250 mg) days 2–5.

**Benzathine penicillin G (IM, single dose):** < 27 kg: 600,000 units IM; ≥ 27 kg: 1,200,000 units IM. Alternative when oral adherence is uncertain.

**Post-treatment:**
- No repeat RADT/culture needed if symptoms resolve.
- Test-of-cure throat culture only if: history of RF/RHD, family member with RF/RHD, or outbreak context.
- Return if not improving after 48–72h of antibiotic.

**RF prevention:** Treating GAS pharyngitis within 9 days of onset prevents RF. In India's RF-endemic context, this window is non-negotiable. Any febrile pharyngitis with McIsaac ≥ 2 gets RADT.

### 6.3 Croup management (in-clinic)

**All croup (mild, moderate, severe):**
- Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg PO or IM single dose (max 10 mg) [AAP-Croup-2021].
- For mild croup: discharge after observation (30–60 min); single dose is sufficient.

**Moderate croup (Westley 3–7):**
- Dexamethasone as above.
- Nebulised adrenaline 0.5 mL/kg of 1:1000 (max 5 mL) via nebuliser. Onset 10–30 min; duration 2h.
- Observe for minimum 2–4 hours post-nebulised adrenaline (rebound possible).
- If sustained improvement after observation period: discharge with prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day × 2 days backup.
- If no improvement or recurrence → §7.1 ER.

**Severe croup (Westley ≥ 8) or impending respiratory failure:**
- Dexamethasone + nebulised adrenaline as above.
- Call emergency services → §7.1 ER transfer.
- Keep child calm (agitation worsens obstruction); avoid throat examination.

### 6.4 Peritonsillar cellulitis management

- Amoxicillin-clavulanate 45 mg/kg/day (amoxicillin component) PO in 2 divided doses × 10 days.
- OR if unable to swallow: IM ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg (max 1 g) single dose + switch to oral when swallowing improves.
- Reassess at 24–48h: if improving → continue oral. If worsening (increasing trismus, fluctuance developing) → §7.2 ENT for peritonsillar abscess drainage.
- IV fluid replacement if unable to swallow adequately.

### 6.5 Rheumatic fever secondary prophylaxis

For children with confirmed RF or RHD (diagnosed by B1-16 Cardiac or paediatric cardiology):
- **Benzathine penicillin G IM:** < 27 kg: 600,000 units; ≥ 27 kg: 1,200,000 units. Every 3 weeks (preferred in India due to high RF recurrence) or every 4 weeks [IAP-RF-2008].
- **Duration:** per IAP/WHO guidelines — RF without carditis: 5 years or until age 21 (whichever is longer); RF with carditis but no residual RHD: 10 years or until age 21; RF with RHD: 10 years or until age 40 (or lifelong if severe RHD).
- Injection administered at SKIDS clinic by trained nurse; documented in Companion with next-due-date alert.
- Penicillin allergy: erythromycin 250 mg PO BD daily (continuous).
- Cross-care: B1-16 Cardiac for echocardiographic monitoring of valvular disease.

### 6.6 Post-tonsillectomy / post-adenoidectomy follow-up

- Post-operative follow-up at SKIDS (coordinated with ENT partner): day 7–10 post-op.
- Assess: pain control, oral intake, healing, post-operative bleeding risk (days 5–10 are highest risk).
- Advise: soft/cool diet × 2 weeks; adequate hydration; avoid strenuous activity × 2 weeks; avoid NSAIDs (bleeding risk — paracetamol only for pain).
- If post-operative bleeding: any bleeding → §7.1 ER (even minor).
- Reassess at 4 weeks: ENT surgeon clears; B1-07 reassesses OSA symptoms; B1-02 reassesses OME if relevant.

### 6.7 Contraindications and precautions

| Therapeutic | Contraindication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Amoxicillin | Confirmed penicillin allergy | Use cephalexin (non-anaphylactic) or azithromycin (anaphylactic) |
| NSAIDs | Post-tonsillectomy (bleeding risk) | Paracetamol only |
| Nebulised adrenaline | None absolute; use with cardiac monitoring if known cardiac condition | Proceed with monitoring |
| Throat examination | Suspected epiglottitis | Do NOT examine; call emergency services |

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)

- [ ] **Epiglottitis** — acute stridor + fever + drooling + toxic appearance + sitting upright/tripod position. → Do NOT examine throat. Call emergency services → ER. Administer IV access if safe; do NOT delay transfer.
- [ ] **Severe croup (Westley ≥ 8) not responding to dexamethasone + nebulised adrenaline after 2 doses** → ER.
- [ ] **Peritonsillar abscess with airway compromise** — trismus + stridor + drooling → ER + ENT surgeon.
- [ ] **Post-tonsillectomy bleeding** — any volume → ER + ENT surgeon.
- [ ] **Deep neck space infection** — retropharyngeal or parapharyngeal abscess (neck stiffness, torticollis, toxic, bulging posterior pharynx). → ER + ENT surgeon.
- [ ] **Foreign body in pharynx / larynx with respiratory distress** → ER.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Peritonsillar abscess (non-compromised airway)** → ENT for drainage within 24–48h.
- [ ] **Tonsillar asymmetry — suspicion of malignancy** (firm, non-tender, unilateral enlargement without infection) → ENT + paediatric oncology workup within 7 days.
- [ ] **Persistent cervical lymphadenopathy > 2 cm, non-tender, fixed, > 4 weeks** → paediatric oncology workup.
- [ ] **Recurrent croup (≥ 3 episodes)** in child > 2 years → ENT for airway evaluation (rule out subglottic stenosis, haemangioma).

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)

- [ ] **Recurrent tonsillitis meeting Paradise criteria** → ENT for tonsillectomy.
- [ ] **Adenotonsillar hypertrophy grade 3–4 with OSA (B1-07 PSQ positive)** → ENT for adenotonsillectomy.
- [ ] **Adenotonsillar hypertrophy with OME + hearing loss ≥ 25 dB (B1-02)** → ENT for adenoidectomy ± grommets.
- [ ] **Adenotonsillar hypertrophy with dysphagia or failure to thrive** → ENT for tonsillectomy.
- [ ] **Chronic stridor from birth with feeding difficulty, FTT, or cyanotic episodes** (laryngomalacia requiring intervention) → ENT for laryngoscopy.
- [ ] **Nasal polyps in child** — atypical; consider CF → ENT + B1-14 cross-care.
- [ ] **Suspected subglottic stenosis** (recurrent croup, fixed stridor, history of intubation) → ENT for airway evaluation.
- [ ] **RF/RHD confirmed** — initiate secondary prophylaxis (§6.5) + B1-16 Cardiac cross-care for echocardiography.
- [ ] **GAS pharyngitis with suppurative complication** (peritonsillar cellulitis not responding to 48h antibiotic) → ENT.

### 7.4 Handoff communication

Standard packet:
1. Oropharyngeal examination findings (tonsillar grade, laterality).
2. Tonsillitis episode log (dates, criteria, RADT/culture results).
3. B1-07 PSQ score and oximetry results (if OSA indication).
4. B1-02 audiometric results (if OME indication).
5. Westley croup score and treatment given (if croup indication).
6. Companion-generated referral summary.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

| Scenario | First follow-up | Subsequent |
|---|---|---|
| Viral pharyngitis | No routine follow-up; return if not resolving in 5 days | — |

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Viral pharyngitis:

> "[Aanya] has a viral sore throat. The rapid test for strep was negative, so she does not need antibiotics. Paracetamol for pain, warm fluids, and rest. It should improve in 3–5 days. If the fever lasts more than 5 days or she gets worse, bring her back."

GAS pharyngitis:

> "[Aanya] has a strep throat — the rapid test was positive. She needs 10 days of antibiotic. It's important to finish all 10 days even though she will feel better in 2–3 days. Stopping early increases the risk of the infection coming back and, in rare cases, of rheumatic fever, which can affect the heart. She should feel much better within 48 hours of starting the antibiotic."

Recurrent tonsillitis approaching Paradise:

> "[Arjun] has had [number] episodes of tonsillitis in the past [timeframe]. We keep a log of each episode. If the pattern continues, at a certain point the evidence says removing the tonsils reduces the number of infections he gets. We're not there yet — we'll continue monitoring and treating each episode. If we reach that threshold, I'll refer him to the ENT surgeon."

Croup:

> "[Arjun] has croup — a viral infection that makes the airway swell and causes that barking cough. The steroid medicine we gave will reduce the swelling over the next few hours. The barking cough may last 3–5 days but the worst is usually the first night. If the noisy breathing comes back at rest, or he looks like he's struggling to breathe, bring him straight to hospital."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

- *"Tonsils should just be removed."* Some Indian families request tonsillectomy for any recurrent sore throat. Counsel that tonsillectomy has surgical risks (bleeding, infection, pain) and that evidence supports surgery only when the infection frequency meets specific thresholds — or when the tonsils are causing breathing or hearing problems.
- *Antibiotic expectation for every sore throat.* Many Indian families expect antibiotics for every pharyngitis episode. Counsel that most sore throats are viral and do not respond to antibiotics; the rapid test tells us which ones need treatment. Frame as "using the right medicine for the right infection."
- *RF awareness.* RF remains common in India. Frame GAS treatment completion as heart protection: "The full 10-day course protects [his] heart." This resonates more than abstract antibiotic-stewardship messaging.
- *Injection preference (benzathine penicillin).* Some families prefer "the injection" for GAS because they believe it is stronger. For single-episode GAS, oral amoxicillin is equally effective and less painful. IM benzathine penicillin is reserved for adherence concerns or RF prophylaxis.
- *Post-tonsillectomy diet myths.* Some families restrict diet excessively post-operatively (only liquids for weeks). Counsel: soft diet is sufficient; ice cream and cold foods are fine and soothing; adequate protein and calories support healing.
