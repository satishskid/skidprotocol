# SKIDS Blood Clinic

> The SKIDS Blood Clinic delivers structured evaluation of anaemia beyond IDA (B1-09 manages IDA), thalassemia-trait identification, bleeding-disorder screening, unexplained bruising/petechiae workup, lymphadenopathy evaluation, and malignancy-suspicion triage. Transfusion-dependent thalassemia major, sickle-cell complications, and confirmed malignancy route per §7 to pediatric haematology/oncology.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Evaluates anaemia not responding to B1-09 oral-iron therapy (Hb not improving after 4 weeks therapeutic iron): peripheral smear, reticulocyte count, iron studies, Hb electrophoresis, B12/folate, LDH/haptoglobin for haemolysis workup.
- Identifies thalassemia trait (alpha and beta) and sickle-cell trait via Hb electrophoresis / HPLC; provides genetic counselling for carrier families; routes thalassemia major/intermedia and sickle-cell disease to §7.3.
- Screens for bleeding disorders: prolonged bleeding after minor trauma, easy bruising, menorrhagia in adolescent girls (cross B1-13), family history — PT, aPTT, platelet count, von Willebrand panel where indicated.
- Evaluates unexplained bruising/petechiae: differentiates ITP (isolated thrombocytopenia, otherwise well child) from leukaemia/aplastic anaemia (pancytopenia, hepatosplenomegaly, bone pain, lymphadenopathy → §7.2).
- Manages immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP) in typical presentation (age 2–10, isolated thrombocytopenia, well child, no hepatosplenomegaly): observation if platelet > 20,000 and no active bleeding; routes severe/refractory ITP to §7.3.
- Evaluates persistent lymphadenopathy (> 2 cm, non-tender, > 4 weeks) per A1 §7.2 malignancy-suspicion pathway as bridge to pediatric oncology.
- Provides newborn screening result interpretation: haemoglobinopathy panel, G6PD deficiency confirmation, and family counselling.
- Cross-cares with B1-09 (IDA management), B1-13 (adolescent menorrhagia-related anaemia), B1-17 (anaemia of CKD).

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Manage transfusion-dependent thalassemia major (→ §7.3 haematology centre).
- Manage sickle-cell disease with active complications (→ §7.3).
- Diagnose or manage leukaemia / lymphoma (→ §7.2 oncology).
- Perform bone-marrow aspiration/biopsy (→ §7.3).
- Administer blood transfusion (→ §7.1/§7.2 hospital).
- Manage haemophilia beyond initial screening (→ §7.3 haematology).

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 POC Hb screening; first B1-18 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — anaemia workup; thalassemia-trait identification; up to 4 visits; genetic counselling.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited visits; bleeding-disorder workup; ITP monitoring; multi-protocol cross-care.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1. §7 to pediatric haematology (thalassemia major, SCD, haemophilia), pediatric oncology (malignancy), ER (severe anaemia, active bleeding).

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Anaemia workup (beyond IDA)
- FBC with peripheral smear (morphology: microcytic / normocytic / macrocytic; target cells, sickle cells, spherocytes, schistocytes).
- Reticulocyte count (low = production failure; high = destruction/blood loss).
- Iron studies (serum iron, TIBC, ferritin) — if not already done at B1-09.
- Hb electrophoresis / HPLC (thalassemia trait, SCD trait, HbH, HbE).
- B12, folate (if macrocytic — cross B1-09).
- LDH, haptoglobin, bilirubin (indirect), Coombs test (if haemolysis suspected).
- If pancytopenia → §7.2 urgent (bone-marrow failure or malignancy).

### 4.2 Thalassemia-trait identification
- Hb electrophoresis / HPLC: HbA2 > 3.5% = beta-thal trait; HbF, HbS, HbE patterns.
- MCV < 80 fL with normal iron studies = thal trait until proven otherwise.
- Genetic counselling: carrier risk, partner testing before marriage/pregnancy, prenatal diagnosis options.
- Thal trait = does NOT need iron (iron is contraindicated if iron stores normal).

### 4.3 Bleeding-disorder screening
- PT, aPTT, platelet count, bleeding time (if available).
- Prolonged aPTT with normal PT → intrinsic pathway (haemophilia screen: factor VIII, IX activity).
- Normal PT/aPTT with prolonged bleeding → von Willebrand disease screen (vWF antigen, vWF activity, factor VIII).
- Platelet function disorders → §7.3 haematology for platelet aggregation studies.

### 4.4 ITP assessment
- Typical: age 2–10; preceded by viral illness; isolated thrombocytopenia (platelet < 100,000); no hepatosplenomegaly; no lymphadenopathy; no blast cells on smear; child otherwise well.
- Atypical features (any) → §7.2: age < 1 or > 10, pancytopenia, hepatosplenomegaly, lymphadenopathy, bone pain, weight loss.
- Age < 1 year: consider non-accidental injury (NAI) in the differential for unexplained bruising/petechiae in infants — mandatory safeguarding screen.

### 4.5 Lymphadenopathy evaluation
- > 2 cm, non-tender, fixed, > 4 weeks → FBC, peripheral smear, ESR, CRP, LDH, uric acid, chest X-ray.
- If suspicious (peripheral blast cells, B-symptoms, hepatosplenomegaly) → §7.2 oncology within 7 days.
- If reactive (tender, mobile, < 2 cm, recent infection) → observe 4 weeks; if persistent → workup.

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | Thalassemia trait identified; G6PD deficiency confirmed; isolated mild anaemia responding to workup-guided treatment | §6.1 — counsel + monitor |
| **Moderate** | ITP typical (platelet 20–100K, no bleeding); anaemia workup in progress; von Willebrand trait | §6.2 — observe ITP; treat anaemia per cause |
| **Severe** | ITP with platelet < 20K or active mucosal bleeding; severe anaemia (Hb 5–7); haemolysis confirmed | §6.3 / §7 |
| **Beyond Tier 1** | Thalassemia major; SCD with complications; haemophilia; malignancy; pancytopenia | §7.2 / §7.3 |

### Boundary rules
- Hb < 5 = §7.1 ER. Hb 5–7 = §7.2 + same-week B1-18. Hb 7–cutoff = B1-18 manages.
- Pancytopenia = always §7.2 (never manage at B1-18 without haematology input).
- ITP typical (platelet > 20,000, no mucosal bleeding) = B1-18 observes. ITP with platelet ≤ 20,000 OR mucosal bleeding = §6.2 treatment initiation + §7.3 haematology guidance. ITP with platelet < 10,000 OR severe bleeding = §7.2 urgent haematology.
- Thalassemia TRAIT = B1-18 counsels. Thalassemia MAJOR = §7.3 haematology.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier
- Thalassemia trait: genetic counselling; partner-testing advice; NO iron supplementation if iron-replete.
- G6PD deficiency: avoidance list (fava beans, naphthalene, specific medications — primaquine, dapsone, sulfonamides); parent-app G6PD card activated; school/medication awareness letter.
- Mild anaemia of identified cause: treat per cause (B12 for macrocytic, folate, iron if actually deficient).

### 6.2 Standard tier
- ITP (typical, platelet 20–100K, no bleeding): observation; activity restriction (no contact sports until platelet > 50K); weekly platelet count × 4 weeks; if spontaneous recovery → discharge. If persisting > 3 months → chronic ITP → §7.3.
- ITP (platelet ≤ 20,000 or mucosal bleeding): initiate treatment per haematology guidance (IVIG or short-course prednisolone) — route §7.3 for haematology input before treatment if not emergent; §7.2 if platelet < 10,000 or severe bleeding.
- von Willebrand disease type 1: desmopressin (DDAVP) trial for response assessment → §7.3 haematology for ongoing management plan.

### 6.3 Intensive tier
- Severe anaemia (Hb 5–7, stable): §7.2 same-week to haematology + hospital with transfusion capacity.
- Haemolysis: stabilise; route §7.2.

### 6.4 G6PD management programme
- Avoidance counselling; G6PD card issuance; annual education reinforcement; school letter.

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency (same day)
- [ ] **Hb < 5 g/dL OR haemodynamic compromise at any Hb** → ER with transfusion capacity.
- [ ] **Active severe bleeding with coagulopathy** → ER.
- [ ] **Acute haemolytic crisis** (jaundice + dark urine + rapidly dropping Hb — G6PD, autoimmune) → ER.

### 7.2 Urgent (within 7 days)
- [ ] **Hb 5–7 g/dL (stable)** → haematology + hospital.
- [ ] **Pancytopenia** → haematology/oncology.
- [ ] **Lymphadenopathy with malignancy features** (B-symptoms, blast cells, hepatosplenomegaly) → oncology.
- [ ] **ITP atypical** → haematology.
- [ ] **ITP platelet < 10,000 OR severe/life-threatening bleeding** → haematology urgent.

### 7.3 Routine (within 4 weeks)
- [ ] **Thalassemia major / intermedia confirmed** → haematology centre.
- [ ] **Sickle-cell disease confirmed** → haematology centre.
- [ ] **Haemophilia confirmed (factor VIII/IX < 40%)** → haematology.
- [ ] **von Willebrand disease (types 2, 3)** → haematology.
- [ ] **Chronic ITP (> 3 months)** → haematology.
- [ ] **Unexplained persistent cytopenias** → haematology for bone marrow.
- [ ] **Persistent lymphadenopathy (> 4 weeks, > 2 cm, workup inconclusive)** → biopsy via surgery/oncology.

### 7.4 Handoff: FBC, peripheral smear, Hb electrophoresis, iron studies, coagulation profile, LDH/haptoglobin, reticulocyte count.

## §8–§12 (Compressed)

**§8:** ITP: weekly platelet × 4 weeks → monthly × 3 months. Thal trait: annual FBC. Anaemia: per cause treatment, recheck at 4 weeks.

**§9:** Standard pattern. Key: FBC/smear result display with morphology classification; Hb electrophoresis result; ITP platelet trend graph.

**§10:** Thal trait: "This is a carrier state — not a disease. [He] is healthy and will remain healthy. The reason we identify it is so that when [he] grows up and chooses a partner, we can test them too. If both partners carry the trait, there is a chance their children could have thalassemia major, which is a serious blood condition. Testing prevents this." ITP: "[Her] body is temporarily making antibodies against her own platelets. This is very common after viral infections in children, and most of the time the platelet count recovers on its own within a few weeks to months."

**§10.5:** Thal carrier stigma in Indian marriage context — frame as empowerment, not defect. G6PD — "fava bean and mothball" awareness specific to Indian households. Bleeding-disorder stigma in boys — normalise.

**§11:** Tiering: anaemia workup = Basic/Advanced; ITP + thal + bleeding = Premium.

**§12:** Metrics: ≥ 95% thal-trait identified children receive genetic counselling; ITP observation-only rate ≥ 70% (avoid over-treatment); ≥ 100% pancytopenia referred §7.2.
