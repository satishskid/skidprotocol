# SKIDS Adolescent Clinic

> The SKIDS Adolescent Clinic delivers structured psychosocial
> screening (HEEADSSS), pubertal-development assessment, contraception
> and STI counselling, menstrual-health management, sports-clearance
> evaluation, and joint care for adolescent presentations across the
> behavioural / nutritional / endocrine / dermatological domains.
> Active suicidality, severe substance dependence, severe eating
> disorder, suspected pregnancy, and confirmed STI requiring partner
> notification route per §7. The privacy / confidentiality framework
> from A1 and B1-08 is the operational core.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Conducts structured HEEADSSS psychosocial interviews for every adolescent visit, with the documented privacy framework [AAP-BF-4E].
- Performs Tanner staging, menarche-history-taking, and assessment of pubertal trajectory (timing, tempo, sequence); routes pubertal disorders to §7.3.
- Counsels on contraception (barrier, hormonal, emergency) per Indian-context cultural and legal framing, with private adolescent agency per the framework documented in §10.5.
- Screens, counsels, and tests for sexually transmitted infections at adolescent request and per HEEADSSS-driven indication.
- Manages dysmenorrhoea, primary amenorrhoea workup, secondary amenorrhoea workup (with B1-09 / endocrinology cross-care), oligomenorrhoea, abnormal uterine bleeding, premenstrual dysphoric symptoms.
- Manages mild adolescent acne in joint-care with B1-05 where psychosocial impact is significant.
- Manages adolescent obesity components in joint-care with B1-10 Obesity & Metabolic.
- Conducts adolescent depression / anxiety initial assessment and routes to B1-08 for management; co-manages where psychosocial dimension is dominant.
- Conducts substance-use brief intervention (CRAFFT-graded) and routes severe SUD per §7.
- Performs sports-clearance and pre-participation evaluations.
- Counsels on body-image, healthy relationships, online safety, road safety, helmet use.
- Provides safe transition to adult care at age 18 with structured handoff.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Initiate antipsychotics or mood stabilisers (§7.3 to pediatric psychiatry).
- Manage active suicidality (§7.1 emergency, joint with B1-08).
- Manage suspected pregnancy beyond initial confirmation + counselling (§7.1 / §7.2 to obstetric / family-planning specialist depending on context).
- Initiate isotretinoin (§7.3 per B1-05).
- Manage confirmed pregnancy beyond appropriate handoff.
- Manage severe substance dependence (§7.2 to addiction medicine).
- Manage primary amenorrhoea ≥ age 15 with workup-confirmed pathology (→ B2-01 Endocrine if available, otherwise §7.3).
- Manage suspected disorders of sexual development (DSD) or significant pubertal disorders (→ B2-01).

**Age range covered:** 10 – 18 years (with handoff at 18 to adult primary care).

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 routing supported. First B1-13 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — up to 4 B1-13 visits per year included; HPV vaccination programme included; menstrual-health management included.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited B1-13 visits within the clinically indicated set; LISSUN therapy sessions for psychosocial dimension included; priority §7 partner referral.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 with a key allied-staff dependency on the SKIDS adolescent counsellor (often shared role with B1-08 counsellor) and on the LISSUN partnership for therapy. Protocol governance with named adolescent medicine specialist or DBP with adolescent training. Complex tier per §7 escalates to pediatric psychiatry, pediatric / adolescent gynaecology partner, B2-01 Endocrine (where available), or named adolescent-medicine specialist for unusual presentations.

**Departures:**

1. *Privacy framework strict.* Per A1 / B1-08, adolescent psychosocial information is confidential except for self-harm intent, harm-to-others intent, abuse / exploitation. This is more restrictive than typical Indian primary-care practice [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm legal alignment with POCSO mandatory reporting and family-engagement expectations].

2. *Contraception counselling delivery.* B1-13 provides counselling and (where adolescent is competent and the counselling indicates) hormonal-method initiation. Some Indian centres restrict contraception counselling to gynaecology / family-planning specialists. The departure aligns with AAP / WHO youth-friendly-services framing but requires careful cultural delivery [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm SKIDS network position].

3. *Disclosure-to-parent on substance use.* Adolescent substance use disclosed under privacy framework is not disclosed to parent except where escalation to §7 / safety concern fires. This is more restrictive than typical Indian practice but is the operational enabler of adolescent disclosure and brief-intervention engagement.

4. *POCSO mandatory reporting.* Confirmed sexual abuse / exploitation triggers POCSO mandatory reporting; this overrides confidentiality. Framing for the adolescent is matter-of-fact and supportive.

5. *Age-of-consent for hormonal contraception.* Indian legal framework around adolescent contraception is complex; B1-13 follows the framework that adolescent's reproductive-health decisions are confidential when the adolescent is competent to decide and is at low safeguarding risk. Operational details require confirmation [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS].

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Structured history

Visit structure: parent + adolescent present for medical/general history; **then private adolescent interview for HEEADSSS**, following the documented confidentiality script (see §10.5). Visit time allocation: 45–60 minutes.

HEEADSSS components (private interview):
- **H**ome — household composition, relationships, recent changes, conflict.
- **E**ducation / Employment — school engagement, performance, attendance, bullying, academic stressors.
- **E**ating — diet, weight perception, eating-disorder screen (SCOFF), body image.
- **A**ctivities — peers, hobbies, screen time, online behaviour, gaming, social media.
- **D**rugs — alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, prescription misuse, others (CRAFFT).
- **S**exuality — relationship status, sexual activity, contraception, STI risk, gender identity, sexual orientation (asked sensitively, not assumed).
- **S**uicide / self-harm — direct screen with PHQ-A; explicit ask if PHQ-A 9 not used.
- **S**afety — exposure to violence, abuse, weapons, road / online safety.

Medical history (with parent if adolescent consents): atopic comorbidities, chronic conditions, medications, immunisation status, family history, prior surgeries, prior hospitalisations.

Menstrual history (in girls; conducted privately): age of menarche, cycle length, regularity, duration, flow, dysmenorrhoea, premenstrual symptoms.

### 4.2 Examination

- Growth: BMI, plot on IAP 5–18 chart.
- BP (per AAP-2017 in adolescent).
- Tanner staging (with chaperone + consent; only when clinically necessary).
- General + dermatological (acne grading; B1-05 cross).
- Cardiovascular (especially for sports-clearance — murmur, BP, peripheral pulses).
- Thyroid examination.
- Spine examination (scoliosis screening from age 8).
- Where indicated: pelvic / breast examination — B1-13 generally does NOT perform; routes to gynaecology partner if needed.
- For STI symptoms: targeted external genital examination with chaperone + consent, OR routes to STI specialist if examiner not comfortable.

### 4.3 Scales

| Scale | Domain | Cutoffs |
|---|---|---|
| HEEADSSS (structured) | Psychosocial | Disclosure of harm = §7 |
| PHQ-A | Depression | Per B1-08 §4.3 |
| GAD-7 | Anxiety | Per B1-08 §4.3 |
| CRAFFT | SUD | Per B1-08 §4.3 |
| SCOFF | Eating disorder | ≥ 2 → workup + §7.3 if criteria |
| Tanner stages | Pubertal | Sequence and timing assessed against age norms |
| BMI percentile | Weight status | < 5th underweight; 85th–95th overweight; ≥ 95th obese (B1-10 cross) |

### 4.4 In-clinic device tests

- BP cuff (adult and large adolescent sizes).
- Stadiometer + scale.
- Urine pregnancy test (β-hCG).
- Urine dipstick for STI / UTI screening as adjunct.
- Privacy room for HEEADSSS and any examination.

### 4.5 Laboratory

Order on indication:
- Urine pregnancy test (β-hCG) — for menstrual-irregularity workup, suspected pregnancy, before any teratogen prescription (isotretinoin via B1-05; SSRI per B1-08).
- FBC + ferritin — for menorrhagia, fatigue, eating-disorder workup.
- TSH — for menstrual irregularity, weight change, mood symptoms.
- Lipid screen — once between 17–21 per AAP universal.
- HbA1c — if obesity (cross with B1-10) or family history T1DM/T2DM.
- STI testing per CDC-STI-2021: HIV (with pre-and-post-test counselling), syphilis, gonorrhoea / chlamydia (urine NAAT preferred), hepatitis B, others by risk profile.
- LH / FSH / oestradiol / prolactin / testosterone — for primary amenorrhoea workup ≥ age 15 OR pubertal-disorder workup; results inform §7.3 routing.
- Fasting insulin / glucose / SHBG — for suspected PCOS workup, pre-routing to B2-01.

### 4.6 Decision tree

```
Adolescent visit
        │
        ▼
General history with parent + private HEEADSSS with adolescent
        │
        ├── §7.1 emergency triggers (suicidality, abuse, severe SUD, severe ED) → §7.1 immediate
        │
        ▼
        ├── Psychosocial concerns → §6 + B1-08 cross-care
        ├── Menstrual concerns → §4.5 workup → §6 management or §7
        ├── Contraception request → §6.4 private counselling + provision
        ├── STI risk / request → §4.5 testing + §6 / §7
        ├── Suspected pregnancy → urine β-hCG → §6 / §7
        ├── Acne / dermatological → joint with B1-05
        ├── Weight / nutrition / ED → joint with B1-09 / B1-10; §7 if ED criteria
        ├── Pubertal concern → §4.5 workup → §7.3 if pathology
        ├── Sports clearance → §4 cardiac + Tanner → §6 clearance / restrict
        │
        ▼
§6 plan + privacy-respected post-visit summary + manager handoff
```

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier

- HEEADSSS-driven counselling (Mild concerns; reassurance, parent-app activations).
- Sleep / nutrition / activity counselling.
- HPV vaccination per IAP IIS (girls and boys; ideally before sexual debut; catch-up through age 26).
- Tdap booster, MenACWY (where indicated), annual influenza.
- Routine periodic-visit counselling (safety, online behaviour, body image, healthy relationships).
- Sports-clearance: per cardiac history + exam; restrict / cleared / refer per AHA pre-participation framework.

### 6.2 Standard tier

**Dysmenorrhoea (Moderate):**
- Naproxen 500 mg PO BD or ibuprofen 400 mg PO TDS, started day before period if predictable, continued for 2–3 days.
- Heat application; lifestyle.
- If not adequately controlled after 3 cycles, consider combined oral contraceptive (COC) — discussed with adolescent (and with parent if adolescent consents); levonorgestrel-containing or drospirenone-containing OCP per cycle effects.

**Menorrhagia (Moderate):**
- NSAID + tranexamic acid 1 g PO TDS during heaviest days.
- Iron supplementation (joint with B1-09).
- COC / progestin if NSAID + tranexamic insufficient.
- §7.3 if structural / bleeding-disorder suspected (von Willebrand disease workup is appropriate at §7.3).

**Adolescent depression / anxiety Moderate:**
Joint with B1-08 — fluoxetine + LISSUN CBT per B1-08 §6.2.

**Adolescent SUD CRAFFT ≥ 2:**
Joint with B1-08 § 6.2 — brief intervention programme.

**STI treatment (uncomplicated):**
- Gonorrhoea: ceftriaxone 500 mg IM single dose [CDC-STI-2021].
- Chlamydia: doxycycline 100 mg PO BD × 7 days OR azithromycin 1 g PO single (where doxycycline contraindicated).
- Trichomoniasis: metronidazole 2 g PO single OR 500 mg BD × 7 days.
- Counsel: partner notification, abstain or barrier-only until course complete + 7 days, retest at 3 months.

**Suspected pregnancy:**
- Confirm with β-hCG.
- Options counselling, neutral framing, all legal options reviewed (continuation with prenatal care + handoff; medical / surgical termination per Indian law — Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act framework).
- Route per §7 to appropriate specialist.

### 6.3 Intensive tier
- Active suicidality = §7.1 (joint with B1-08).
- Severe ED with crisis features = §7.2.
- PCOS workup with full hormonal panel + lifestyle programme + B2-01 routing + B1-10 obesity programme co-management.

### 6.4 Contraception counselling and provision

The counselling is conducted privately with the adolescent. Goals:
- Establish goals (contraception, cycle regulation, dysmenorrhoea management, acne, all of the above).
- Method-specific education: efficacy, side-effect profile, ease of use, accessibility, cost, partner-disclosure dynamics.
- Indian-context cultural framing: respect family-and-religious context without coercing disclosure.

Methods at B1-13:
- **Barrier:** condoms — counselling + provision; emphasise dual protection (STI + pregnancy).
- **Combined oral contraceptive (COC):** pre-screen for contraindications (smoking, migraine with aura, thrombotic risk, uncontrolled HTN, severe liver disease); start with low-dose ethinylestradiol-containing OCP; review at 6 weeks then 3-monthly.
- **Progestin-only pill (POP):** alternative where COC contraindicated.
- **Depot medroxyprogesterone (DMPA):** every 3 months IM; counsel on bone-density implications and reversibility.
- **Long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) — IUD (Cu-IUD or LNG-IUS), implant:** B1-13 generally counsels and routes to gynaecology partner for insertion; SKIDS may not insert IUDs at all clinics depending on training [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS].
- **Emergency contraception:** levonorgestrel 1.5 mg PO single dose within 72h (up to 120h with reduced efficacy); ulipristal acetate 30 mg up to 120h alternative.

### 6.5 Tapering / stopping
- COC: continued as long as goals met and no contraindications develop; annual review.
- DMPA: every 3 months; option to discontinue any time.
- NSAIDs: cycle-related use; not chronic.
- SSRI for depression: per B1-08 §6.7.

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral

- [ ] **Active suicidality** — per B1-08 §7.1. → ER + LISSUN psychiatrist.
- [ ] **Disclosure of ongoing abuse / exploitation / trafficking** — POCSO mandatory reporting. → Child-protection authority + ER + LISSUN.
- [ ] **Acute substance intoxication / withdrawal.** → ER.
- [ ] **Severe eating disorder with crisis features** (bradycardia, postural hypotension, electrolyte abnormality, syncope). → ER + pediatric psychiatry.
- [ ] **Suspected ectopic pregnancy / acute abdominal pain with positive β-hCG.** → ER + obstetric / gynaecology.
- [ ] **Acute pelvic / scrotal pain with severe presentation** (suspected ovarian torsion, testicular torsion). → ER + surgical specialty.
- [ ] **Severe acute mental health crisis** (acute psychosis, mania). → ER.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Severe depression / anxiety not crisis** — per B1-08 §7.2.
- [ ] **Severe SUD** — per B1-08 §7.2.
- [ ] **Adolescent eating disorder rapid weight loss** — per B1-09 / B1-08.
- [ ] **Confirmed pregnancy** with options-counselling complete. → Obstetric / family-planning specialist within 7 days.
- [ ] **Confirmed HIV** → ART centre within 7 days.
- [ ] **Suspected PID** → Gynaecology within 7 days.
- [ ] **Suspected pregnancy of concern (under age of consent + risk of coercion)** → Child-protection assessment + safeguarding-led pathway.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)

- [ ] **Pubertal disorders** (precocious, delayed, DSD suspected) → endocrinology / B2-01.
- [ ] **PCOS workup completion** → endocrinology / B2-01.
- [ ] **Primary amenorrhoea ≥ 15** with workup → endocrinology / gynaecology.
- [ ] **Confirmed syphilis** → STI specialist.
- [ ] **Confirmed hepatitis B / C** → hepatology.
- [ ] **Refractory dysmenorrhoea / menorrhagia** → gynaecology.
- [ ] **Heavy menstrual bleeding with bleeding-disorder suspicion** (von Willebrand workup) → haematology.
- [ ] **Refractory eating-disorder behaviours without crisis** → pediatric psychiatry + B1-09.
- [ ] **Family-conflict severe with safeguarding concern** (ongoing but not acute) → child-protection + family-therapy partner.
- [ ] **Sports-clearance with cardiac findings** → pediatric cardiology / B1-16.

### 7.4 Handoff communication

Standard packet with adolescent-privacy-respected content + safeguarding details where indicated.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Routine adolescent visit:

> "We do part of this visit with you here, [parent], and part privately with [Aanya]. The private part is so [she] can talk about anything where privacy matters — mental health, body, relationships, anything. What [she] shares with me stays private unless [she] tells me [she] wants to harm herself, harm someone else, or is being hurt by someone. Then I'd involve you. Otherwise, I keep it confidential. This is the same script we use with every adolescent, not specific to [her]."

Contraception counselling (private):

> "Several methods work for what you're asking about. Let's go through them — what each one does, how often you'd use it, what the side effects are, and how easy it is to keep up. There's no right or wrong choice for you; whatever fits your life. We can change methods anytime."

§7.1 suicidality (per B1-08 script).

POCSO mandatory disclosure:

> "What you've told me is something I have to share with the child-protection team and with your parent / caregiver. The reason: it's the law, and it's there to protect you. I'm telling you what's about to happen so you're not surprised. You did the right thing telling me. The team will support you through what comes next."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

- *Privacy framework absolute (within legal limits).* Confidentiality script identical for every adolescent. Parents are not entitled to confidential adolescent information without adolescent consent unless §7.1 / POCSO criteria fire.
- *Contraception framing.* Some Indian families view adolescent contraception as forbidden. Engage adolescent privately; respect family system; do not expose adolescent-disclosed sexual activity to parent without consent. If contraception need is established and parent disclosure would create safeguarding risk, manage confidentially per the framework.
- *Sexual orientation and gender identity.* Asked sensitively, never assumed. Use adolescent's own language. Confidentiality protected.
- *Marriage and family-planning conversations in adolescent girls.* Indian family conversations about marriage / fertility may surface in clinic. Engage clinically; protect adolescent autonomy.
- *POCSO mandatory reporting overrides confidentiality.* Confirmed sexual abuse / exploitation = mandatory reporting; the script for the adolescent (above §10.1) is matter-of-fact.
- *Religious / cultural contraception preferences.* Respect; offer all evidence-based options; never coerce.
- *Caste, class, language* — same-script-for-everyone discipline.
- *Gender-based violence.* Direct screen via HEEADSSS. Disclosure → safeguarding pathway.
- *Body-image and beauty narratives.* Receive clinically; manage; route per ED screen.
