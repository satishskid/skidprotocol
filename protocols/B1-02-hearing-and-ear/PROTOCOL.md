# SKIDS Hearing & Ear Clinic

> The SKIDS Hearing & Ear Clinic delivers OAE-based hearing surveillance
> aligned with JCIH 1-3-6, tympanometry and audiometric evaluation,
> otitis media management (AOM and OME), cerumen management, hearing-
> aid candidacy assessment and routing, speech-language cross-care for
> hearing-related delay, and ENT surgical-partner routing for
> grommets, adenoidectomy, and cochlear-implant candidacy. UNHS
> reconciliation at birth-visit is the front door.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Performs and interprets otoacoustic emissions (OAE) screening at the 7 A1-defined timepoints (newborn, 9m, 18m, 4y, 8y, 12y, 16y) using the SoundScout or equivalent device [AAP-BF-4E].
- Reconciles Universal Newborn Hearing Screening (UNHS) results at first SKIDS visit; initiates JCIH 1-3-6 pathway (screen by 1 month, diagnose by 3 months, intervene by 6 months) for newborn refer results [JCIH-2019].
- Performs tympanometry (226 Hz standard; 1000 Hz for infants < 6 months) to assess middle-ear function; classifies per Jerger types (A, B, C, As, Ad).
- Performs pure-tone audiometry (play audiometry 3–5 years; conventional audiometry ≥ 5 years) in the in-clinic sound booth or quiet room; classifies hearing loss by type (conductive / sensorineural / mixed), laterality, and severity (WHO classification: mild 26–40 dB, moderate 41–60 dB, severe 61–80 dB, profound > 80 dB).
- Diagnoses and manages acute otitis media (AOM) per AAP 2013 guidelines: watchful waiting for non-severe AOM in ≥ 2 years; amoxicillin first-line for treated AOM [AAP-2013-AOM].
- Diagnoses and monitors otitis media with effusion (OME): watchful waiting for ≤ 3 months; audiometric assessment; routes to ENT partner for grommet consideration if OME persists > 3 months with hearing loss ≥ 25 dB [AAO-HNS-2016-OME].
- Manages cerumen impaction using curette or irrigation per AAO-HNS guidelines; does not use ear candling [AAO-HNS-2016-Cerumen].
- Screens for hearing-related speech-language delay (cross with B1-11 Speech & Language when drafted; cross with B1-23 Developmental Surveillance when drafted).
- Refers for hearing-aid fitting (mild-moderate SNHL) to named audiologist partner; refers for cochlear-implant candidacy assessment (bilateral severe-profound SNHL) to named ENT surgeon partner (Tier 3).
- Counsels on noise-induced hearing loss prevention in adolescents (earphone/headphone use, concert exposure, occupational noise for working adolescents).

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform auditory brainstem response (ABR) or auditory steady-state response (ASSR) — these require specialised equipment and personnel; route to named pediatric audiology partner for diagnostic ABR when OAE refer is confirmed and behavioural audiometry is not developmentally possible.
- Insert grommets (ventilation tubes) — §7.3 to ENT surgeon partner.
- Perform adenoidectomy or tonsillectomy — §7.3 to ENT surgeon partner.
- Manage cholesteatoma, mastoiditis, or complicated AOM — §7.1/§7.2 to ENT surgeon.
- Perform cochlear-implant surgery or manage cochlear-implant programming — §7.3 to ENT surgeon + cochlear-implant centre.
- Manage sensorineural hearing loss beyond initial diagnosis, audiometric classification, and referral — ongoing management with audiologist and ENT partner.
- Manage facial-nerve palsy (§7.2 to ENT/neurology).

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (with UNHS reconciliation at newborn visit).

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — OAE screening at A1 periodic visits; otoscopy; AOM management.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — full audiometric assessment (tympanometry + pure-tone audiometry); OME monitoring programme; cerumen management; speech-language cross-care referral.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — serial audiometric monitoring for children with hearing loss; priority ENT partner referral; hearing-aid candidacy coordination; noise-exposure counselling programme.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 with allied-staff dependency on the SKIDS audiologist or audiometry-trained nurse for tympanometry and pure-tone audiometry. OAE screening can be performed by trained nurse without audiologist. Protocol governance with named pediatric audiologist or pediatric ENT. Complex tier per §7 escalates to ENT surgeon partner (Tier 3) for surgical intervention and to pediatric audiology partner for diagnostic ABR / cochlear-implant candidacy.

**Departures from cited guidelines:**

1. *ABR not performed at SKIDS.* JCIH recommends diagnostic ABR for confirmed-refer newborns and for infants who cannot perform behavioural audiometry. SKIDS does not stock ABR equipment; confirmed-refer newborns are routed per §7.2 to pediatric audiology partner for diagnostic ABR within the JCIH 1-3-6 timeline. This is a logistics departure.

2. *OAE as primary screening tool (not AABR).* JCIH permits OAE or AABR for UNHS. SKIDS uses OAE (SoundScout) because it is faster, cheaper, and trainable to non-audiologist nurses. OAE misses auditory neuropathy spectrum disorder (ANSD); risk-indicator-driven AABR routing compensates (§4.3 risk indicators → refer for diagnostic ABR regardless of OAE result).

3. *OME 3-month watchful waiting.* AAO-HNS recommends 3 months of watchful waiting before considering surgical intervention. Some Indian ENT practices intervene earlier. SKIDS follows the 3-month guideline to reduce unnecessary surgery; the departure is documented for parents who request earlier intervention.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 UNHS reconciliation (newborn / first SKIDS visit)

1. Confirm whether UNHS was performed at birthing facility.
2. If UNHS performed and passed: document; schedule routine OAE at 9-month A1 visit.
3. If UNHS performed and referred: confirm referral result; initiate JCIH 1-3-6:
   - If already > 1 month of age and no diagnostic ABR done → §7.2 urgent to pediatric audiology partner within 7 days.
   - If diagnostic ABR done and normal: document; routine surveillance.
   - If diagnostic ABR done and abnormal: classify; initiate early intervention per §7.3.
4. If UNHS not performed (home delivery, facility without UNHS): perform OAE at this visit; if refer → repeat in 2 weeks; if confirmed refer → §7.2.
5. Review risk indicators for delayed-onset hearing loss (see §4.3); if present, schedule 6-monthly OAE surveillance regardless of initial pass.

### 4.2 Audiometric assessment (age-appropriate)

**OAE (all ages):**
- Screening tool; pass/refer binary.
- Distortion-product OAE (DPOAE) or transient-evoked OAE (TEOAE) per device.
- Pass = normal outer hair cell function at tested frequencies.
- Refer = possible hearing loss; proceed to tympanometry + audiometry.
- Limitation: OAE does not detect ANSD; does not detect mild conductive loss.

**Tympanometry:**
- 226 Hz probe for children ≥ 6 months; 1000 Hz probe for infants < 6 months.
- Classification per Jerger:
  - **Type A:** normal middle-ear function.
  - **Type B (flat):** middle-ear effusion or TM perforation. If with normal ear-canal volume = OME; with large volume = perforation.
  - **Type C:** negative middle-ear pressure (Eustachian tube dysfunction).
  - **Type As:** reduced compliance (ossicular fixation — rare in children).
  - **Type Ad:** high compliance (ossicular discontinuity or hypermobile TM).

**Pure-tone audiometry:**
- Play audiometry: 3–5 years (conditioned play response).
- Conventional audiometry: ≥ 5 years.
- Test at 250, 500, 1000, 2000, 4000, 8000 Hz (air conduction); 500, 1000, 2000, 4000 Hz (bone conduction if air-conduction threshold elevated).
- Classify:
  - **Type:** conductive (air-bone gap > 10 dB), sensorineural (both elevated equally), mixed.
  - **Severity (WHO):** mild 26–40 dB, moderate 41–60 dB, severe 61–80 dB, profound > 80 dB.
  - **Laterality:** unilateral, bilateral.
  - **Configuration:** flat, sloping, rising, notched (noise-induced).

**Speech audiometry (where available):**
- Speech reception threshold (SRT) and speech discrimination score (SDS).
- Useful for confirming pure-tone results and assessing functional hearing.

### 4.3 Risk indicators for delayed-onset hearing loss

Per JCIH-2019, children with any of the following require 6-monthly hearing surveillance through age 3 regardless of initial screen result:
- Family history of permanent childhood hearing loss.
- NICU stay > 5 days.
- Hyperbilirubinaemia requiring exchange transfusion.
- Aminoglycoside exposure (> 5 days cumulative).
- Ototoxic medication (cisplatin, loop diuretics).
- Congenital CMV, rubella, toxoplasmosis, meningitis.
- Craniofacial anomalies (including cleft palate, ear anomalies, microtia).
- Syndromes associated with hearing loss (Down, Waardenburg, Usher, Treacher Collins, etc.).
- Neurodegenerative disorder.
- Head trauma with temporal bone fracture.

### 4.4 AOM pathway

**Diagnosis per AAP-2013-AOM:**
- Moderate-severe bulging of TM, OR new-onset otorrhoea not from otitis externa, OR mild bulging of TM with recent (< 48h) onset ear pain or intense erythema of TM.
- Distinguish from OME (TM not bulging; no acute symptoms).

**Severity classification:**
- **Non-severe AOM:** mild otalgia < 48h, temperature < 39°C.
- **Severe AOM:** moderate-severe otalgia, otalgia ≥ 48h, OR temperature ≥ 39°C.
- **Recurrent AOM:** ≥ 3 episodes in 6 months OR ≥ 4 in 12 months with ≥ 1 in past 6 months.

### 4.5 OME pathway

**Diagnosis per AAO-HNS-2016-OME:**
- TM retracted or neutral with amber/blue discolouration; air-fluid level visible; pneumatic otoscopy with reduced mobility; tympanometry Type B.
- No acute symptoms (no otalgia, no fever).
- Duration: classify as acute (< 3 months) or chronic (≥ 3 months).

### 4.6 Cerumen assessment

- Visualise TM obstructed by cerumen.
- If TM visible and cerumen not impacted: no intervention needed.
- If TM not visible and hearing assessment / otoscopy needed: cerumen removal per §6.5.

### 4.7 Decision tree

```
Child presents with hearing concern / ear complaint
        │
        ▼
§4.1 UNHS reconciliation (newborn / first visit)
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

### 5.1 Hearing loss severity (WHO classification)

| Severity | Threshold (better ear, pure-tone average 500-4000 Hz) | Impact | Intervention tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | ≤ 25 dB | None | Surveillance per A1 |
| **Mild** | 26–40 dB | Difficulty hearing soft speech; may affect school performance | §6.6 + monitoring; hearing-aid assessment if bilateral and persistent |
| **Moderate** | 41–60 dB | Difficulty hearing normal conversational speech | §6.6 hearing-aid referral + §7.3 audiology partner |
| **Severe** | 61–80 dB | Only hears loud speech or amplified sound | §7.3 hearing-aid fitting + cochlear-implant candidacy assessment |
| **Profound** | > 80 dB | Cannot hear speech without amplification | §7.3 cochlear-implant candidacy → Tier 3 ENT surgeon |

### 5.2 AOM severity

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Non-severe, ≥ 2 years, unilateral** | Mild otalgia < 48h, temp < 39°C | §6.1 watchful waiting option |
| **Non-severe, ≥ 2 years, bilateral** | Mild otalgia < 48h, temp < 39°C, bilateral | §6.2 amoxicillin |
| **Non-severe, < 2 years** | Any | §6.2 amoxicillin (watchful waiting not recommended < 2 years) |
| **Severe, any age** | Moderate-severe otalgia, ≥ 48h, or temp ≥ 39°C | §6.2 amoxicillin (high-dose) |
| **AOM with complications** | Mastoid tenderness, facial-nerve palsy, intracranial signs | §7.1 ER + ENT |

### 5.3 OME severity

| Duration / Impact | Action |
|---|---|
| OME < 3 months | §6.3 watchful waiting; recheck at 3 months |
| OME ≥ 3 months with hearing ≥ 25 dB (normal) | Continue monitoring every 3 months |
| OME ≥ 3 months with hearing < 25 dB (hearing loss ≥ 25 dB) | §7.3 ENT for grommet consideration |
| OME ≥ 3 months in child with speech-language delay | §7.3 ENT for grommet + B1-11 speech cross-care |
| OME ≥ 3 months in child with learning / developmental concern | §7.3 ENT for grommet + B1-23 developmental cross-care |

### 5.4 Boundary rules

- First-episode non-severe AOM in ≥ 2 years, unilateral: watchful waiting is acceptable; antibiotic only if no improvement at 48–72h.
- Bilateral AOM in < 2 years: always treat with antibiotic (no watchful waiting per AAP-2013-AOM).
- OME: the 3-month watchful-waiting threshold is non-negotiable before surgical referral unless the child has significant speech-language delay or developmental concern.
- Sensorineural hearing loss = always §7.3 (beyond B1-02 management scope for ongoing care; B1-02 performs initial diagnosis and audiometric classification).
- Recurrent AOM (≥ 3/6m or ≥ 4/12m) = §7.3 ENT for grommet consideration regardless of current episode severity.
- Unilateral hearing loss: counsel on preferential seating; refer to audiology for FM system assessment; §7.3 only if moderate or worse.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (watchful waiting / preventive)

**AOM watchful waiting (non-severe, ≥ 2 years, unilateral):**
- Analgesic: ibuprofen 10 mg/kg/dose PO TDS or paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose PO QDS × 48–72h.
- Re-evaluate at 48–72h: if no improvement → initiate amoxicillin per §6.2.
- Safety-net instruction: return if fever > 39°C, worsening pain, new discharge, or no improvement in 72h.

**Noise-induced hearing loss prevention (adolescents):**
- Counsel: 60/60 rule (60% maximum volume on earphones, maximum 60 minutes continuous).
- Counsel: hearing protection at concerts, loud environments.
- Parent-app content activation.

**OME observation:**
- Counsel: OME usually resolves spontaneously within 3 months.
- No antihistamines, decongestants, or nasal steroids for OME (no evidence of benefit per AAO-HNS-2016-OME).
- Recheck at 3 months with tympanometry + audiometry.

### 6.2 Standard tier (AOM treatment)

**Amoxicillin first-line:**
- Standard-dose: amoxicillin 80–90 mg/kg/day PO in 2 divided doses × 10 days (< 2 years or severe) or × 5–7 days (≥ 2 years, non-severe) [AAP-2013-AOM].

**Second-line (treatment failure at 48–72h OR amoxicillin allergy):**
- Amoxicillin-clavulanate 90 mg/kg/day (amoxicillin component) PO in 2 divided doses × 10 days.
- OR ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IM single dose × 3 days.
- Penicillin allergy (non-anaphylactic): cefdinir, cefuroxime, or cefpodoxime (IAP-preferred oral cephalosporins).
- Penicillin allergy (anaphylactic): azithromycin or clindamycin (limited evidence; §7.3 ENT if no improvement).

**AOM with TM perforation / otorrhoea:**
- Oral antibiotic as above + topical antibiotic (ofloxacin 0.3% otic drops BD × 7 days; safe with perforation).
- No aminoglycoside-containing otic drops through perforated TM (ototoxicity risk).

**Recurrent AOM prophylaxis:**
- No routine antibiotic prophylaxis (antimicrobial stewardship).
- §7.3 ENT for grommet consideration if ≥ 3 episodes in 6 months or ≥ 4 in 12 months.
- Ensure pneumococcal and influenza vaccination up to date (cross with B1-22).

### 6.3 OME monitoring programme

- Tympanometry + audiometry at 3-month intervals.
- If OME resolves (Type A tympanogram, hearing normal): discharge to A1 surveillance.
- If OME persists ≥ 3 months with hearing loss ≥ 25 dB: §7.3 ENT for grommet consideration.
- If OME persists ≥ 3 months with normal hearing: continue monitoring every 3–6 months; ENT referral if hearing deteriorates or speech-language delay emerges.

### 6.4 Otitis externa management

- Acetic acid 2% + hydrocortisone 1% otic drops TDS × 7 days (first-line for mild).
- Ciprofloxacin 0.3% + dexamethasone 0.1% otic drops BD × 7 days (moderate or not improving).
- Keep ear dry; cotton ball with petroleum jelly for bathing.
- Recheck at 7–10 days; if not improving → §7.3 ENT.
- Malignant otitis externa (immunocompromised, diabetic, severe pain, granulation tissue) → §7.1 ER.

### 6.5 Cerumen management

Per AAO-HNS-2016-Cerumen:
- **Curette removal:** under direct vision with otoscope or headlight; gentle curette technique; appropriate for cooperative children ≥ 4 years.
- **Irrigation:** warm water (body temperature) via syringe or electronic irrigator; contraindicated if known TM perforation, previous ear surgery, or active otitis externa.
- **Cerumenolytic pre-treatment:** sodium bicarbonate ear drops or olive oil drops × 3–5 days before planned irrigation if cerumen very hard.
- **Do NOT use:** ear candles (fire hazard, no efficacy, potential TM damage); cotton buds (impaction risk).

### 6.6 Hearing-loss management pathway

**Mild SNHL (26–40 dB, bilateral):**
- Confirm on repeat audiometry at 4–6 weeks.
- Preferential seating in school.
- Speech-language assessment (B1-11 cross).
- Hearing-aid assessment referral to audiologist partner.
- 6-monthly audiometric monitoring.

**Moderate SNHL (41–60 dB):**
- §7.3 to audiologist partner for hearing-aid fitting.
- B1-11 speech-language therapy cross-care.
- B1-23 developmental surveillance if developmental concern.
- 3–6-monthly audiometric monitoring at B1-02.
- School communication: FM system recommendation.

**Severe-profound SNHL (> 60 dB bilateral):**
- §7.3 to ENT surgeon partner for cochlear-implant candidacy assessment.
- Hearing-aid trial (if CI not immediately available).
- B1-11 speech-language therapy.
- Early-intervention programme referral.
- Genetic counselling referral if syndromic features or family history.

**Conductive hearing loss (isolated, non-OME):**
- §7.3 to ENT for ossicular evaluation if persistent conductive loss without middle-ear effusion.

### 6.7 Contraindications and precautions

| Therapeutic | Contraindication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Ear irrigation | Known TM perforation, previous ear surgery | Use curette only |
| Aminoglycoside otic drops | TM perforation | Use fluoroquinolone drops instead |
| Watchful waiting for AOM | < 2 years bilateral, immunocompromised, cochlear implant, craniofacial anomaly | Treat with antibiotic |
| Nasal decongestants / antihistamines for OME | Any | Not indicated; no evidence of benefit |

### 6.8 Dispensary stock

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)

- [ ] **Acute mastoiditis** — post-auricular swelling, tenderness, erythema, protruding pinna; may have fever, toxic appearance. → ER + ENT surgeon.
- [ ] **Intracranial complication of AOM** — meningismus, altered consciousness, focal neurological deficit, persistent high fever. → ER + ENT + neurosurgery.
- [ ] **Facial-nerve palsy associated with AOM** → ER + ENT surgeon.
- [ ] **Malignant otitis externa** — immunocompromised child, severe pain, granulation tissue in external canal, osteomyelitis suspicion. → ER + ENT.
- [ ] **Sudden sensorineural hearing loss** (SSNHL — acute onset, unilateral, no middle-ear pathology). → ENT within 24h (steroid window).

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Newborn confirmed-refer on OAE / UNHS** — repeat OAE refer confirmed at SKIDS. → Pediatric audiology partner for diagnostic ABR within 7 days (JCIH 1-3-6).
- [ ] **Infant with risk indicators for delayed-onset SNHL + failed OAE at any age** → Pediatric audiology partner within 7 days.
- [ ] **AOM not responding to second-line antibiotic (amoxicillin-clavulanate or ceftriaxone × 3 days)** → ENT for tympanocentesis / myringotomy consideration within 7 days.
- [ ] **Cholesteatoma suspected** — retraction pocket with keratin debris, foul-smelling discharge, conductive hearing loss. → ENT within 7 days.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)

- [ ] **OME ≥ 3 months with hearing loss ≥ 25 dB** → ENT for grommet consideration.
- [ ] **OME ≥ 3 months with speech-language delay** → ENT for grommet + B1-11 speech therapy.
- [ ] **Recurrent AOM (≥ 3 in 6 months or ≥ 4 in 12 months)** → ENT for grommet consideration.
- [ ] **Bilateral SNHL moderate (41–60 dB)** → Audiologist partner for hearing-aid fitting.
- [ ] **Bilateral SNHL severe-profound (> 60 dB)** → ENT surgeon for cochlear-implant candidacy.
- [ ] **Mixed hearing loss** → ENT for ossicular and middle-ear evaluation.
- [ ] **Persistent conductive loss without middle-ear effusion** → ENT for ossicular evaluation.
- [ ] **Unilateral SNHL moderate or worse** → Audiology for FM system + ongoing monitoring.
- [ ] **Tinnitus persistent in child / adolescent** → ENT + audiology.
- [ ] **External ear anomaly / microtia / atresia** → ENT + craniofacial team.
- [ ] **Syndromic hearing loss suspected** (Waardenburg, Usher, Pendred, branchio-oto-renal, etc.) → ENT + clinical genetics.

### 7.4 Handoff communication

Standard packet:
1. Audiometric results (OAE, tympanometry, pure-tone audiogram with air and bone conduction).
2. Type of hearing loss, laterality, severity (WHO classification).
3. AOM/OME history with treatment timeline.
4. Risk indicators for hearing loss.
5. Speech-language developmental status (B1-11 cross-care summary if available).
6. Companion-generated referral summary.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

| Scenario | First follow-up | Subsequent |
|---|---|---|
| AOM (treated) | 48–72h (phone or in-person if not improving) | 4 weeks (confirm resolution; check for OME) |
| AOM (watchful waiting) | 48–72h mandatory | As per treatment outcome |
| OME (observation) | 3 months (tympanometry + audiometry) | Every 3 months until resolution or §7.3 |
| OME (post-grommet) | Per ENT; B1-02 audiometric monitoring at 3 months post-insertion | 6-monthly |
| SNHL (mild, monitoring) | 6 months (repeat audiometry) | 6-monthly |
| SNHL (moderate+, post-hearing-aid) | Per audiologist partner; B1-02 audiometric check at 3 months | 6-monthly at B1-02 |
| Risk-indicator child (delayed-onset risk) | 6-monthly OAE/audiometry through age 3 | Annual thereafter |
| Noise-exposure counselling | Annual at A1 | Annual |

§8.2 What to recheck: audiometric thresholds (for SNHL monitoring); tympanometry (for OME monitoring); TM status (for AOM follow-up); speech-language milestones (cross B1-11/B1-23); school performance impact.

§8.3 Success: AOM resolution with no hearing sequelae; OME resolution within 3 months or successful grommet outcome; SNHL appropriately amplified with hearing aid or cochlear implant and speech-language development on track; JCIH 1-3-6 milestones met for newborn-refer cases.

§8.7 Loss-to-follow-up: critical for newborn-refer (JCIH pathway); Companion alert to manager + parent at 7 days if newborn-refer follow-up not completed. Standard re-engagement for OME and SNHL monitoring.

## §9 Companion software workflow

### 9.1 Doctor Module screens

**Screen 1: Pre-consult summary** — A1 hearing flag status; UNHS result; prior OAE results; prior audiometric results; AOM/OME history; risk indicators for delayed-onset SNHL; B1-11 speech-language cross-reference.

**Screen 4: AOM / OME classification** — structured diagnosis; severity; duration; treatment history.

**Screen 5: Severity grading** — hearing-loss classification (auto-calculated from audiogram); AOM/OME severity; §7 trigger check.

**Screen 6: Therapeutic plan** — antibiotic selection (weight-based dosing auto-calculated); watchful-waiting decision with 48–72h phone follow-up scheduling; cerumen management documentation; hearing-aid referral workflow.

**Screen 7: §7 review** — referral trigger checklist; JCIH milestone tracker for newborn-refer.

**Screen 8: Follow-up scheduling** — auto-suggests next visit per §8; OME monitoring calendar; SNHL monitoring calendar.

### 9.6 Edge cases

- OAE refer in sleeping infant: retry with sleeping infant repositioned; if still refer, schedule repeat in 2 weeks before escalating.
- Child unable to perform pure-tone audiometry (< 3 years or developmental delay): rely on OAE + tympanometry + behavioural observation audiometry (BOA); document limitation; consider §7.3 ABR referral.
- Bilateral cerumen impaction preventing OAE: remove cerumen per §6.5, then perform OAE.
- Crying infant during tympanometry: invalid result; document; retry when calm or at next visit.
- Offline: paper audiometric record; upload when connectivity restored.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Normal OAE result:

> "[Arjun]'s hearing screen today was normal. The device checks whether the inner ear is working properly — it passed on both sides. We screen hearing at regular intervals because some types of hearing loss can develop later. The next screening is at [age]."

OAE refer:

> "Today's hearing screen showed a 'refer' result on [one side / both sides]. This does not necessarily mean [Arjun] has hearing loss — sometimes wax, fluid, or a fussy baby can cause a refer. We will repeat the test in 2 weeks. If it refers again, we will do a more detailed hearing assessment."

AOM:

> "[Arjun] has an ear infection. The eardrum is red and bulging. [He] will need [antibiotic / pain relief and we will wait to see if it clears on its own — I will check [him] again in 2–3 days]. If [he] has severe pain, fever above 39°C, or is not improving in 3 days, call us."

OME:

> "There is fluid behind [Arjun]'s eardrum. This is very common after ear infections and usually goes away on its own within 3 months. It can muffle hearing temporarily, which is why we test [his] hearing today. We will recheck in 3 months. No antibiotics or decongestants will help — the body clears this fluid on its own."

Hearing loss confirmed:

> "[Arjun] has [mild / moderate / severe] hearing loss in [one / both] ear[s]. This means [he] has difficulty hearing [soft speech / normal conversation / most sounds without amplification]. We are going to refer [him] to a specialist audiologist who will [fit a hearing aid / assess whether a cochlear implant is appropriate]. In the meantime, [his] school should seat [him] at the front of the class, and you should face [him] when speaking."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

- *Hearing loss stigma.* Some Indian families associate hearing loss with intellectual disability. Address directly: hearing loss does not affect intelligence; with appropriate amplification and speech therapy, most children with hearing loss develop normal language.
- *Hearing-aid resistance.* Some families resist visible hearing aids due to social stigma. Modern hearing aids are small and discreet; behind-the-ear aids for children are durable and effective. Frame as enabling, not marking.
- *Cochlear-implant hesitancy.* Surgery anxiety is common. Explain that CI is a well-established procedure with excellent outcomes when done early (ideally before age 2 for bilateral severe-profound SNHL). Provide parent-testimonial resources from CI centres.
- *"He'll grow out of it."* Families may minimise hearing concerns. Quantify: untreated hearing loss in the first 3 years permanently affects language development. The JCIH 1-3-6 timeline exists because delay matters.
- *Traditional remedies for ear infections.* Some families use warm oil drops or garlic-oil drops. These are not harmful for intact TM but should not replace antibiotic treatment for AOM. For OME, no home remedy accelerates resolution.
- *Cost of hearing aids.* Hearing aids are expensive (₹15,000–₹1,50,000 per ear). Government programmes (ADIP scheme, state disability schemes) provide subsidised or free hearing aids. SKIDS manager should assist families with applications.

§11.3 Top parent objections:

| Objection | Manager response |
|---|---|
| "The hearing screen was just a refer — it's probably nothing." | "You may be right — many refers turn out to be nothing. But the repeat test tells us for sure. We don't want to miss something when catching it early makes all the difference." |
| "Ear infections are normal — why does he need a specialist?" | "One or two ear infections a year are common. [He] has had [number] in [timeframe], which puts [him] at risk for fluid buildup and hearing problems. The ENT specialist will check if a small tube in the ear would prevent future infections." |
| "Hearing aids are too expensive." | "I understand. There are government schemes that can help cover the cost. I can help you with the application. Early amplification makes a huge difference for [child's name]'s speech and learning." |
| "She hears fine at home." | "Children with mild hearing loss often cope well at home where it's quiet. The challenge is in noisy classrooms. The hearing test shows exactly how much she hears at different pitches." |

## §12 Quality and audit

§12.1 Metrics:
- 100% of newborn-refer cases have diagnostic ABR completed within 3 months (JCIH milestone).
- ≥ 95% of A1 OAE screening completed at scheduled timepoints.
- AOM antibiotic first-line (amoxicillin) adherence ≥ 90%.
- OME monitoring programme adherence: ≥ 85% of children with OME attend 3-month recheck.
- SNHL detection-to-intervention time: hearing-aid fitting or CI referral within 6 months of diagnosis for bilateral moderate+ SNHL.
- Cerumen management: 0% use of ear candles (hard quality fail); 0% aminoglycoside otic drops through perforated TM.
- ≥ 95% of §7 triggers result in documented referral with complete handoff packet.
- Loss-to-follow-up for newborn-refer: 0% target.

§12.2 Quarterly: protocol owner reviews all newborn-refer cases (JCIH compliance), all §7 referrals, AOM treatment appropriateness (watchful waiting vs. antibiotic decision audit), OME monitoring adherence, random sample of 30 B1-02 visits.

§12.3 Certification: every SKIDS pediatrician must demonstrate OAE device proficiency (SoundScout), pneumatic otoscopy technique, and AOM diagnostic criteria. Audiometry and tympanometry interpretation training (4 hours) for pediatricians; full audiometric training for SKIDS audiologist/nurse (IAP-accredited audiology course, 16 hours). Cerumen-removal practical competency. Annual update (2 hours).

§12.4 Fellowship: SKIDS does not currently have a Pediatric Otology/Audiology Fellowship pathway. Pediatricians with interest may pursue IAP Pediatric Otology and Audiology certification. Protocol governance sits with the named pediatric audiologist or ENT specialist.
