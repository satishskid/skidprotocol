# SKIDS Screening (the front door)

> A1 is the universal pediatric screening protocol that runs on every
> child arriving at a SKIDS clinic. It generates the longitudinal PHR,
> grades age-banded screening findings into a four-tier flag set, and
> routes orange and red flags to the appropriate B1 specialty clinic
> or §7 hard referral.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Establishes the longitudinal PHR for every child arriving at a SKIDS clinic, on first visit and at every subsequent periodic visit per §8.
- Performs age-band-specific screening — growth, development, vision, hearing, oral, behavioural-emotional, vaccination status, anaemia (selective), blood pressure (≥3 years), psychosocial (adolescents) — using the named scales and devices listed in §4.
- Grades each screening domain into one of four flag tiers (Green / Yellow / Orange / Red — see §5) and writes the result to the PHR.
- Routes Orange flags to the appropriate B1 specialty clinic by the end of the same visit; routes Red flags to §7 hard referral; manages Yellow flags with anticipatory guidance and a defined recheck interval.
- Delivers age-banded anticipatory guidance per AAP Bright Futures and IAP Periodicity recommendations [AAP-BF-4E, IAP-Period-2024].
- Confirms vaccination status against IAP IIS 2024-25 and refers to B1-22 Vaccination Clinic for catch-up or scheduled doses [IAP-IIS-2024].

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Treat any condition. Treatment is delivered in the relevant B1 specialty clinic to which A1 routes.
- Replace acute / sick-visit care. A child arriving febrile or in respiratory distress is triaged at the front desk per the SKIDS acute-visit workflow before A1 screening commences; A1 may be deferred or skipped until the acute episode is managed.
- Substitute for the credentialed subspecialty assessment that a §7 referral or a B2 clinic provides.

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (newborn first visit through completion of adolescent care). Age-band-specific screening batteries are defined in §4.2.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 Screening included in full. Yellow-flag anticipatory guidance and recheck included. Orange-flag routing identified but B1 specialty clinic visits charged per use.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — A1 Screening included in full. Up to four B1 specialty clinic visits per year included for any Orange-flag routing.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — A1 Screening included in full. Unlimited B1 specialty visits within the family's clinically indicated set; priority booking for any §7 referral handoff.

The subscription tier match is operational, not clinical. Every child receives the same screening battery, the same flag grading, and the same clinical routing regardless of tier; the tier determines what is included in the bundle vs. paid per visit. See §11 for Manager-side enrolment logic.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 (universal screening; the front door of the SKIDS clinical system). A1 is delivered by every SKIDS pediatrician in every SKIDS clinic from day one. Protocol governance sits with the named developmental-behavioural pediatrician identified in §2 (assignment pending at editorial pass). Complex screening findings escalate either to a B1 specialty clinic (Orange flag) or to §7 hard referral (Red flag); A1 itself does not have a "complex tier" — its complex tier is the receiving B1 protocol.

## §2 Evidence base

This protocol governs universal pediatric screening at SKIDS clinics. It follows the most current recommendations from:

- **[AAP-BF-4E]** — Hagan JF, Shaw JS, Duncan PM, eds. *Bright Futures: Guidelines for Health Supervision of Infants, Children, and Adolescents*, 4th edition. American Academy of Pediatrics, 2017. Governing source for the well-child visit content, age-banded anticipatory guidance, and the periodicity schedule.
- **[AAP-Period-2024]** — AAP Periodicity Schedule, 2024 update. The visit-by-visit checklist of recommended screening at each well-child visit.
- **[IAP-Period-2024]** — Indian Academy of Pediatrics. *IAP Growth, Development and Behavioural Surveillance Recommendations*, 2024. The Indian-context periodicity schedule, used in preference to AAP where the two diverge (see departures below).
- **[IAP-IIS-2024]** — IAP Immunisation Schedule 2024-25. Governing source for vaccination status assessment in §4.
- **[WHO-2024-Growth]** — WHO Child Growth Standards (0–5 years) and IAP 2015 growth charts (5–18 years). Governing sources for plotting and interpreting growth.
- **[AAP-2017-HBP]** — AAP Clinical Practice Guideline for Screening and Management of High Blood Pressure in Children and Adolescents, 2017. Governing source for BP screening from age 3.
- **[WHO-2011-Anaemia]** — WHO Haemoglobin concentrations for the diagnosis of anaemia. Governing source for selective anaemia screening thresholds.
- **[AAP-2019-ADHD]** — AAP ADHD Clinical Practice Guideline, 2019. Cited in §4 for behavioural screening cutoffs in school-age children.
- **[CDC-Milestones-2022]** — CDC Developmental Milestones (revised 2022 with AAP). Used alongside ASQ-3 and Trivandrum DST as the developmental surveillance reference set.
- **[NELSON-21:CH12]** — Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics, 21st ed., Chapter 12 (Health Supervision). Reference textbook for the well-child framework.
- **[IAP-TB-6E:CH4]** — IAP Textbook of Pediatrics, 6th ed., Chapter on health supervision and growth surveillance.
- **[RBSK-2013]** — Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram, Government of India, 2013 (with subsequent updates). The Indian school-health reference for the 4D screening framework (Defects at birth, Diseases, Deficiencies, Developmental delays); A1 incorporates the RBSK domains within the Bright Futures framework.

**Departures from cited guidelines:**

1. *Periodicity schedule.* AAP Bright Futures recommends 13 visits in the first 24 months. IAP recommends 9. A1 follows the IAP visit count, with the Bright Futures content domains delivered across the IAP visit cadence. Rationale: Indian families' attendance pattern and out-of-pocket cost structure make the AAP cadence operationally infeasible without harming completion rates [IAP-Period-2024].

2. *BP screening start age.* AAP 2017 recommends BP screening from age 3 at every well-visit. IAP recommends from age 3 with risk-based intensification. A1 follows AAP — universal BP at every well-visit from age 3, not risk-based — because the additional clinic time is small and the cost of missed pediatric hypertension is high in an Indian-population context with rising obesity rates [AAP-2017-HBP].

3. *Anaemia screening.* AAP recommends universal screening at 12 months. IAP recommends universal screening at 9–12 months and at 5 years given high Indian prevalence. A1 follows IAP (more screening, not less) [IAP-Period-2024, WHO-2011-Anaemia].

4. *Adolescent psychosocial screening.* AAP Bright Futures recommends HEEADSSS at every adolescent visit. A1 follows AAP, with cultural adaptation of the Sexuality and Drugs domains per IAP Adolescent Health Academy guidance — see B1-13 Adolescent Clinic for the adapted interview protocol [AAP-BF-4E].

No other material departures.

## §3 Presenting concerns

A1 differs from a B1 specialty protocol: most children arriving at A1 are not "presenting" with a clinical concern. They are arriving for routine periodic screening or for their first SKIDS visit. The "presenting concern" in A1 terms is *the reason this child is in front of me today*, not *the symptom that brought them*.

A child arrives at SKIDS A1 Screening via one of these paths:

| Parent language | Clinical framing | Routing |
|---|---|---|
| "I want to enrol my child at SKIDS for the first time." | First-visit baseline screening; PHR creation. | A1 full age-band battery + subscription enrolment via §11. |
| "We're due for our regular check-up." | Scheduled periodic well-visit per §8. | A1 age-band battery (delta-only re-screen for items already captured at last visit if interval <6 months). |
| "School needs a fitness certificate / medical form." | School-entry or sports-clearance screen. | A1 age-band battery + B1-16 Cardiac sports-clearance addendum if competitive sports. |
| "She just finished a course of antibiotics for [infection]; can you check she's fine now?" | Post-acute recheck. | A1 mini-battery: weight, hydration, exam relevant to recent illness. Full A1 only if next periodic visit due. |
| "He's not growing like his brother did." | Growth concern. | A1 + Orange flag routing to B1-09 Nutrition & Growth if growth criteria in §5.1.2 met. |
| "She's behind on her vaccines." | Vaccination catch-up request. | A1 vaccination domain + Orange flag routing to B1-22 Vaccination. |
| "He doesn't seem to be talking like other kids his age." | Speech/developmental concern. | A1 + ASQ-3 / REELS as indicated; route Orange to B1-11 Speech & Language and/or B1-23 Developmental Surveillance. |
| "Her teacher says she can't see the board." | Vision concern. | A1 + Welch Allyn Spot Vision; route Orange to B1-01 Vision. |
| "He's so fussy at night, never sleeps through." | Sleep concern. | A1 + BEARS; route Orange to B1-07 Sleep if BEARS positive. |
| "She seems sad and withdrawn lately." | Behavioural-emotional concern. | A1 + age-appropriate scale (CBCL / SDQ / PHQ-A); route Orange to B1-08 Behavioural; if PHQ-A item 9 (suicidality) positive, §7.1 emergency. |
| "I read about [condition] online and want him checked." | Parent-driven health-anxiety presentation. | A1 + targeted history/exam for the named concern; reassure with documented findings if Green; route Orange/Red per protocol if flag-positive. |
| "The school nurse said his BP was high last week." | External-referrer flag. | A1 + repeat BP per AAP three-reading rule; route Orange to B1-17 Kidney & Urology if confirmed elevated [AAP-2017-HBP]. |
| "Our pediatrician retired; we need a new one." | Care-transition first-visit. | Same as enrolment first-visit; reconcile records from prior provider into PHR. |
| "Government health camp told us to come" (RBSK referral). | School-screening positive flag; needs definitive screening. | A1 with explicit attention to the RBSK-flagged domain; document in PHR; route per §5. |

**Routing decisions:**
- If a child arrives with a fully formed B1 presenting concern (e.g., asthma exacerbation, eczema flare, suspected ADHD diagnosis), A1 is performed in compressed form (growth + vaccination + delta-update of PHR) and the consult moves directly to the relevant B1 protocol. The child does not have to "complete A1" before being seen for the symptom that brought them.
- If a child arrives acutely unwell — fever in infant <3 months, respiratory distress, suspected sepsis, suspected non-accidental injury — the front-desk acute-visit workflow takes precedence; A1 is deferred. See §7.1.
- If a child arrives for their scheduled periodic visit and screening is clean (all Green), the consult ends with anticipatory guidance per §6.1 and a follow-up date per §8. No B1 routing required.

**Coming from elsewhere — automatic upstream flags:**
- *Government RBSK school-screening referral:* the RBSK card identifies the flagged domain (often vision, hearing, or growth). The Companion intake screen pre-tags the corresponding A1 domain for explicit re-screening with the SKIDS device set.
- *Pediatrician handover from another clinic:* prior records are reconciled into the PHR by the SKIDS pediatrician at the first visit; gaps in periodicity are filled by a delta-screen at this visit and re-baselined per §8.
- *Parent-uploaded prior records (via the parent app):* the Companion ingests prior growth charts, vaccination cards, and discharge summaries; A1 confirms the most recent status and updates the PHR.

A1 does not have its own §3 *symptom-driven* presentation set in the same way a B1 protocol does — its presenting set is a routing matrix into the rest of the library. The B1 protocols carry the symptom-specific presentations; A1's job is to get the child to the right one.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

A1's diagnostic workflow is an **age-band screening battery** rather than a single diagnostic pathway. The battery domains are constant across age bands; the specific tools, scales, and thresholds change with age.

### 4.1 Structured history

Ask, in this order, at every A1 visit:

1. *Interval history since last visit.* Any hospitalisations, ER visits, courses of antibiotics, new medications, accidents/injuries, or new chronic diagnoses.
2. *Current symptoms.* Open-ended "anything you've been worried about with [child]?" — capture parent's exact words for the PHR.
3. *Feeding / nutrition.* Age-appropriate detail: breastfeeding status (0–24m), introduction of solids (4–6m), 24-hour diet recall (≥24m), eating concerns (any age).
4. *Sleep.* Total hours, awakenings, snoring, sleep environment. Use BEARS (Bedtime, Excessive daytime sleepiness, Awakenings, Regularity, Snoring) for ages 2–18 [Owens-2005-BEARS].
5. *Elimination.* Bowel pattern (per Rome IV functional constipation criteria for ≥4 years), urinary continence (≥5 years for daytime, ≥7 years for nighttime — see B1-20 Bedwetting).
6. *Development / school.* Age-appropriate developmental concerns; for school-age, academic performance, attention, peer interactions; for adolescents, HEEADSSS.
7. *Vaccination status.* Verbal review against the IAP IIS card; reconcile with Companion record [IAP-IIS-2024].
8. *Family history.* Update on any new pediatric-relevant family conditions (atopy, T1DM, T2DM, thalassemia trait, congenital heart disease, hereditary deafness, intellectual disability).
9. *Social / environmental.* Caregiver structure; school/childcare; significant life events (parental separation, bereavement, migration); for adolescents, the full HEEADSSS interview [AAP-BF-4E].
10. *Parental concerns the protocol has not yet asked about.* Always end with "is there anything else you wanted me to look at today?"

**Red flags to elicit at history (any age):**
- Witnessed seizure-like activity.
- Loss of consciousness or syncope.
- Acute weight loss or anorexia.
- Bruising in unusual distribution (back, ears, neck, genitals); injury inconsistent with developmental ability.
- Caregiver reports of self-harm thoughts (any age) or suicidal ideation (school-age and above).
- Sexual-abuse disclosures (direct or indirect).

Used at the indicated bands:

| Scale | Domain | Age | Administered by | Cutoff for Yellow flag | Cutoff for Orange flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **WHO Growth chart Z-score** | Growth | 0–5y | Nurse (measurement) + Companion (auto-plot) | weight or height between −1 and −2 SD | < −2 SD or > +2 SD; or crossing two major centiles downward [WHO-2024-Growth] |

| **GAD-7** | Anxiety | 12–18y | Adolescent self-completed (Companion tablet, private) | score 5–9 | score ≥10 |
| **CRAFFT** | Substance use | 12–18y | Adolescent self-completed (Companion tablet, private) | score 1 | score ≥2 |
| **HEEADSSS** | Adolescent psychosocial | 12–18y | Doctor (structured private interview) | one risk domain | two or more, or any disclosure of abuse, exploitation, or self-harm |

Required device-based screenings per band:

| Device | Domain | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Welch Allyn Spot Vision Screener** | Vision (refractive error, strabismus, anisocoria) | Every well-visit from 12 months [AAP-BF-4E] |
| **SoundScout** (or equivalent OAE / age-appropriate audiometry) | Hearing | Newborn (universal newborn hearing screen), 9m, 18m, 4y, 8y, 12y, 16y |
| **Pulse oximetry** | Oxygen saturation | Newborn (24-hour CCHD screen), and any visit with respiratory symptoms |
| **Digital BP cuff** (appropriate paediatric size) | Blood pressure | Every visit from age 3 |
| **MUAC tape** | Acute malnutrition | Every visit 6m–5y |
| **Otoscope (pneumatic)** | Tympanic membrane | Every visit; targeted use if hearing concern or recent AOM |
| **Stadiometer + infantometer + electronic scale** | Growth | Every visit |

Optional / on-indication:
- **AyuSynk digital stethoscope with PCG capture** — when a murmur is heard on auscultation, to capture and grade for B1-16 Cardiac referral decision.
- **Glucometer** — point-of-care capillary glucose if symptoms of hyperglycaemia or unexplained fatigue/polyuria; positive result triggers §7.2 (urgent endocrine referral / B2-01 if available).
- **POC haemoglobin (HemoCue or equivalent)** — at the indicated anaemia screening visits (9–12m, 5y, and adolescent female annually) [WHO-2011-Anaemia, IAP-Period-2024].

### 4.5 Laboratory and imaging

A1 is a screening protocol; routine bloodwork beyond the device-based POC tests is selective.

Order:
- **POC Hb** at 9–12 months, age 5, and every adolescent female visit. If Hb < age-specific WHO threshold, route Orange to B1-18 Blood [WHO-2011-Anaemia].
- **Urinalysis (dipstick)** at age 5 once and at any visit with UTI history or BP elevation [AAP-2017-HBP].
- **Lipid screen** (non-fasting non-HDL acceptable) once between ages 9–11 and once between 17–21 per AAP universal screen, OR risk-based per IAP — A1 follows AAP universal once-per-decade [AAP-BF-4E].
- **TSH** if growth flag-positive (height < −2 SD or crossing centiles downward), pubertal flag-positive, or symptoms suggestive [IAP-TB-6E:CH4].
- **Newborn screening (TSH, 17-OHP, G6PD, haemoglobinopathy panel)** — confirm completion at the first SKIDS visit if newborn; if not done, order per the SKIDS clinic newborn-screen panel.

Do not order routinely:
- Full CBC at every well-visit. Replace with POC Hb at the indicated visits; full CBC only if Hb low or other indication.
- Routine urine microscopy. Dipstick suffices for screening.
- Routine vitamin D level. IAP recommends supplementation rather than screening for the general pediatric population [IAP-Period-2024]; B1-09 Nutrition manages the dosing.
- Imaging of any kind without a specific indication. A1 does not order routine imaging.

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

A1's "severity grading" is **flag tiering** — a four-tier system that grades each screening domain independently and determines the clinical action for that domain.

This is not a single severity scale because A1 covers many domains simultaneously. The four tiers are uniform across domains; the tier-defining thresholds per domain are listed in §4.3 and §4.4.

### 5.1 The four flag tiers

| Flag | Meaning | Intervention tier (§6) | Subscription tier match (§11) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **🟢 Green** | Domain screened, no concern. Within normative reference range for age. | §6.1 Anticipatory guidance only | Basic ₹2,999 fully covers this child's needs in this domain |
| **🟡 Yellow** | Mild deviation or borderline finding. Safe to monitor with parent education and a defined recheck interval. | §6.2 Standard A1 — anticipatory guidance + recheck at 3–6 months, in-A1 (not B1) | Basic ₹2,999 covers; Advanced ₹6,999 if multiple Yellow domains or recurring concern |
| **🟠 Orange** | Significant finding requiring B1 specialty clinic evaluation within a defined window. Not life-threatening; not emergent. | §6.3 Intensive A1 — B1 routing within next 4–12 weeks per the named B1 protocol's intake window | Advanced ₹6,999 (single Orange domain); Premium ₹12,999 if ≥2 Orange domains or expected long-running B1 management |
| **🔴 Red** | §7 hard referral fires. Emergency, urgent, or routine — see §7. | Out of A1 scope | Premium ₹12,999 covers priority handoff; tier match is operational, not gating — Red routing is delivered regardless of subscription |

### 5.2 Threshold sources by domain

| Domain | Tier source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Growth | WHO 0–5y, IAP 5–18y | See §4.3 row 1–2; thresholds are SD/centile based [WHO-2024-Growth, IAP-Period-2024] |
| Development | Trivandrum DST + ASQ-3 + M-CHAT-R | See §4.3 [AAP-BF-4E, CDC-Milestones-2022] |
| Vision | Welch Allyn Spot device output | Manufacturer cutoffs map to Green / Refer (Orange) directly; any "refer" output → Orange to B1-01 |
| Hearing | OAE / SoundScout output | Pass = Green; refer = Orange to B1-02 (newborn refer = Red within 1 month if confirmed) |
| Behavioural-emotional | SDQ, Vanderbilt, CBCL, M-CHAT-R | Per §4.3 cutoffs [AAP-2019-ADHD, AAP-BF-4E] |
| Sleep | BEARS | Per §4.3 cutoffs |
| Adolescent psychosocial | HEEADSSS + PHQ-A + GAD-7 + CRAFFT | Per §4.3 cutoffs; **PHQ-A item 9 positivity = automatic §7.1 Red regardless of total score** [AAP-BF-4E] |
| Anaemia | POC Hb vs. WHO age-specific cutoff | < 7.0 g/dL = §7.2 Red urgent; 7.0–WHO cutoff = Orange [WHO-2011-Anaemia] |
| BP | AAP 2017 percentile tables | 90th–<95th = Yellow, ≥95th confirmed = Orange, ≥99th + 5 mmHg or symptoms = §7.2 Red [AAP-2017-HBP] |
| Acute malnutrition | MUAC | 12.5–13.5 cm = Yellow, 11.5–12.5 cm = Orange to B1-09 (MAM), < 11.5 cm = §7.1 Red (SAM emergency) [WHO-2024-Growth] |
| Vaccination | IAP IIS reconciliation | Up-to-date = Green, due-now = Orange to B1-22, severely overdue or measles-outbreak-context catch-up = §7.3 Red routine same-week B1-22 |
| Oral health | Visual caries inspection + AAPD risk score | Visible caries Yellow if early enamel only; Orange to B1-04 if cavitation; Red §7.3 if facial cellulitis or dental abscess |

### 5.3 Boundary rules

- **Borderline Green / Yellow:** when a domain's measurement is within reference range but at the edge (e.g., weight at 25th centile in a child with both parents at 10th centile), default to Green and document the family-context rationale in the PHR. Do not soft-Yellow children with normal-for-context findings.
- **Borderline Yellow / Orange:** when a measurement straddles the threshold (e.g., a Vanderbilt with 5 inattentive items meeting partial but not full criteria), default to Yellow plus a specific recheck at 3 months. Record the specific items elevated so the recheck has a comparator.
- **Re-grading after Yellow:** at the recheck visit, re-administer the same scale or repeat the same measurement under standardised conditions. If unchanged or improved → Green. If worse → Orange (B1 routing). Do not leave a domain in Yellow indefinitely; resolve to Green or Orange by the second recheck.
- **Multiple Yellow flags:** three or more concurrent Yellow flags in the same child trigger an automatic step-up to Advanced subscription enrolment recommendation in §11 and a single coordinated B1 review (most often B1-09 Nutrition & Growth or B1-23 Developmental Surveillance, depending on which domains).
- **Severity progression triggers:** any domain that progresses from Green at one visit to Orange at the next (no Yellow intermediate) is logged as a "fast-progression flag" in the PHR and reviewed at the next §12 quarterly case review.
- **§5 to §7 boundary:** the Red column of §5.2 is the same set of triggers enumerated in §7. They are listed in both places deliberately — §5 from the screening-tiering side, §7 from the medico-legal-handoff side.

## §6 Therapeutic options

A1 does not deliver therapy in the conventional sense — therapy lives in the B1 protocols. A1's "therapeutic options" are the **interventions A1 itself delivers**: anticipatory guidance, vaccination administration, parent-education content activation, and routing to the B1 specialty clinic.

The intervention tiers below correspond to flag tiers in §5.

### 6.1 Light tier (all-Green visit)

**Pharmacological:**
- Routine vaccination per IAP IIS schedule for this visit, administered in-clinic [IAP-IIS-2024].
- Vitamin D supplementation per IAP guidance: 400 IU/day for 0–12m breastfed/partially breastfed; 600 IU/day for 1–18 years if dietary intake is inadequate (most Indian children) [IAP-Period-2024]. Dispensed from in-clinic stock at Basic+ subscription tiers.
- Iron supplementation per IAP guidance: 1 mg/kg/day elemental iron prophylactically from 6m–5y (with parental discretion below 12m if exclusive breastfeeding without iron-fortified weaning). Adolescent menstruating females: 60 mg elemental iron weekly (IFA programme) for prophylaxis; therapeutic dosing (3–6 mg/kg/day, max 200 mg/day) only for confirmed IDA per B1-09 [IAP-IDA-2021, IAP-Period-2024].

**Behavioural / Lifestyle anticipatory guidance** (delivered verbally, with parent app reinforcement per §10.4):
- Age-appropriate sleep duration and sleep hygiene targets.
- Age-appropriate screen-time limits (AAP/IAP joint position: no screens <18m other than video-calling; <1 hr/day high-quality co-viewed for 2–5y; consistent limits with content/timing rules for 6+) [AAP-BF-4E, IAP-Period-2024].
- Age-appropriate physical activity target (≥60 minutes moderate-vigorous per day for ≥5y).
- Age-appropriate feeding guidance (exclusive breastfeeding 0–6m, complementary feeding 6m+, family-meal practices, balanced diet for older children).
- Safety counselling — car-seat use, safe-sleep practices for infants, water safety, helmet use, age-appropriate poison/fire/electric-socket precautions, online safety for adolescents.

**Parent-applied:**
- Activate the age-banded HABITS / Practice content on parent.skids.clinic for this child (see §10.4).

**Allied staff:**
- Allied staff are not routinely involved in a Green A1 visit.

**Dispensary stock used:**
- Vaccine vials (per IAP IIS schedule for this visit's age).
- Vitamin D drops / tablets, age-appropriate strength.
- Iron syrup / tablets, age-appropriate strength.

**Expected timeline to next contact:** per §8 periodicity.

### 6.2 Standard tier (Yellow flag in any domain)

In addition to all of §6.1:

**Behavioural / Lifestyle:**
- Domain-specific anticipatory guidance is enriched. For example, a Yellow growth flag triggers a structured 24-hour diet recall, family-meal observation prompts, and a feeding-environment review the parent completes through the parent app before the recheck visit. A Yellow sleep flag triggers a sleep-diary the parent completes for 14 days before the recheck.

**Parent-applied:**
- Domain-specific HABITS / Practice modules activated on parent.skids.clinic — e.g., the "Toddler Sleep Foundations" 14-day programme for Yellow sleep, or the "Picky Eater Reset" 30-day programme for Yellow growth-around-feeding.

**Recheck:**
- Recheck visit scheduled at 3 months for most Yellow domains; 6 months for Yellow growth that is at-the-edge with strong family context. The recheck re-administers the specific scale or measurement that was Yellow.

**Expected timeline to resolution:** by the second recheck (≤ 6 months from initial Yellow) the domain should be either Green or Orange. Persistent Yellow without progression to Green is itself an Orange-equivalent and routes to the relevant B1 for evaluation.

### 6.4 Contraindications and precautions

A1's interventions are anticipatory guidance, vaccination, and routine supplementation. Contraindications:

| Therapeutic | Contraindication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Routine vaccine (any) | Acute moderate-severe illness with fever; previous anaphylaxis to that vaccine | Defer dose; re-attempt at next visit; for true anaphylaxis substitute alternative vaccine where one exists per IAP IIS, otherwise §7.3 routine to B1-22 [IAP-IIS-2024] |
| Iron supplementation | Confirmed thalassemia major or known iron overload | Withhold; route to B1-18 Blood for haematology decision |
| Vitamin D supplementation | Documented hypercalcaemia or known granulomatous disease | Withhold; continue at next visit if cleared |
| Anticipatory guidance on infant safe-sleep | Cultural co-sleeping practice | Discuss safe co-sleeping practices (firm surface, no soft bedding, no impaired-caregiver bed-share); do not mandate non-co-sleeping; document the discussion |
| Adolescent contraception counselling | Family or cultural sensitivity | Conduct interview privately with adolescent per HEEADSSS protocol; never disclose findings to parent without adolescent consent unless §7.1 trigger fires; refer to B1-13 Adolescent for any contraception delivery |

### 6.5 Tapering and stopping

A1 is not a treatment protocol; there is no tapering. Routine prophylactic supplementation (iron, vitamin D) continues per the IAP age-band schedule in §6.1 and is reviewed at every A1 visit.

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

A1's §7 captures the screening findings that take a child out of the A1 + B1 catalogue altogether — to ER, to a hospital, to a Tier 3 partner, or to immediate B2 subspecialty care. These are the **Red flags** of the A1 grading system.

The case escalates out of A1's scope when ANY of the following is true:

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day, transport directly to ER)

- [ ] **Suspected non-accidental injury (NAI) at any age.** Bruising in non-mobile infants, bruising in protected sites (back, ears, neck, genitals, frenulum), torus or metaphyseal fractures on incidental imaging, injury inconsistent with developmental ability, or caregiver disclosure suggestive. → Named partner pediatric ER + child-protection referral within the same hour. SKIDS pediatrician does not discharge the child to the suspected-perpetrator caregiver; clinic protocol is to retain the child within the clinic environment until ER transfer is effected. [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm SKIDS clinic legal/operational protocol for child-protection retention with Dr. Rath]
- [ ] **Active suicidal ideation (PHQ-A item 9 positive, or direct disclosure during HEEADSSS).** → Named partner pediatric ER with mental-health capacity, with LISSUN consultant pre-notified, within the same day. Adolescent does not leave SKIDS clinic unaccompanied; clinic protocol mandates adult caregiver hand-off or direct ambulance transfer to the named ER.
- [ ] **Severe acute malnutrition (MUAC < 11.5 cm OR weight-for-height < −3 SD with bilateral pitting oedema).** → Named partner pediatric inpatient unit / WHO-protocol SAM ward within 4 hours [WHO-2024-Growth].
- [ ] **Severe acute respiratory distress** at the screening visit (RR > age-percentile severe threshold, retractions, grunting, SpO₂ < 92% on room air at sea level). → Named partner pediatric ER within 1 hour. A1 is paused, acute-visit workflow takes over.
- [ ] **Newborn with concerning vital signs** at the first SKIDS visit (temperature instability, jaundice in first 24 hours, lethargy, poor feeding with weight loss > 10% birth weight). → Named partner neonatal unit within 4 hours.
- [ ] **Acute neurological event** witnessed or strongly suspected (active seizure, sustained altered consciousness, focal weakness of new onset, signs of raised ICP). → Named partner pediatric ER within 1 hour.
- [ ] **Confirmed Hb < 5 g/dL** or signs of haemodynamic compromise at any Hb level (tachycardia at rest disproportionate to age, pallor with capillary refill > 3 seconds). → Named partner pediatric inpatient unit with transfusion capacity within 4 hours [WHO-2011-Anaemia].
- [ ] **Suspected meningitis or sepsis** (any age) — fever with petechial rash, fever with altered consciousness, neck stiffness in a child old enough to demonstrate it. → Named partner pediatric ER within 1 hour.
- [ ] **Suspected sexual abuse** disclosed at A1, regardless of timing. → Named partner pediatric ER + child-protection referral within the same hour. Same retention protocol as NAI above.
- [ ] **Anaphylaxis history without an action plan and adrenaline auto-injector**, presenting in a child whose dietary or environmental risk has not been assessed. → Same-day routing to B1-06 Allergy is not sufficient if the family has had a recent anaphylaxis event. Send to named partner pediatric allergy specialist or pediatric ER for action-plan + adrenaline immediately.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Confirmed BP ≥99th percentile + 5 mmHg** for age/sex/height on three readings, with or without symptoms. → Named partner pediatric nephrology or pediatric cardiology + same-day B1-17 routing as a bridge [AAP-2017-HBP].
- [ ] **Hb 5–7 g/dL** with no haemodynamic compromise. → Named partner pediatric haematology + same-week B1-18 Blood [WHO-2011-Anaemia].
- [ ] **POC capillary glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL** OR symptoms suggestive of new-onset diabetes (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss). → Named partner pediatric endocrinologist (or B2-01 Endocrine if available at this clinic) within 7 days; intermediate routing to nearest pediatric ER if any signs of DKA (Kussmaul breathing, dehydration, vomiting).
- [ ] **Newborn confirmed-refer on hearing screen.** → Named partner pediatric audiology / ENT within 4 weeks per JCIH 1-3-6 (screen by 1m, diagnose by 3m, intervene by 6m); urgent within 7 days at A1 because the SKIDS clinic confirms the screen result and books the diagnostic audiology appointment.
- [ ] **Suspected pediatric malignancy** — unexplained persistent lymphadenopathy >2 cm with B-symptoms, abdominal mass on examination, unexplained pallor + bruising + bone pain, white pupil reflex (leukocoria). → Named partner pediatric oncology + same-week B1-18 Blood for haematological workup as a bridge.
- [ ] **Neurological developmental regression** — loss of previously-attained milestones at any age. → Named partner pediatric neurologist (or B2-02 Neurology if available) + same-week B1-23 Developmental Surveillance as a bridge.
- [ ] **Any HEEADSSS disclosure of ongoing abuse, exploitation, or trafficking** (not currently emergent but ongoing risk). → Named child-protection authority + named pediatric mental-health partner + LISSUN within 7 days; ongoing safety planning with adolescent during this window.

### 7.3 Routine referral (next available appointment, ≤ 4 weeks)

A1's "routine" §7 referrals are findings that exceed the B1 catalogue's scope but are not time-sensitive within days.

- [ ] **Suspected complex congenital syndrome** (e.g., a previously-undiagnosed Down phenotype, suspected Turner, suspected Marfan habitus). → Named partner clinical genetics + pediatric subspecialty as appropriate within 4 weeks.
- [ ] **Suspected major congenital heart disease** in a child not previously diagnosed (significant murmur >grade 3, signs of heart failure, cyanosis with exertion). → Named partner pediatric cardiology + B1-16 as a bridge, within 4 weeks.
- [ ] **Suspected hereditary hematological condition** that B1-18 Blood cannot manage at primary tier (sickle cell disease with active complications, transfusion-dependent thalassemia major). → Named partner pediatric haematology centre within 4 weeks.
- [ ] **Suspected primary immunodeficiency** beyond the recurrent-infections workup that B1-19 manages (≥ 2 serious infections in lifetime, recurrent deep-tissue infections, family history of PID). → Named partner pediatric immunology + B1-19 as a bridge, within 4 weeks.
- [ ] **Confirmed pediatric pulmonary tuberculosis** identified during A1 (clinical features + positive TB screen + radiological evidence, where SKIDS has done the workup). → Named NTEP-registered DOTS centre within 1 week of diagnosis [NTEP-2020]. (Note: this is "routine" in §7 terms but is operationally the most urgent of the routine triggers.)

### 7.4 Handoff communication

When A1 fires a §7 referral, the SKIDS pediatrician must send the receiving clinician (auto-generated by Companion):

1. **Child identification:** name, date of birth, child_id (PHR identifier), parent contact details, parent consent confirmation, current medications, known allergies.
2. **The trigger:** which §7 sub-trigger fired, with the underlying finding (e.g., "PHQ-A item 9 score = 2; total score = 14" or "BP three-reading mean = 142/91, child age 12y, height 50th centile, ≥99th + 5 mmHg per AAP-2017 tables").
3. **Clinical context:** relevant history, examination findings, prior A1 visit summaries (last 2–3 if available), all concurrent flags from this visit.
4. **Investigations done at SKIDS:** any POC results from this visit (Hb, glucose, BP, oximetry, urinalysis), any imaging or labs ordered.
5. **Bridge interventions started:** for example, if iron has been initiated and the child is being referred for a haematology workup; or if anticipatory guidance has been given pending the urgent appointment.
6. **The reason for referral, in clinical terms:** one to two sentences from the SKIDS pediatrician on what they need from the receiving clinician (e.g., "evaluation for new-onset hypertension; prior-secondary-cause workup including renal USG and aldosterone:renin ratio not yet done").
7. **Urgency tier and target window:** §7.1 / §7.2 / §7.3 with the time bound.
8. **The named partner / clinic this is being sent to** and the appointment booking status (confirmed time, awaiting confirmation, walk-in, ambulance-transfer).

This packet is auto-generated by Companion when the §7 trigger fires. The SKIDS pediatrician reviews and signs off before transmission. For §7.1 triggers, the receiving clinician is also called by phone by the SKIDS pediatrician — the Companion-generated packet is a record, not a substitute for the verbal handoff.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

A1's follow-up schedule is the **periodicity schedule** — when the child's next routine A1 visit is. It is overlaid by recheck visits for Yellow flags and by B1 visits for Orange flags.

### 8.1 Periodicity

A1 follows the IAP visit cadence (with AAP Bright Futures content) [IAP-Period-2024, AAP-BF-4E]:

| Age band | A1 periodic visits |
|---|---|
| Newborn | 3–5 days, 2 weeks, 1 month |
| Infant | 2 m, 4 m, 6 m, 9 m, 12 m |
| Toddler | 15 m, 18 m, 24 m, 30 m, 36 m |
| Preschool | 4 y, 5 y, 6 y |
| School-age | 7 y, 8 y, 10 y, 12 y |
| Adolescent | 13 y, 15 y, 17 y |

This is 21 routine A1 visits over 18 years, against AAP's 33 — the difference is concentrated in infancy where IAP cadence is sparser. Yellow-flag rechecks and Orange-flag B1 visits add to this baseline.

### 8.2 What to recheck at each follow-up

At every A1 periodic visit, regardless of band:

- All previously Yellow domains — re-administer the same scale or measurement, document delta, regrade.
- All previously Orange domains — confirm the B1 visit happened, retrieve the B1 plan, document A1's surveillance role going forward.
- All previously Red triggers — confirm the §7 outcome, document longitudinal status in the PHR.
- Growth — plot every visit; assess trajectory (centile crossing) not just the current value.
- Vaccination status — reconcile against IAP IIS for this age.
- Anticipatory guidance refresh per age band [AAP-BF-4E].

### 8.3 Treatment success criteria

A1 is screening, not treatment, so "success" is operational rather than clinical:

- The child is up-to-date on the IAP IIS schedule.
- The child is up-to-date on the periodicity schedule (no missed periodic visit > 6 months overdue).
- All Yellow flags have either resolved to Green or progressed to Orange (no domains stuck in Yellow indefinitely).
- All Orange flags have a confirmed B1 visit or an active B1 management plan.
- All Red triggers have a documented §7 outcome.
- The PHR is complete for the age — every domain has been screened in the last periodicity-defined window.

A SKIDS clinic operating well has ≥85% of its enrolled children meeting all six criteria at any audit point [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm 85% target with operational data once available].

### 8.4 Step-down criteria

A1 does not "step down" in the usual sense — it is universal and continuous through to age 18. What changes within A1 over time is:

- Yellow → Green resolution: see §6.2.
- Resolved Orange (B1 management complete, child returned to surveillance only): the domain returns to A1's scope at standard periodicity; the B1 protocol's discharge note documents the surveillance schedule.

### 8.5 Step-up criteria (within A1)

- A new Yellow flag at a periodic visit where the domain was previously Green: the standard recheck schedule starts.
- A new Orange flag: B1 routing per §6.3.
- A new Red flag: §7 referral.

### 8.6 Step-out criteria

Beyond A1 / B1 escalation is captured entirely in §7. A1 does not have an additional step-out path.

### 8.7 Disenrolment / loss-to-follow-up

If a child enrolled at SKIDS has not attended an A1 visit for > 12 months and has any active Yellow/Orange/Red flag in their PHR:

- 30 days post-due: WhatsApp reminder from Manager Console (auto-generated).
- 60 days post-due: phone call from clinic Manager.
- 90 days post-due: phone call from named pediatrician with documented attempt logged in PHR.
- > 6 months post-due with active Orange or Red: the clinic Manager flags as "loss-to-follow-up" and the named protocol owner reviews at the next §12 quarterly case review.

### 9.6 Edge cases and overrides

**Doctor disagrees with auto-calculated flag tier.**
Doctor uses the override on Screen 5 with a mandatory free-text reason. The override is logged with `ped_id` and timestamp. All overrides are surfaced for §12 quarterly review. An override that downgrades an Orange to Green or Yellow is flagged as a "downgrade override" and reviewed individually by the protocol owner.

**A scale or device cannot be administered.**
Doctor marks the domain as "unable to assess" with one of the structured reasons (child non-cooperative, device unavailable, time pressure). The domain flag tier is recorded as "Yellow — unable to assess" with a mandatory recheck at the next visit (≤ 3 months). Persistent inability across two visits triggers a §12 audit-flag.

**A §7 trigger fires mid-A1.**
Screen 7 takes priority. The standard flow pauses. The doctor cannot complete the A1 visit (and cannot send the family home) until the §7 packet is signed off and the receiving clinician is contacted (verbal for §7.1).

**Network connectivity is lost.**
Doctor Module supports offline A1 with local-only data. Growth charts, scale calculations, and the §5 flag tiering are computed locally from cached reference tables. On reconnection, data syncs to PHR, alerts fire, dispensary orders queue, and B1 / §7 referrals route. If a §7.1 emergency trigger fires offline, the Doctor Module displays a hard-stop instructing the doctor to phone the named partner immediately and confirm transfer arrangements before discharging the family from clinic.

**A1 visit deferred mid-visit due to acute symptoms.**
If during A1 the child develops or reveals an acute condition (febrile distress, suspected sepsis), the doctor uses the "defer A1; switch to acute-visit workflow" action. The partial A1 data is saved as `visit_status = "A1_deferred_partial"` and resumed at the next visit. Acute care is delivered per the SKIDS acute-visit workflow.

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

A1 is administered to families across the SKIDS catchment, including families from a wide range of socioeconomic, religious, and regional contexts. Specific points the SKIDS pediatrician handles deliberately:

- **Adolescent privacy and confidentiality.** HEEADSSS and any §4.1 history items related to sexual activity, substance use, or mental health are conducted privately with the adolescent. Confidentiality limits are explained at the start of the private interview: information is kept private *unless* the adolescent reports active intent to harm self, intent to harm others, or being abused / exploited. This is non-negotiable; the script is identical across families. The Companion logs the confidentiality discussion was held.
- **Mental-health stigma.** A Yellow / Orange flag in the behavioural-emotional domain is communicated using clinical language ("we want to look at this more carefully with our behavioural clinic") rather than diagnostic labels ("depression," "anxiety") at the screening visit. Diagnostic labelling, where appropriate, happens in B1-08 Behavioural after a structured assessment. This is to reduce premature labelling and the stigma that can foreclose future engagement.
- **Co-sleeping practices.** Indian families commonly co-sleep with infants and toddlers; the safe-sleep counselling is framed around safe co-sleeping practices (firm surface, no soft bedding, no alcohol/sedative-impaired caregiver bed-share, no smoking caregiver) rather than mandating non-co-sleeping. This deviates from the AAP "back to sleep, alone, in a crib" framing in language but not in safety substance.
- **Vaccination hesitancy.** A1 documents vaccination status as a clinical fact and offers schedule-aligned doses; vaccine hesitancy is engaged with via the IAP "TalkingTalkingNot" / motivational-interviewing approach, not coercion. If a family declines, the pediatrician documents the discussion, the specific vaccine declined, and the next planned conversation. Refusal does not result in disenrolment from SKIDS.
- **Religious and dietary practices.** Nutrition guidance accommodates vegetarian / Jain / halal / kosher / ethnic-regional dietary practices. The dietitian-supplied family-meal templates have variants per dietary practice [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm content set with B1-09 Nutrition team].
- **Caste-, language-, and class-related factors in clinic interactions.** The SKIDS clinic's protocol is to address every family with the same script, the same time allocation, and the same content depth. The Manager Console flags any visit where a family has been seen for materially less time than the median for their age band.

## §11 Manager handoff

A1 is the principal subscription-conversion moment in the SKIDS clinical model. Most enrolment decisions are made at or shortly after a child's first A1 visit (or at the visit where their flag profile changes). §11 specifies the Manager Console behaviour and the clinic-side scripts.

### 11.1 Manager Console renders

After every A1 visit, the Manager Console surfaces a card per child with:

- Child's name, age, sex.
- Date and type of A1 visit.
- Diagnosis-equivalent (parent-friendly summary of the day's flag profile — e.g., "all checks normal" / "monitoring growth and sleep" / "starting follow-up with our vision and behaviour clinics").
- Recommended subscription tier (per §11.2 logic).
- Recommended intervention tier within that subscription (Light / Standard / Intensive — corresponds to flag tier in §5).
- Pricing reference (current SKIDS pricing — auto-populated from operations module).
- Parent contact details (phone / WhatsApp).
- Parent-app engagement score (0–100; based on activations and check-in usage; influences the script).
- Suggested next action: "in-person enrolment now" / "digital follow-up via WhatsApp" / "no upgrade indicated".

### 11.2 Subscription tier match

| Flag profile from this A1 visit | Recommended subscription |
|---|---|
| All Green; no active prior B1 management | **Basic ₹2,999** — covers periodic A1, anticipatory guidance, vaccinations, supplementation. |
| Yellow flag(s) only; no active B1; no prior B1 in last 12 months | **Basic ₹2,999** if family is cost-sensitive; **Advanced ₹6,999** if multiple Yellow domains or known parent preference for inclusive packages. |
| Orange flag in any domain; expected B1 use ≥ 2 visits in next 12 months | **Advanced ₹6,999** — covers the four B1 visits typical for one Orange-domain follow-through. |
| Multiple Orange flags in different domains; expected B1 use ≥ 6 visits in next 12 months OR an active B1 management plan involving allied staff (dietitian, counsellor) | **Premium ₹12,999** — covers unlimited B1 visits within the clinically indicated set, plus priority booking. |
| Any §7 referral fired this visit (regardless of urgency) | **Premium ₹12,999** is *recommended but not gating* — the §7 referral handoff and follow-through happens regardless of subscription. The Premium tier covers SKIDS-side coordination during the partner's care, and the longitudinal re-entry care once the partner discharges. |

The matching logic is non-coercive. The pediatrician explicitly states in §10.2 that clinical care does not change with subscription tier. The Manager Console script frames the recommendation as a financial-planning conversation, not a clinical-gatekeeping one.

### 11.4 Digital enrolment flow (QR-scan parents)

For parents arriving via post-screening QR scan or via the parent app:

1. After the A1 visit completes, the parent app shows a "consider [recommended tier]" card on the home screen.
2. Tapping the card opens the bundle-comparison view with the recommended tier highlighted, plus the line-item breakdown.
3. The parent can choose: subscribe now (digital signature + payment), schedule a manager call-back, or dismiss.
4. If the parent subscribes, the subscription becomes active immediately; the next clinic visit and any pending B1 visits are processed under the new tier.
5. If the parent schedules a call-back, the manager call-back queue surfaces the child in the manager workflow.
6. If the parent dismisses, the recommendation re-surfaces at the next periodic A1 visit.

### 11.5 In-person enrolment script (walk-in parents)

Used when the family is at the clinic front desk after the A1 visit:

1. Manager greets by name, references the visit just completed.
2. Manager opens the Manager Console card for the child; positions the screen so the parent can see it.
3. Manager: "Dr. [name] has shared today's check-up summary. Based on what was found today, we recommend the [tier name] subscription. Want me to walk through what that includes?"
4. Manager walks through the bundle in a fixed structure: what's included, what's not, what the family would otherwise pay per-visit over 12 months, and the discount structure.
5. Manager addresses any questions; if scripted-objection match, uses §11.3 responses.
6. Manager offers three closes: enrol now / schedule a follow-up call / take the printed comparison and decide later.
7. Whichever the family chooses, manager logs the disposition in Manager Console with reason.

The clinic Manager does not pressure conversion. Conversion targets are tracked as quality metrics, not commission targets, in §12.

## §12 Quality and audit

### 12.1 Outcome metrics

A1 is working well when, across the clinic's panel of enrolled children:

- ≥ 90% of enrolled children have an up-to-date IAP IIS vaccination status [IAP-IIS-2024].
- ≥ 85% of enrolled children attend their periodic A1 visits within 30 days of the scheduled date.
- ≥ 95% of Orange flags result in a confirmed B1 visit within the protocol's specified intake window (§6.3).
- 100% of §7 emergency triggers (§7.1) result in a documented same-day handoff to the named partner.
- ≥ 90% of Yellow flags resolve to Green or progress to Orange by the second recheck (i.e., no domains stuck in Yellow for > 6 months).
- The flag-detection rate per domain is within 1 SD of the SKIDS-network median for similar age-band volumes (an outlier clinic — too few or too many flags — is reviewed for screening fidelity).
- The §11 conversion rate (recommended subscription accepted within 30 days) is ≥ [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — set target with operations team once initial SKIDS clinic data is available].
- The parent-app activation rate (parents engaging with at least one activated module within 14 days of a Yellow flag) is ≥ 70% [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm baseline with parent.skids.clinic team].

### 12.2 Quarterly case review

Every quarter, the protocol owner (named in §2) reviews:
- A random sample of 30 A1 visits from this clinic, balanced across age bands.
- All §7 referrals (§7.1, §7.2, §7.3) made in the quarter.
- All flag-tier overrides (Screen 5 in §9.1) made in the quarter, with particular attention to Orange→Yellow or Orange→Green downgrades.
- All "unable to assess" domains where the inability has persisted across two visits.
- All loss-to-follow-up cases per §8.7.
- All cases where a child progressed Green → Orange (skipping Yellow) — the "fast-progression flags".

Review output: a 1-page report submitted to SKIDS clinical operations, with any recommended protocol updates flagged for the next version. The protocol owner has authority to update §4 thresholds, §5 tier boundaries, and §6 intervention specifications; changes to §7 (sacred section) require sign-off by the named clinical leadership.

### 12.3 Pediatrician certification on this protocol

To deliver A1, a SKIDS pediatrician must:
- Complete the A1 onboarding chapter (8 hours — A1 is the longest onboarding because it spans every domain).
- Pass a structured case-vignette assessment (20 cases across age bands).
- Demonstrate device proficiency (Welch Allyn Spot, SoundScout, MUAC, BP cuff with pediatric sizing, otoscopy with pneumatic insufflation, POC Hb).
- Demonstrate growth-charting proficiency (correct plotting + interpretation of Z-scores and centile crossing on WHO and IAP charts).
- Complete the LISSUN partner onboarding for adolescent mental-health handoff (the single named pathway for §7.1 PHQ-A item 9 escalation).
- Maintain a minimum case load of 200 A1 visits per quarter (the front-door volume).
- Complete annual continuing education: 10 hours covering updates to AAP Bright Futures, IAP Periodicity, IAP IIS, AAP BP guideline, WHO growth standards, and any major new screening recommendations.

### 12.4 The SKIDS Fellowship pathway

A1 does not have a single corresponding Fellowship subspecialty (it spans every domain), but two pathways are relevant:

- **Developmental-Behavioural Pediatrics fellowship** (DBP) — pediatricians completing this fellowship deepen expertise in §4 developmental scales, the §5 behavioural-emotional flag tiering, and the B1-23 Developmental Surveillance and B1-08 Behavioural protocols. They become preferred candidates for A1 protocol owner.
- **Adolescent Medicine fellowship** — pediatricians completing this deepen expertise in HEEADSSS, the adolescent psychosocial domain of A1, and the B1-13 Adolescent protocol. They become preferred candidates for governance of the adolescent-band sections of A1.

Both pathways are funded by The SKIDS Fellowship; see `docs/06-the-skids-fellowship.md`.
