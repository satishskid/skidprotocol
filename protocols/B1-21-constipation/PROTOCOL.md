# SKIDS Constipation Clinic

> The SKIDS Constipation Clinic delivers Rome IV–based functional
> constipation management in children ≥ 6 months, including
> disimpaction protocols, maintenance osmotic-laxative therapy,
> dietary fibre guidance, toileting-behaviour training, and
> encopresis management. Organic constipation and refractory
> cases (> 3 months) route to B1-15 GI. Hirschsprung disease
> alarm features route per §7.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Diagnoses functional constipation per Rome IV criteria (≥ 2 of: ≤ 2 stools/week; ≥ 1 episode faecal incontinence/week; stool-retentive posturing; painful/hard stools; large-calibre stools; large faecal mass on exam — for ≥ 1 month (infants and toddlers < 4 years) or ≥ 2 months (children ≥ 4 years) [Rome-IV-2016]) [NASPGHAN-2018-FC, Rome-IV-2016].
- Manages disimpaction (faecal loading) with high-dose oral PEG 3350 (polyethylene glycol) protocol: 1.5 g/kg/day (max 100 g) × 3–6 days until impaction clears [NASPGHAN-2018-FC].
- Maintains laxative therapy with PEG 3350 0.4–0.8 g/kg/day for 3–6 months minimum (aim for soft daily stool, Bristol 3–4), then gradual weaning.
- Provides dietary-fibre counselling with SKIDS dietitian (cross B1-09): age + 5 g/day fibre target; adequate fluid intake; reduce excessive dairy if constipating.
- Delivers toileting-behaviour training: regular post-meal toilet sits (5–10 minutes, 2–3 times/day, using gastrocolic reflex); foot stool for posture; positive reinforcement (sticker chart); no punishment for soiling.
- Manages overflow faecal incontinence (encopresis) secondary to constipation with impaction — addresses the constipation, not the soiling (soiling resolves when constipation is managed).
- Screens for alarm features at every visit: delayed meconium passage (> 48h), failure to thrive, abdominal distension out of proportion, bloody stool without fissure, absent anal wink, ribbon stools → §7.3 to B1-15 / pediatric GI / surgery.
- Cross-cares with B1-20 Bedwetting (constipation-associated enuresis: treat constipation first; enuresis often resolves).
- Counsels on laxative safety: PEG is safe long-term; not habit-forming; address family concerns about "dependency."

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Manage organic constipation (Hirschsprung disease, anorectal malformation, spinal cord lesion, hypothyroidism-induced) — §7.3 to B1-15 or pediatric surgery.
- Perform rectal biopsy, anorectal manometry, or barium enema — §7.3.
- Manage functional constipation refractory to 3 months of optimised B1-21 management — escalates to B1-15 for organic-cause evaluation.
- Prescribe stimulant laxatives (bisacodyl, senna) as first-line — reserved for short-term rescue per §6.3.

**Age range covered:** 6 months – 18 years.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 elimination screening; first B1-21 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — structured constipation programme (disimpaction + maintenance + toileting training); dietitian cross-care; up to 6 visits.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited visits; B1-20 cross-care; B1-15 escalation coordination.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1. Protocol governance with named pediatric gastroenterologist (may share with B1-15). §7 escalates to B1-15 (refractory/organic) or pediatric surgery (Hirschsprung).

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 History
1. Stool pattern: frequency, consistency (Bristol Stool Scale), size, pain, blood on surface, posturing.
2. Onset: from birth (Hirschsprung concern) vs. toilet-training onset (functional) vs. post-illness (post-infectious).
3. Meconium passage: within 48h (normal); delayed > 48h (Hirschsprung disease alarm feature). Note: passage between 24–48h is within normal variation and is NOT an alarm feature.
4. Diet: fibre intake, fluid intake, excessive milk/dairy (> 500 mL/day constipating in toddlers).
5. Toileting: routine, avoidance of school/public toilets, punishment history.
6. Medications: iron supplementation (A1 prophylactic iron is common cause), opioids, anticonvulsants.
7. Encopresis: frequency, awareness vs. unaware (unaware = overflow from impaction).
8. Enuresis: cross B1-20 (faecal impaction compresses bladder → enuresis).
9. Family history: constipation, Hirschsprung disease, hypothyroidism.

### 4.2 Examination
- Abdominal: faecal mass palpable (left iliac fossa, suprapubic); distension.
- Perianal: fissure, skin tags (IBD concern if complex), soiling, rash (irritant dermatitis from soiling).
- Anal wink (test S2–4 reflex): absent → neurological cause → §7.3.
- Lumbosacral: sacral dimple, tuft, lipoma (spinal dysraphism).
- **No routine digital rectal examination in children** — only by pediatric GI/surgery at §7.3 if Hirschsprung suspected.

### 4.3 Investigations
- **None routinely required for functional constipation** [NASPGHAN-2018-FC].
- Abdominal X-ray: NOT recommended routinely; only if clinical assessment unclear.
- TSH: if constipation + growth faltering + other hypothyroid features.
- Coeliac screen (tTG-IgA): if constipation + growth faltering or refractory (cross B1-15).
- Further investigations at §7.3: barium enema, rectal biopsy (Hirschsprung), anorectal manometry, spinal MRI.

### 4.4 Bristol Stool Scale classification
Type 1–2: hard (constipated); Type 3–4: normal (target); Type 5–6: soft/loose; Type 7: watery.

### 4.5 Decision tree

```
Child with constipation symptoms
        │
        ├── Alarm features (delayed meconium, FTT, absent anal wink,
        │   ribbon stools, bloody stool without fissure) → §7.3
        │
        ├── Rome IV functional constipation criteria met
        │       ├── Impacted (palpable faecal mass) → §6.2 disimpaction first
        │       ├── Not impacted → §6.1 / §6.2 maintenance + toileting
        │
        ├── Refractory after 3 months optimised B1-21 → B1-15
        │
        ▼
§5 severity → §6 PEG + toileting programme
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | Rome IV met; < 1 soiling/week; no impaction; some hard stools | §6.1 dietary + toileting + low-dose PEG |
| **Moderate** | Rome IV met; soiling 1–3/week; palpable faecal mass; painful defaecation | §6.2 disimpaction + maintenance PEG + toileting programme |
| **Severe** | Rome IV met; daily soiling; large impaction; retentive behaviour dominant; psychosocial impact | §6.2 disimpaction + §6.3 intensive maintenance + B1-08 cross |
| **Refractory** | Optimised B1-21 management × 3 months without improvement | → B1-15 for organic-cause workup |

### Boundary rules
- Functional constipation = no alarm features + Rome IV criteria.
- Alarm features = always investigate (§7.3).
- Encopresis is a symptom of constipation, not a separate diagnosis — treat the constipation.
- Blood on stool surface + visible anal fissure = constipation-related fissure; treat constipation; fissure heals. Blood mixed with stool without fissure = alarm feature.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (mild)
- Dietary fibre: age + 5 g/day (e.g., 8-year-old needs 13 g/day); increase fruits, vegetables, whole grains, dal.
- Adequate fluid intake (water, not juice).
- Reduce excessive dairy (≤ 500 mL milk/day for toddlers; ≤ 400 mL for school-age).
- Regular toilet sits: 5–10 minutes after meals, 2–3 times/day; foot stool for correct posture (knees above hips).
- Low-dose PEG 3350: 0.4 g/kg/day; titrate to Bristol 3–4 daily stool.

### 6.2 Standard tier (moderate — disimpaction + maintenance)

**Disimpaction (first 3–6 days):**
- PEG 3350: 1.5 g/kg/day (max 100 g/day), dissolved in water/juice, given over the day.
- Target: watery stools (Bristol 6–7) confirming impaction cleared.
- If PEG alone insufficient after 6 days: add stimulant rescue (bisacodyl suppository 5–10 mg or sodium picosulfate 2.5–5 mg) × 1–2 days.

**Maintenance (minimum 3–6 months):**
- PEG 3350: 0.4–0.8 g/kg/day; titrate to Bristol 3–4 daily.
- Toileting-behaviour programme (as §6.1; more structured; parent-app tracking).
- Dietary modification (B1-09 dietitian cross).
- Do NOT stop PEG prematurely — premature withdrawal is the most common cause of relapse.

**Weaning:** after ≥ 3 months of daily soft stools, reduce PEG by 25% every 2–4 weeks. If stools harden at any step, return to previous dose × 4 weeks.

### 6.3 Intensive tier (severe)
- Disimpaction as §6.2.
- High-dose maintenance PEG (0.8–1.0 g/kg/day initially).
- Stimulant laxative adjunct (bisacodyl 5 mg or sodium picosulfate 2.5–5 mg at bedtime) × short-term (≤ 2 weeks) if PEG alone insufficient.
- B1-08 cross-care if soiling causes significant psychosocial distress (bullying, school avoidance, family conflict).
- B1-20 cross-care if enuresis co-exists.
- If refractory after 3 months optimised intensive → B1-15 for organic-cause workup.

### 6.4 Encopresis management
- Explain to family: soiling is involuntary (overflow around impaction); the child is not doing it on purpose; punishment makes it worse.
- Treat the constipation (disimpaction + maintenance); soiling resolves as impaction clears.
- Clean-up routine: non-punitive; child participates in changing/cleaning as age-appropriate (builds awareness, not shame).
- Underwear protection (dark-coloured underwear, spare underwear at school).
- School communication: if soiling at school → B1-21 provides school letter explaining medical condition.

### 6.5 Contraindications

| Therapeutic | Contraindication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| PEG | Bowel obstruction; perforation | §7.1 ER |
| Stimulant laxative (long-term) | Any (> 2 weeks) | Short-term rescue only |
| Mineral oil / liquid paraffin | Aspiration risk (< 1 year; neurological impairment with swallowing difficulty) | Do not use in at-risk children |
| Routine enemas | Children (traumatic; use oral route) | Rectal route only for acute disimpaction failure at §7.3 |

### 6.6 Dispensary stock
- PEG 3350 sachets (various sizes).
- Bisacodyl suppositories 5 mg, 10 mg.
- Sodium picosulfate drops.
- Lactulose solution (second-line if PEG not tolerated).
- Bristol Stool Scale visual chart (for parent/child reference).

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)
- [ ] **Suspected bowel obstruction** — bilious vomiting, abdominal distension, absent bowel sounds, severe pain. → ER + pediatric surgery.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)
- [ ] **Delayed meconium passage (> 48h) in infant with constipation** → pediatric surgery for Hirschsprung evaluation.
- [ ] **Constipation + failure to thrive + abdominal distension** (infant) → B1-15 / pediatric GI within 7 days.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)
- [ ] **Alarm features** (absent anal wink, ribbon stools, lumbosacral abnormality, bloody stool without fissure) → B1-15 / pediatric surgery for Hirschsprung / spinal workup.
- [ ] **Refractory functional constipation after 3 months optimised B1-21** → B1-15 for organic-cause evaluation.
- [ ] **Constipation + hypothyroid features** → B2-01 Endocrine + B1-21 continues.
- [ ] **Constipation + coeliac features** → B1-15 for coeliac screening + B1-21 continues.
- [ ] **Severe psychosocial impact of encopresis** → B1-08 for psychological support.

### 7.4 Handoff communication
Standard: stool diary, Bristol Scale log, PEG dose history, disimpaction response, alarm-feature screen result, examination findings.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

| Scenario | First follow-up | Subsequent |
|---|---|---|
| Mild (PEG initiation) | 4 weeks | Monthly × 3; then 3-monthly during weaning |
| Post-disimpaction | 1–2 weeks (confirm clearance) | Monthly × 3; then as above |
| Severe / encopresis | 2 weeks | Monthly × 6; then weaning phase |
| Weaning phase | Every 4 weeks during dose reduction | Discharge when off PEG × 3 months with normal stools |

§8.3 Success: daily soft stool (Bristol 3–4); no soiling; no pain; toileting routine established; PEG weaned successfully; no relapse at 3 months off PEG.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

> "[Aanya]'s bowel has been stretched by the hard stool building up inside. The muscle can't push properly, and loose stool leaks around the blockage — that's why she soils. We need to clear the blockage first with a strong dose of medicine for a few days, then keep the stool soft with a daily medicine for several months so the bowel can shrink back to normal size. This is not her fault, and the soiling will stop once we fix the constipation."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities
- *Laxative fear.* "Won't she become dependent?" — PEG is not habit-forming; it is an osmotic agent that holds water in the stool. The bowel works normally once PEG is stopped; the long course is needed for the stretched bowel to recover.
- *Encopresis shame.* "He's doing it on purpose." — He is not. The stretched bowel loses sensation; the child does not feel the leak. Punishment makes it worse. Frame as a medical problem, not a behaviour problem.
- *Diet-only approach.* "Can't we just change the diet?" — Diet helps, but once impaction has developed, diet alone cannot resolve it. The medicine clears the blockage; the diet prevents recurrence.
- *Traditional remedies.* Isabgol (psyllium husk) is a reasonable fibre supplement; castor oil is harsh and not recommended for children; "gripe water" has no evidence for constipation.
