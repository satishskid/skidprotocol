## What was drafted

All 12 sections per `SPECS.md` §1 are present at or near target depth.
B1-01 is the third protocol in the library and the first device-and-
allied-staff-driven B1 protocol; it inherits the voice, three-tier
severity grading (Mild / Moderate / Severe with §7 Refractory), and
§9 Companion structure from A1 + B1-05.

Domains covered:
- Refractive error (myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism) graded Mild /
  Moderate / High, with in-clinic spectacle dispensing.
- Progressive myopia with the IMI-2021-aligned myopia-control
  programme (counselling + behavioural + low-dose atropine 0.01% /
  0.05%).
- Allergic conjunctivitis (Mild / Moderate); vernal / atopic / GPC
  to §7.3.
- Bacterial conjunctivitis (uncomplicated, > 48 hours purulent);
  hyperacute / gonococcal / herpetic to §7.1.
- Convergence insufficiency / digital eye strain; structured 6-week
  home programme; refractory to §7.3.
- Colour-vision testing (Ishihara) once between 8–12 and at
  adolescent transition.
- External lid disease mild (chalazion, hordeolum, blepharitis).

§7 sacred section drafted with explicit thresholds:
- §7.1 Emergency: leukocoria, acute traumatic eye injury, sudden
  vision loss, suspected orbital cellulitis, hyperacute / gonococcal
  conjunctivitis, suspected herpetic keratitis, acute angle closure,
  papilloedema / sixth-nerve palsy, suspected NAI with eye findings.
- §7.2 Urgent (≤ 7 days): confirmed amblyopia in child < 7 years,
  new-onset strabismus < 6 months, acquired ptosis with axis
  obstruction, suspected uveitis, diplopia with cranial-nerve
  palsy, asymmetric red reflex.
- §7.3 Routine (≤ 4 weeks): constant manifest strabismus, intermittent
  strabismus > 4 months age, refractory convergence insufficiency,
  Rx not improving acuity to age cutoff, progressive myopia not
  controlled on atropine 0.05%, high myopia in young child, vernal
  / atopic / GPC conjunctivitis, persistent chalazion, recurrent
  hordeolum / blepharitis, congenital or new-onset nystagmus,
  family request for orthokeratology / contact-lens fitting,
  inability to obtain undilated fundus view, anisometropia > 1.50 D.

## Judgement calls the editorial reviewer should re-check

- **Universal myopia-control offer to all confirmed myopes ≥ 6
  years.** Departure from AAPOS (selective for documented
  progressive cases). Defended in §2 on Indian-context epidemiology
  and IMI-2021 evidence base. Re-check at editorial pass —
  particularly whether the §11 subscription tier framing aligns
  with the conversion-rate implications of universal-offer.

- **Atropine concentration ceiling at 0.05% for B1-01.** Per
  IMI-2021, higher concentrations require pediatric ophthalmology
  supervision. The 0.05% ceiling is conservative; some centres go
  to 0.10%. Re-check.

- **In-clinic spectacle dispensing.** Operational departure with
  conversion-rate / dropout-rate implications. The clinical case
  is strong (Indian out-of-pocket cost-sensitive families have
  high optical-shop dropout); the operational case requires
  vendor / supply-chain confirmation.

- **B1-01 cannot operate without an in-clinic optometrist.**
  Stated explicitly in §1. Re-check that every SKIDS clinic
  launching B1-01 has an optometrist on day one; if not, the §1
  fallback (B1-01 degrades to routing-only) needs operational
  detail in the SKIDS clinic-operations document.

- **Topical steroid eye drops not prescribed at B1-01.** All
  indications route §7.3 to pediatric ophthalmology. This is
  conservative but defensible — pediatric topical steroid use
  warrants slit-lamp surveillance for IOP and corneal-thinning,
  which is outside Tier 1 capability.

- **Bacterial conjunctivitis treatment threshold (> 48 hours
  purulent).** A modest conservatism on top of AAO; many Indian
  pediatricians treat empirically at 24 hours. Confirm at
  editorial pass.
