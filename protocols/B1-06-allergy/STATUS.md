## What was drafted

Sixth protocol in the library. Closes the A1 §7.1 anaphylaxis-without-
action-plan loop — every at-risk family now has a defined B1-06
pathway to action plan + auto-injector dispensing.

Domains covered:
- Allergic rhinitis (ARIA-graded; mild / moderate / severe persistent
  with stepped antihistamine + intranasal corticosteroid + montelukast +
  SLIT for monosensitised cases).
- Food allergy (IgE-mediated; SPT + sIgE confirmation; structured
  elimination; action plan + auto-injector dispensing).
- Atopic dermatitis with food trigger (joint with B1-05).
- Cow's milk protein allergy (joint with B1-09).
- Acute and chronic urticaria (UAS7 graded; up-dose H1-antihistamine
  to 4× standard; refractory to §7.3).
- Drug allergy initial assessment + alert-card issuance.
- Insect venom allergy with action plan + VIT candidacy routing.
- Aeroallergen sensitisation — SLIT for monosensitised house-dust-mite
  or single-pollen ≥ 5 years.
- Atopy-prevention counselling per LEAP / NIAID — early peanut
  introduction at 4–6 months in atopic-risk infants.

Established in this protocol:
- Adrenaline auto-injector dispensing operational pattern (weight-
  banded dose: 150 µg for 7.5–25 kg; 300 µg for ≥ 25 kg; ampoule +
  IM kit for < 7.5 kg).
- Action plan generation pattern (signed by ped + parent + adolescent;
  copy to school + PHR).
- In-clinic oral food challenge (OFC) low-risk pattern — 4-hour
  observation + full resuscitation kit verified pre-procedure +
  Companion hard-block on procedure start without kit-check.
- SLIT initiation in primary care (departure from some Indian-context
  practices that restrict to specialist).
- Resuscitation-kit-mandatory-in-room rule for any SPT or OFC.

§7 sacred section drafted with explicit thresholds:
- §7.1 Emergency: anaphylaxis at presentation (with IM adrenaline
  given immediately at B1-06 before transfer), suspected SJS / TEN /
  DRESS (cross with B1-05 §7.1), suspected HAE acute attack, severe
  drug-induced anaphylaxis, OFC / SPT-induced systemic reaction at
  B1-06.
- §7.2 Urgent: severe chronic urticaria refractory to 4× antihistamine,
  suspected HAE, suspected mastocytosis, FPIES suspected, suspected
  eosinophilic oesophagitis.
- §7.3 Routine: refractory rhinitis at 12 weeks, multi-food allergy,
  OIT candidacy, high-risk OFC, insect venom anaphylaxis history for
  VIT, polysensitisation aeroallergen, suspected primary
  immunodeficiency, drug-allergy challenge candidacy, patch testing.
