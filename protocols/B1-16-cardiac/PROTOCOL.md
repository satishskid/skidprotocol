# SKIDS Cardiac Clinic

> The SKIDS Cardiac Clinic delivers structured murmur evaluation
> (innocent vs. pathological), BP monitoring for confirmed
> hypertension (co-referred with B1-17), sports-clearance cardiac
> assessment, CCHD screen reconciliation, chest-pain evaluation,
> syncope triage, RF/RHD echocardiographic monitoring (cross B1-03),
> and routing to pediatric cardiology for echocardiography, ECG
> interpretation, and intervention. Functions as a bridge protocol
> between A1 screening and pediatric cardiology partner.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Evaluates murmurs detected at A1: differentiates innocent (Still's, venous hum, pulmonary flow) from pathological (pansystolic, diastolic, grade ≥ 3/6, associated with symptoms/signs) using clinical assessment and AyuSynk digital stethoscope PCG capture [AAP-BF-4E].
- Manages confirmed hypertension in coordination with B1-17 Kidney & Urology: B1-16 performs serial BP monitoring, ECG, echocardiography referral (target-organ assessment), and lifestyle management for obesity-related hypertension (cross B1-10) [AAP-2017-HBP].
- Conducts pre-participation sports-clearance cardiac evaluation for competitive sports: structured cardiac history (exertional symptoms, family history of sudden cardiac death, syncope), cardiac examination, ECG referral where indicated [AHA-PPE-2014].
- Reconciles CCHD (critical congenital heart disease) newborn screening results at first SKIDS visit: confirms pulse oximetry CCHD screen was performed at birthing facility; if not done → perform at B1-16 (SpO₂ right hand + either foot; fail = difference > 3% or any reading < 95%) [AAP-CCHD-2011].
- Evaluates pediatric chest pain: structured differential (musculoskeletal most common; costochondritis; exercise-induced asthma cross B1-14; GORD cross B1-15; anxiety cross B1-08; cardiac rare) [AAP-ChestPain-2015].
- Triages syncope: vasovagal (most common; benign) vs. cardiac (exertional, without prodrome, family history of sudden death → §7.1/§7.2).
- Monitors children with known congenital heart disease (post-repair or un-operated) in coordination with pediatric cardiology partner: growth, exercise tolerance, medication compliance, prophylaxis adherence.
- Co-manages RF/RHD echocardiographic monitoring with B1-03 Throat & Airway: B1-03 manages benzathine penicillin prophylaxis; B1-16 manages echocardiographic surveillance of valvular disease.
- Monitors cardiac safety for ADHD pharmacotherapy: pre-stimulant ECG where indicated, BP and HR monitoring at every B1-08 ADHD visit (cross B1-08).
- Co-manages dyslipidaemia pharmacotherapy candidacy from B1-10: children with LDL persistently elevated despite dietary trial route to B1-16 for cardiovascular-risk assessment and statin consideration.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform echocardiography (§7.3 to pediatric cardiology partner for echo).
- Interpret ECG independently (ECG ordered at B1-16; interpretation by pediatric cardiologist or auto-interpretation with cardiologist over-read) [TO BE DECIDED BY SKIDS PEDIATRICIAN].
- Manage heart failure, arrhythmias, or cardiomyopathy — §7.2/§7.3 to pediatric cardiology.
- Perform cardiac catheterisation or intervention — Tier 3.
- Manage Kawasaki disease beyond initial recognition and §7.1 routing.

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 cardiac screening (BP from age 3, pulse oximetry CCHD, murmur auscultation); first B1-16 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — serial BP monitoring programme; AyuSynk PCG murmur capture; sports-clearance; up to 4 B1-16 visits; ECG referral coordination.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited visits; echocardiography coordination; RF/RHD monitoring; multi-protocol cross-care (B1-16 + B1-03 + B1-10 + B1-17); priority pediatric cardiology referral.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 bridge protocol. B1-16 evaluates, stratifies, and monitors — pediatric cardiology partner (Tier 3) provides echocardiography, ECG interpretation, and definitive cardiac management. Protocol governance with named pediatric cardiologist.

**Departures:**

1. *ECG not routine at sports clearance.* AHA 2014 recommends history + physical for sports clearance in the US (no routine ECG). Some European/Asian protocols include ECG. SKIDS performs clinical assessment at B1-16; ECG is ordered only if history/exam raises concern. This is AHA-concordant.

2. *Echo not performed at SKIDS.* SKIDS does not have in-clinic echocardiography; echo referral is to pediatric cardiology partner. B1-16 is a bridge, not a definitive cardiac clinic.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Murmur evaluation

**Innocent murmur characteristics (all must be present):**
- Grade 1–2/6 systolic.
- Musical / vibratory quality (Still's), or soft blowing at upper left sternal border (pulmonary flow), or continuous hum at neck (venous hum, abolished by turning head or lying flat).
- No associated symptoms (no cyanosis, dyspnoea, feeding difficulty, failure to thrive).
- Normal cardiac examination otherwise (normal S1, S2, no clicks, no gallop).
- Normal growth.

**Pathological murmur indicators (any one triggers §7.3):**
- Grade ≥ 3/6.
- Diastolic murmur (any grade).
- Pansystolic / harsh quality.
- Associated with symptoms (exertional dyspnoea, cyanosis, syncope, feeding difficulty in infant, FTT).
- Associated with abnormal cardiac exam (widely split fixed S2, ejection click, gallop rhythm).
- AyuSynk PCG confirms pathological pattern.

**Decision:** innocent murmur → reassure, document, no referral needed (re-evaluate at next A1). Pathological or uncertain → §7.3 echocardiography referral.

### 4.2 Hypertension evaluation

- Confirm: ≥ 3 readings of BP ≥ 95th centile for age/height/sex on separate occasions [AAP-2017-HBP].
- **Stage 1 HTN:** BP 95th–99th centile + 5 mmHg. → lifestyle modification + recheck 1–2 weeks; if persistent → §7.3 workup.
- **Stage 2 HTN:** BP > 99th centile + 5 mmHg. → §7.2 urgent evaluation.
- **Co-refer B1-17** for renal cause exclusion (renal ultrasound, urinalysis, creatinine, electrolytes).
- **B1-10 cross-care** if obesity-related (lifestyle management addresses both HTN and obesity).
- Ambulatory BP monitoring (ABPM): §7.3 to pediatric cardiology/nephrology if white-coat HTN suspected.

### 4.3 Sports-clearance cardiac assessment

Pre-participation evaluation per AHA-PPE-2014:
1. **History:** exertional chest pain? exertional dyspnoea disproportionate to effort? exertional syncope/near-syncope? palpitations? family history of sudden cardiac death < 50 years? family history of cardiomyopathy, long QT, Marfan syndrome?
2. **Examination:** BP; auscultation (murmur assessment); femoral pulses; Marfan features (arm span, arachnodactyly, pectus, scoliosis, lens subluxation — if suspected → §7.3 genetics).
3. **ECG:** NOT routine; ordered only if positive history or abnormal exam.
4. **Decision:** all negative → sports-clearance certificate issued. Any positive → §7.3 for echo/ECG before clearance.

### 4.4 Chest-pain evaluation

**Differential (by frequency in children):**
1. **Musculoskeletal** (most common) — reproducible with palpation, history of injury/strain, costochondritis.
2. **Exercise-induced asthma** (cross B1-14) — chest tightness with exercise, relieved by rest/SABA.
3. **GORD** (cross B1-15) — burning, postprandial, positional.
4. **Anxiety** (cross B1-08) — situational, associated with hyperventilation, no exercise trigger.
5. **Cardiac** (rare) — exertional, associated with syncope/palpitations/family history of sudden death → §7.2.

**Cardiac red flags in chest pain:**
- Exertional chest pain with syncope.
- Palpitations with chest pain.
- Chest pain with known CHD.
- Family history of sudden cardiac death < 50 years.
- Any of above → §7.2 urgent → ECG + echo via pediatric cardiology.

### 4.5 Syncope triage

| Feature | Vasovagal (benign) | Cardiac (dangerous) |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Prolonged standing, heat, dehydration, pain, emotional | Exertion, swimming, loud noise (LQTS), none |
| Prodrome | Dizziness, warmth, nausea, visual dimming | None or palpitations only |
| Recovery | Rapid, complete | Slow; may have seizure-like activity |
| Family history | Common (vasovagal runs in families) | Sudden death, cardiomyopathy, LQTS, defibrillator |
| Action | §6.1 education + hydration | §7.1/§7.2 → ECG + echo + Holter via cardiology |

### 4.6 CCHD screen reconciliation

- Confirm CCHD pulse-oximetry screen performed at birthing facility (≥ 24h age).
- If not done: perform at B1-16 — SpO₂ right hand + either foot.
- **Pass:** both ≥ 95% AND difference ≤ 3%.
- **Fail:** any reading < 95% OR difference > 3% → repeat in 1h → if still fails → §7.2 echo urgent.

### 4.7 ADHD cardiac monitoring (B1-08 cross)

- Pre-stimulant: baseline BP, HR. ECG if: personal history of cardiac disease, family history of sudden death / LQTS / cardiomyopathy / WPW, or cardiac symptoms.
- On-stimulant: BP and HR at every B1-08 visit. If BP ≥ 95th centile or HR > age-appropriate range → B1-16 evaluation.
- Do NOT withhold stimulants for "routine" cardiac concern without documented cardiac pathology — ADHD treatment benefit outweighs low cardiac risk in structurally normal hearts [AAP-2019-ADHD].

### 4.8 In-clinic devices

- **AyuSynk** (or equivalent digital stethoscope with PCG): captures murmur waveform for remote cardiologist review.
- **BP cuff** (pediatric sizes; adult size for obese/adolescent): 3-reading protocol.
- **Pulse oximeter** (CCHD screen; any cardiac assessment).
- **ECG machine** [TO BE DECIDED BY SKIDS PEDIATRICIAN — confirm whether SKIDS clinics stock 12-lead ECG].

### 4.9 Decision tree

```
Child with cardiac concern
        │
        ├── Murmur → §4.1 → innocent (reassure) / pathological (§7.3 echo)
        ├── HTN → §4.2 → confirm + B1-17 renal + B1-10 obesity cross
        ├── Sports clearance → §4.3 → clear / §7.3 if positive
        ├── Chest pain → §4.4 → non-cardiac (treat cause) / cardiac (§7.2)
        ├── Syncope → §4.5 → vasovagal (§6.1) / cardiac (§7.1/§7.2)
        ├── CCHD screen → §4.6 → pass / fail (§7.2 echo)
        │
        ▼
§5 severity → §6 management → §7 cardiology
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

### 5.1 Murmur

| Finding | Action |
|---|---|
| Innocent murmur (all criteria met) | Reassure; no referral; re-evaluate next A1 |
| Uncertain / borderline | §7.3 echo to confirm |
| Pathological indicators present | §7.3 echo urgent |

### 5.2 Hypertension

| Stage | BP level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Elevated BP | 90th–<95th centile | Lifestyle; recheck 6 months |
| Stage 1 | 95th to <95th + 12 mmHg (or 130/80 to <140/90 in adolescents ≥ 13y) | Lifestyle + recheck 1–2 weeks; if persistent → §7.3 |
| Stage 2 | ≥ 95th centile + 12 mmHg (or ≥ 140/90 in adolescents, whichever lower) | §7.2 urgent |
| Hypertensive emergency | ≥ Stage 2 + symptoms (headache, visual, seizure) | §7.1 ER |

### 5.3 Chest pain / syncope

| Pattern | Action |
|---|---|
| Non-cardiac chest pain (musculoskeletal, GORD, anxiety) | Treat cause; no cardiac workup |
| Vasovagal syncope | §6.1 education |
| Cardiac red flags (exertional symptoms, family hx sudden death) | §7.2 urgent cardiology |
| Exertional syncope | §7.1 immediate |

### 5.4 Boundary rules
- Innocent murmur does NOT require echocardiography; only pathological or uncertain murmurs do.
- BP elevation at a single visit does NOT confirm HTN; ≥ 3 readings on separate occasions required.
- Non-cardiac chest pain is the overwhelming majority in children; cardiac chest pain is rare but must be ruled out by history.
- Exertional syncope = cardiac until proven otherwise → §7.1/§7.2.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier
- **Innocent murmur:** reassurance; no activity restriction; no follow-up beyond routine A1.
- **Vasovagal syncope:** increase fluid + salt intake; avoid prolonged standing; counter-pressure manoeuvres (leg crossing, squatting when prodrome felt); no activity restriction.
- **Elevated BP (pre-hypertension):** lifestyle (DASH-style diet for adolescents; sodium reduction; weight management cross B1-10; aerobic exercise ≥ 60 min/day). Recheck at 6 months.

### 6.2 Standard tier
- **Stage 1 HTN (persistent):** lifestyle intensification; if not improving after 6 months lifestyle → §7.3 for renal workup (B1-17 cross) + consideration of antihypertensive (initiated by pediatric cardiologist/nephrologist, not B1-16).
- **RF/RHD echo monitoring:** echocardiography at diagnosis, then per rheumatic-cardiology protocol (typically annual for mild; 6-monthly for moderate). B1-03 manages prophylaxis adherence; B1-16 tracks echo findings.
- **ADHD cardiac monitoring:** per §4.7; ongoing at every B1-08 visit; B1-16 evaluates if abnormality detected.

### 6.3 Intensive tier
- Known CHD monitoring (§6.4).
- Dyslipidaemia statin assessment: cardiovascular-risk assessment for B1-10 referrals with LDL ≥ 160–190 mg/dL after 6-month dietary trial. Statin initiation by pediatric cardiologist (§7.3), not B1-16.

### 6.4 Known CHD monitoring programme
- Growth tracking (CHD children at risk for FTT).
- Exercise tolerance assessment.
- Medication compliance (digoxin, diuretics, ACE inhibitors — as prescribed by cardiology).
- Endocarditis prophylaxis awareness (high-risk lesions: prosthetic valves, unrepaired cyanotic CHD, recent repair with prosthetic material).
- Echo/ECG per cardiologist's follow-up schedule — B1-16 coordinates and tracks.
- Transition to adult cardiology at 18 years.

### 6.5 RF/RHD monitoring (cross B1-03)
- B1-03 delivers benzathine penicillin G prophylaxis.
- B1-16 orders echocardiography per schedule; documents valvular status; communicates changes to B1-03 (prophylaxis duration depends on valve involvement).

### 6.6 Dispensary stock
- BP cuffs (pediatric sizes: infant, child, small adult, large adult).
- AyuSynk device (or equivalent digital stethoscope).
- Pulse oximeter.
- ECG machine or ECG referral pathway [TO BE DECIDED BY SKIDS PEDIATRICIAN].

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)
- [ ] **Exertional syncope** → ER + pediatric cardiology (risk of sudden cardiac death from HCM, LQTS, ARVC).
- [ ] **Hypertensive emergency** — Stage 2 HTN + symptoms (headache, visual changes, seizure, chest pain). → ER.
- [ ] **Cyanotic episode in infant** (known or unknown CHD). → ER.
- [ ] **Suspected Kawasaki disease** — fever ≥ 5 days + ≥ 4 of: bilateral conjunctivitis, oral mucous changes, rash, extremity changes, cervical lymphadenopathy. → Pediatric cardiology within 24h (echocardiography for coronary assessment; IVIG within 10 days of fever onset).
- [ ] **Acute chest pain with cardiac features** (exertional, with syncope, with known CHD, with palpitations + haemodynamic compromise). → ER.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)
- [ ] **Stage 2 hypertension** (asymptomatic) → Pediatric cardiology/nephrology.
- [ ] **CCHD screen fail** (confirmed on repeat) → Pediatric cardiology for echo within 7 days.
- [ ] **Cardiac syncope suspected** (no prodrome, exertional trigger, family history sudden death) → Pediatric cardiology for ECG + echo + Holter.
- [ ] **Chest pain with cardiac red flags** (exertional + family history + palpitations, without haemodynamic compromise) → Pediatric cardiology.
- [ ] **Pathological murmur detected** (grade ≥ 3, diastolic, associated symptoms) → Echo within 7 days.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)
- [ ] **Uncertain murmur** (not clearly innocent) → Pediatric cardiology for echo.
- [ ] **Stage 1 HTN persistent after 6 months lifestyle** → Pediatric cardiology/nephrology for pharmacotherapy consideration.
- [ ] **Known CHD — routine echo per cardiologist schedule** → Pediatric cardiology.
- [ ] **RF/RHD — routine echo per schedule** → Pediatric cardiology.
- [ ] **Dyslipidaemia statin candidacy assessment** (from B1-10) → Pediatric cardiology for cardiovascular-risk assessment.
- [ ] **Suspected Marfan syndrome** (tall, arachnodactyly, pectus, arm span, family history) → Clinical genetics + pediatric cardiology for aortic-root assessment.
- [ ] **Pre-stimulant ECG with abnormal finding** → Pediatric cardiology review before ADHD pharmacotherapy initiation.
- [ ] **Frequent PVCs or palpitations in otherwise well child** → Pediatric cardiology for Holter.

### 7.4 Handoff communication
Standard: BP log (3-reading × multiple visits), AyuSynk PCG capture, CCHD screen result, cardiac history (family history of sudden death, exertional symptoms), growth trajectory, medication list, RF/RHD prophylaxis history.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Innocent murmur:
> "The murmur I hear in [Aanya]'s heart is an innocent murmur — it is a normal sound made by blood flowing through a healthy heart. About 50% of children have this sound at some point. It does not mean there is anything wrong with [her] heart, and [she] does not need any tests or restrictions. I will listen again at [her] next check-up, and the sound will likely disappear as [she] grows."

Hypertension:
> "[Arjun]'s blood pressure is higher than we'd like for [his] age and height. This doesn't mean he has heart disease — it means we need to investigate why. Sometimes it's related to weight, sometimes to the kidneys, and sometimes it's just the way the body is wired. We'll check it a few more times, do some blood and urine tests, and work on diet and activity. Most children with high blood pressure respond well to lifestyle changes."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities
- *Heart murmur anxiety.* Parents hear "heart murmur" and think "heart disease." Reassure explicitly: "Innocent murmur = normal heart." Use the analogy: "It's like hearing water flow through pipes — the pipes are fine."
- *Sports restriction after murmur.* Parents may restrict activity after innocent-murmur diagnosis. Counsel: no restrictions needed; full sports participation.
- *"BP is for old people."* Many Indian families don't expect BP screening in children. Explain: childhood HTN is increasingly common, especially with rising obesity rates; early detection prevents adult cardiovascular disease.
- *Kawasaki disease awareness.* Low awareness in India; persistent high fever in young children is often attributed to "viral" or treated with repeated antibiotics. B1-16 should maintain high index of suspicion for Kawasaki in fever ≥ 5 days with features.
