# SKIDS Learning Clinic

> The SKIDS Learning Clinic delivers structured assessment of
> school-age learning concerns, screens for specific learning
> disabilities (dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia), coordinates
> with B1-08 for ADHD-learning overlap, coordinates with B1-11
> for speech-language-learning overlap, initiates educational-
> support recommendations, and routes for formal psychoeducational
> assessment to clinical psychologist / educational psychologist.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Evaluates school-age children (≥ 5 years) with learning concerns: academic underperformance, reading difficulty, writing difficulty, mathematics difficulty, homework resistance, school avoidance related to academic demands.
- Screens for specific learning disabilities (SLD) using clinical history, teacher input (structured questionnaire), and screening tools (DAST — Dyslexia Adult/Adolescent Screening Test adaptation, or Indian-adapted learning-disability screening tools).
- Differentiates SLD from: ADHD-related academic difficulty (B1-08 cross — Vanderbilt), intellectual disability (B1-23 cross — psychometric testing), hearing loss (B1-02 cross), vision impairment (B1-01 cross), emotional/behavioural cause (B1-08 cross), and inadequate educational opportunity.
- Coordinates formal psychoeducational assessment: routes to clinical psychologist / educational psychologist for WISC (Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children) / Malin's Intelligence Scale for Indian Children (MISIC) + achievement testing (§7.3).
- Initiates classroom-accommodation recommendations: preferential seating, extra time, simplified instructions, multisensory teaching, reduced homework load — communicated to school via B1-12 school letter.
- Coordinates remedial-education referral: connects family with remedial-education tutors or special-education resource rooms.
- Supports RPWD Act (2016) documentation for SLD: children with formally diagnosed SLD are eligible for accommodations and disability certification in India.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform formal psychometric testing (WISC, MISIC) — §7.3 to clinical/educational psychologist.
- Diagnose SLD formally (requires psychometric testing + achievement discrepancy analysis at §7.3).
- Provide remedial education directly — referral to remedial-education partner.
- Manage ADHD pharmacotherapy — B1-08.
- Manage intellectual disability — B1-23 + §7.3.
- Provide school-placement decisions — advisory only.

**Age range covered:** 5 – 18 years.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 developmental/school screening; first B1-12 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — structured learning assessment; school communication; up to 4 B1-12 visits; psychoeducational assessment coordination.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited visits; multi-protocol cross-care (B1-12 + B1-08 + B1-11 + B1-23); remedial-education coordination; RPWD documentation support.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 with allied-staff dependency on the SKIDS educational counsellor (may share role with B1-08 counsellor or B1-23 coordinator). Protocol governance with named DBP or educational psychologist. §7 escalates to clinical psychologist (formal testing), B1-08 (ADHD management), B1-23 (intellectual disability).

## §2 Evidence base

- **[AAP-LearningDisability-2014]** — Handler SM, Fierson WM; AAP. Learning Disabilities, Dyslexia, and Vision. Pediatrics. 2011;128(5):e1173–e1180. Source for learning-disability assessment framework and vision-learning myth debunking.
- **[DSM-5-SLD]** — American Psychiatric Association. DSM-5 Specific Learning Disorder criteria. Source for diagnostic classification.
- **[RPWD-2016]** — Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (India). Legal framework for SLD accommodations and disability certification.
- **[IAP-Learning-2020]** — Indian Academy of Pediatrics. Position statement on learning disabilities in the Indian context, 2020 [TO BE DECIDED BY SKIDS PEDIATRICIAN].
- **[AAP-BF-4E]** — Already referenced. School-performance screening at periodic visits.
- **[NELSON-21:CH49]** — Nelson, learning-disability chapter.
- **[IAP-TB-6E]** — IAP Textbook, developmental-behavioural chapters.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-30. **Next review:** 2027-04-30.

**Departures:**

1. *SLD screening (not diagnosis) at B1-12.* Formal SLD diagnosis requires psychometric testing + achievement discrepancy, which is beyond Tier 1 scope. B1-12 screens, differentiates, and routes for formal diagnosis at §7.3. This is conservative but protects against mislabelling.

2. *Vision therapy not recommended for SLD.* AAP 2011 is explicit: there is no evidence that vision therapy, coloured lenses, or eye exercises treat learning disabilities. B1-12 follows this position; B1-01 treats refractive error if present but this does not address SLD.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 History
1. Academic history: grade, performance trajectory (when did difficulty begin), subjects affected, report cards (bring last 2–3).
2. Reading: decoding (sounding out words), fluency, comprehension. Read aloud in clinic.
3. Writing: handwriting (legibility, speed, effort), spelling, composition.
4. Mathematics: number sense, arithmetic operations, word problems.
5. Developmental history: language milestones (B1-11 cross), motor milestones (fine motor for writing).
6. Attention and behaviour: ADHD screen (Vanderbilt — B1-08 cross); is the difficulty attention-based or skill-based?
7. Hearing (B1-02) and vision (B1-01): confirm both screened.
8. Emotional: school anxiety, test anxiety, perfectionism, school avoidance, bullying.
9. Home environment: homework supervision, parental literacy, language of instruction vs. home language.
10. School environment: class size, teaching method, teacher concern (structured teacher questionnaire).
11. Family history: learning disabilities, ADHD, dyslexia.

### 4.2 Clinical screening

**Reading screen (in-clinic):**
- Age-appropriate reading passage: assess decoding accuracy, reading speed (words/minute), comprehension (retell/questions).
- Letter/word recognition: assess phonological awareness (rhyming, initial-sound identification, syllable segmentation).

**Writing screen:**
- Handwriting sample: legibility, letter formation, spacing, speed.
- Spelling: age-appropriate word list (dictated).
- Composition: write 3–5 sentences about a topic.

**Mathematics screen:**
- Number recognition, place value, basic operations (age-appropriate), word-problem comprehension.

**Attention screen:**
- Vanderbilt teacher + parent forms (cross B1-08). If ADHD criteria met → B1-08 for ADHD management. ADHD + SLD comorbidity is common (~30–50%).

### 4.3 Teacher input
- Structured teacher questionnaire (sent via Companion parent app to school): academic performance relative to peers, attention in class, behaviour, social interaction, specific areas of difficulty, classroom accommodations already tried.

### 4.4 Screening tools

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **NIMHANS SLD Battery** (Indian) | SLD screening for reading, writing, arithmetic |
| **DAST (adapted)** | Dyslexia screening (adolescents) |
| **Vanderbilt (teacher + parent)** | ADHD differentiation (cross B1-08) |
| **SDQ** | Emotional-behavioural screen |
| **Clinical reading/writing/maths assessment** | In-clinic functional screen |

### 4.5 Decision tree

```
Child with learning concern (≥ 5 years)
        │
        ├── Hearing (B1-02) + Vision (B1-01) → normal? If not → treat first
        │
        ├── ADHD screen (Vanderbilt) positive → B1-08 co-manage
        ├── Intellectual disability suspected (global delay) → B1-23
        ├── Emotional / school avoidance primary → B1-08
        │
        ├── Skill-specific deficit (reading / writing / maths)
        │       → SLD screening positive → §7.3 formal psychoeducational testing
        │
        ├── Borderline / unclear → monitor + classroom accommodations × 1 term
        │
        ▼
§5 severity → §6 accommodations + remedial referral
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **At risk** | Teacher concern + mild academic gap; screening borderline | §6.1 — classroom accommodations + monitor |
| **Probable SLD** | Consistent skill-specific deficit on screening; significant academic gap despite adequate instruction | §6.2 — accommodations + §7.3 formal testing |
| **Confirmed SLD** (post-§7.3 testing) | Psychometric testing confirms SLD + achievement discrepancy | §6.3 — formal accommodations + remedial programme + RPWD support |
| **SLD + ADHD comorbidity** | SLD + ADHD criteria met | §6.2/§6.3 + B1-08 co-management |

### Boundary rules
- Do NOT diagnose SLD at B1-12 — screening only; diagnosis requires §7.3 formal testing.
- Academic underperformance without skill-specific deficit = not SLD; investigate ADHD, emotional, environmental causes.
- Letter reversal is developmentally normal until age 7 — do not screen for dyslexia based on reversal alone in < 7 years.
- Language-of-instruction mismatch (child's home language ≠ school language) can mimic SLD — assess in child's dominant language.
- Intellectual disability (IQ < 70) is a different diagnosis from SLD (IQ ≥ average with specific skill deficit) — B1-23 differentiates.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (at-risk — accommodations + monitoring)
- Classroom accommodations (communicated via school letter): preferential seating, simplified instructions, extra time for tests, reduced homework volume, multisensory teaching.
- Parent guidance: structured homework routine, positive reinforcement for effort, avoid punishment for poor grades.
- Monitor: reassess in 1 term (3–4 months); if improving → continue; if not → §6.2.

### 6.2 Standard tier (probable SLD — accommodations + formal testing)
- All §6.1 accommodations.
- §7.3 referral for formal psychoeducational assessment (WISC/MISIC + achievement testing).
- Remedial-education referral: connect family with certified remedial educator or learning-centre.
- B1-08 co-management if ADHD comorbidity (ADHD pharmacotherapy often improves academic performance even without direct SLD remediation).

### 6.3 Intensive tier (confirmed SLD — formal support)
- All §6.2 interventions.
- RPWD Act documentation: B1-12 provides supporting clinical documentation; formal SLD diagnosis from §7.3 psychologist; family applies for disability certification (SLD is a recognised disability under RPWD 2016).
- Board-examination accommodations: extra time (typically 20 minutes/hour), scribe provision, exemption from specific subjects — coordinated with school board per RPWD provisions.
- Ongoing remedial education: minimum 1 year; structured programme targeting specific deficit areas (Orton-Gillingham for dyslexia; multisensory maths instruction for dyscalculia).
- Psychosocial support: B1-08 cross-care if self-esteem, anxiety, or school avoidance significant.

### 6.4 Contraindications

| Intervention | Not recommended |
|---|---|
| Vision therapy / coloured lenses for SLD | No evidence; AAP position [AAP-LearningDisability-2014] |
| Grade retention ("repeating a year") | Evidence does not support; accommodations are more effective |
| Withholding play / physical activity as punishment for poor academics | Counterproductive; damages motivation and well-being |

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral: None specific to B1-12.
### 7.2 Urgent referral: None specific to B1-12.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)
- [ ] **Probable SLD on B1-12 screening** → Clinical psychologist / educational psychologist for formal psychoeducational assessment (WISC/MISIC + achievement testing).
- [ ] **Suspected intellectual disability** (global academic difficulty + developmental delay) → B1-23 for formal developmental assessment.
- [ ] **ADHD criteria met on Vanderbilt** → B1-08 for ADHD management.
- [ ] **Significant emotional distress related to learning difficulty** (school refusal, anxiety, depression) → B1-08.
- [ ] **Hearing / vision not previously assessed** → B1-02 / B1-01.

### 7.4 Handoff communication
Standard: academic history, report cards, teacher questionnaire, in-clinic reading/writing/maths assessment, Vanderbilt scores (B1-08 cross), hearing/vision status.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

> "[Arjun]'s difficulty with reading is not about intelligence — he is a smart child. His brain processes written words differently, which makes reading harder than it should be. This is called a specific learning disability, and it is very common — about 5–10% of children have it. With the right support — specific teaching methods, extra time, and sometimes technology — most children with learning disabilities do very well. We need to do a formal assessment to confirm the diagnosis and then set up the right support."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities
- *"He's just lazy / not trying hard enough."* This is the most damaging misconception. SLD children often work harder than peers for poorer results. Frame as a brain-wiring difference, not a character flaw.
- *Academic pressure in Indian families.* High academic expectations are culturally deep. Counsel: the goal is for the child to learn to their potential, not to match a class rank. Accommodations level the playing field — they are not "cheating."
- *Stigma of "disability" label.* Many Indian families resist the RPWD pathway because "disability" carries stigma. Frame practically: the certification provides legal rights to accommodations (extra time, scribe) that make a concrete difference in board exams.
- *Tuition mills.* Many families respond to learning difficulty by adding more tuition (private coaching). Generic tuition does not help SLD — specific remedial education targeting the deficit does. Counsel families to invest in remedial education, not more of the same instruction.
- *"Will he ever be normal?"* Many children with SLD develop strong compensation strategies and succeed academically and professionally. Early identification and support are the key variables.
