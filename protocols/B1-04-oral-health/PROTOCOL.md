# SKIDS Oral Health Clinic

> The SKIDS Oral Health Clinic delivers caries-risk assessment using
> AAPD tools, fluoride varnish application, dietary sugar counselling
> for caries prevention, early childhood caries management, oral
> hygiene training, malocclusion screening, dental-trauma triage,
> and pedodontist Tier 3 partner routing for restoration,
> orthodontics, and surgical dental procedures. Completes the
> head-and-neck screening domain alongside B1-01, B1-02, B1-03.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Performs caries-risk assessment using AAPD Caries-Risk Assessment Tool (CAT) at every B1-04 visit and at A1 periodic visits where the SKIDS pediatrician performs the oral screening domain [AAPD-Caries-2023].
- Applies fluoride varnish (5% NaF, 22,600 ppm) from first tooth eruption, 2–4 times per year based on caries-risk category [AAPD-Fluoride-2023].
- Counsels on dietary sugar for caries prevention: sugar-sweetened beverage elimination, juice limitation (≤ 120 mL/day for 1–3 years; ≤ 180 mL/day for 4–6 years; ≤ 240 mL/day for 7–18 years per AAP), bedtime bottle elimination, frequency of sugar exposures per day [AAPD-Caries-2023].
- Trains parents and children on age-appropriate oral hygiene: supervised brushing with fluoridated toothpaste (rice-grain smear < 3 years; pea-size ≥ 3 years), flossing from when teeth are in contact, brush replacement every 3 months.
- Manages early enamel caries (white-spot lesions) with fluoride varnish + dietary modification + enhanced oral hygiene — before cavitation.
- Screens for malocclusion (crossbite, open bite, crowding, overjet), thumb-sucking habit persistence, and routes to pedodontist for orthodontic evaluation when indicated.
- Triages dental trauma (avulsion, intrusion, subluxation, fracture) and routes to pedodontist / ER as appropriate per IADT guidelines.
- Manages eruption concerns: teething pain (non-pharmacological + paracetamol if needed; NOT topical benzocaine — FDA warning), natal/neonatal teeth assessment, eruption cysts (reassurance), delayed eruption (screen for systemic cause).
- Screens for oral mucosal lesions: aphthous ulcers (manage), herpetic gingivostomatitis (manage), oral thrush (manage with nystatin), geographic tongue (reassure), mucocele (route pedodontist), ranula (route ENT/oral surgery).
- Cross-cares with B1-09 Nutrition & Growth for dietary sugar impact on caries and with B1-10 Obesity for SSB-caries-obesity intersection.
- Manages dental anxiety with age-appropriate behavioural guidance; routes severe dental phobia to B1-08 cross-care.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform dental restoration (fillings, crowns, pulpotomy/pulpectomy) — §7.3 to pedodontist partner.
- Perform dental extraction — §7.3 to pedodontist partner (or ER/oral surgery for complicated extraction).
- Provide orthodontic treatment — §7.3 to pedodontist/orthodontist partner.
- Perform dental X-rays (periapical, OPG) — routes to pedodontist or dental radiology centre.
- Manage dental abscess with facial cellulitis — §7.1 ER + pedodontist/oral surgery.
- Manage Ludwig's angina, deep fascial space infection — §7.1 ER.
- Manage complex dental trauma (avulsion of permanent tooth, root fracture, alveolar fracture) beyond initial stabilisation — §7.1 to pedodontist/ER.

**Age range covered:** 0 – 18 years (from birth for natal-tooth assessment; from first eruption for caries prevention).

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 oral screening at periodic visits; fluoride varnish at periodic visits (2×/year); oral hygiene guidance.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — full caries-risk assessment; fluoride varnish 4×/year for high-risk children; dietary-sugar counselling programme; malocclusion screening; pedodontist referral coordination.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited B1-04 visits; intensive caries-management programme for high-risk children; multi-protocol cross-care (B1-04 + B1-09 + B1-10); priority pedodontist scheduling.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1. Every SKIDS pediatrician performs oral screening and fluoride varnish application under this protocol. The pedodontist partner (Tier 3) provides restoration, orthodontics, surgical dental, and complex dental-trauma management. Protocol governance with named pedodontist. B1-04 does not require a dentist on-site at SKIDS — the pediatrician delivers preventive oral health; the pedodontist partner delivers restorative and surgical care at the partner clinic.

**Departures from cited guidelines:**

1. *Fluoride varnish by pediatrician, not dentist.* AAPD and AAP both endorse fluoride varnish application by primary-care physicians. In India, this is less common — many centres reserve fluoride for dental clinics. SKIDS pediatricians apply fluoride varnish at A1 periodic visits and B1-04 visits per AAP/AAPD guidance. This is a deliberate preventive-medicine expansion.

2. *No dental X-rays at SKIDS.* SKIDS clinics do not stock dental X-ray equipment. Clinical visual inspection and AAPD CAT drive the caries-risk assessment. Dental X-rays are obtained at the pedodontist partner clinic when restoration is anticipated. This is a logistics departure.

3. *Fluoride supplementation not routine.* India has variable community water fluoridation. SKIDS does NOT prescribe systemic fluoride supplements (fluoride drops/tablets) due to fluorosis risk in areas with adequate or excessive water fluoride. Topical fluoride (varnish + toothpaste) is the sole fluoride delivery pathway [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm SKIDS network position by region].

| "She fell and knocked a tooth." | Dental trauma. | §4.5 trauma triage |

- A1 Yellow (early enamel caries, no cavitation) = A1 fluoride varnish + enhanced oral hygiene; B1-04 if persistent.

- B1-09 cross: dietary-sugar counselling synchronised with B1-04 caries management.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Visual caries inspection

- Use headlight or dental light; gauze for moisture control.
- Inspect all erupted tooth surfaces systematically: occlusal, buccal, lingual, interproximal (where visible).
- Classify findings:
  - **Sound:** no visible caries.
  - **White-spot lesion (WSL):** chalky-white opacity on enamel, no cavitation = early enamel caries.
  - **Cavitated lesion:** break in enamel surface with dentin exposure.
  - **Extensive caries:** multiple teeth with cavitation; tooth destruction approaching pulp.
  - **Dental abscess:** localised swelling, fluctuance, sinus tract adjacent to carious tooth.
  - **Facial cellulitis:** diffuse facial/periorbital swelling of dental origin → §7.1.

### 4.2 AAPD Caries-Risk Assessment Tool (CAT)

| Risk factor | Low risk | Moderate risk | High risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Caries experience (child) | No caries (including restored) | 1–2 carious/restored teeth | ≥ 3 carious/restored teeth or active white-spot lesions |
| Caries experience (parent/sibling) | No active caries | Active caries | Active caries + untreated |
| Dietary sugar | Low sugar frequency (≤ 3 exposures/day) | Moderate (4–5/day) | High (≥ 6/day or bedtime bottle with sugar) |
| Fluoride exposure | Fluoridated water + fluoride toothpaste | Fluoride toothpaste only | No fluoride exposure |
| Special healthcare needs | None | — | Developmental delay, chronic illness affecting oral hygiene |
| Visible plaque | None | Mild | Heavy plaque / gingivitis |

**Overall classification:** highest-risk factor in any category determines overall risk level.

### 4.3 Gingival assessment

- Visual: colour (pink = healthy; red/swollen = gingivitis), bleeding on gentle probing (disposable periodontal probe or wooden stick), recession.
- Gingivitis in children: usually plaque-induced; managed with oral hygiene; NOT an indication for antibiotics.
- Desquamative gingivitis, gingival hyperplasia (drug-induced — phenytoin, ciclosporin), necrotising gingivitis → §7.3 pedodontist.

### 4.4 Eruption assessment

**Primary dentition timeline (approximate):**
- 6–10 months: lower central incisors.
- 8–12 months: upper central incisors.
- 16–23 months: canines.
- 23–33 months: second molars. Full primary dentition (20 teeth) by ~33 months.

**Permanent dentition:**
- 6 years: first permanent molars ("six-year molars") — most important teeth for B1-04 to protect.
- 6–7 years: lower central incisors.
- 11–13 years: premolars, canines, second molars.
- 17–25 years: third molars.

**Delayed eruption (≥ 6 months beyond expected):** screen for hypothyroidism (TSH), nutritional deficiency, cleidocranial dysplasia, ectodermal dysplasia → §7.3 if systemic cause suspected.

**Natal/neonatal teeth:** assess mobility; if extremely mobile with aspiration risk → pedodontist for extraction consideration.

### 4.5 Dental-trauma triage

Per IADT-2020 guidelines:

| Injury | Primary tooth | Permanent tooth |
|---|---|---|
| **Concussion / subluxation** | Observe; soft diet 2 weeks; follow-up 1 week | Observe; soft diet 2 weeks; follow-up at 1 week, 4 weeks, 6 months |
| **Lateral luxation / extrusion < 3 mm** | Observe; reposition if occlusion interference | §7.1 → pedodontist for splinting within 24h |
| **Intrusion (primary)** | Observe if no damage to permanent germ; monitor for re-eruption | §7.1 → pedodontist within 24h |
| **Avulsion (primary)** | Do NOT replant | — |
| **Avulsion (permanent)** | — | §7.1 EMERGENCY: replant immediately if possible; store in milk/saliva if cannot replant; pedodontist within 30–60 minutes |
| **Enamel fracture** | Smooth edges; observe | Smooth edges; observe; dental follow-up 2 weeks |
| **Enamel-dentin fracture** | Pedodontist within 24h | §7.2 → pedodontist within 24h for bonding/restoration |
| **Enamel-dentin-pulp fracture** | Extract or pulpotomy (pedodontist) | §7.1 → pedodontist ASAP for vital pulp therapy |
| **Root fracture** | Usually extract (pedodontist) | §7.1 → pedodontist for splinting |
| **Alveolar fracture** | §7.1 → oral surgery / ER | §7.1 → oral surgery / ER |

**First-aid for avulsed permanent tooth:**
1. Handle by crown only, NOT root.
2. If dirty: rinse briefly under cold running water or with saline (do NOT scrub the root surface; do NOT soak in water or antiseptic) [IADT-2020].
3. Replant into socket if possible; have child bite on gauze.
4. If cannot replant: store in cold milk, saliva (in cheek), or Hank's Balanced Salt Solution.
5. Time is critical: best outcomes within 30 minutes; poor outcomes after 60 minutes of DRY extra-alveolar time (a tooth kept moist in cold milk or saliva remains viable longer — replantation is still worthwhile if the tooth has been kept moist, even beyond 60 minutes total time out of socket).
6. → §7.1 pedodontist IMMEDIATELY.

### 4.6 Malocclusion and habit screening

- **Crossbite** (anterior or posterior): identify; route to pedodontist for orthodontic evaluation if permanent dentition.
- **Open bite / overjet:** if > 4 mm or associated with thumb-sucking → habit-cessation counselling + pedodontist evaluation if persistent after habit cessation.
- **Crowding:** identify; route at mixed-dentition stage (~8–10 years) for orthodontic assessment.
- **Thumb-sucking / pacifier:** normal < 4 years. Persistent ≥ 4 years → habit-cessation programme (§6.4); route to pedodontist if dental effects (open bite, crossbite).
- **Tongue thrust:** identify; route to pedodontist if persistent with dental effects; B1-11 Speech cross if speech impact.

### 4.7 Oral mucosal assessment

| Finding | Action |
|---|---|
| Aphthous ulcers (< 3, < 1 cm, self-limiting) | §6.5 topical management |
| Recurrent aphthae (> 6 episodes/year) | Screen for nutritional deficiency (B12, iron, folate — cross B1-09), coeliac disease, Behçet disease → §7.3 if systemic |
| Herpetic gingivostomatitis | §6.5 supportive + antiviral if < 72h onset |
| Oral thrush (candidiasis) | §6.5 nystatin |
| Geographic tongue | Reassure; benign; no treatment |
| Mucocele / ranula | §7.3 pedodontist / oral surgery |
| Oral ulcer > 2 weeks not healing | §7.2 for biopsy (malignancy screen) |
| Gingival hyperplasia (drug-induced) | Review medication; §7.3 pedodontist |

### 4.8 Decision tree

```
Child presents with oral / dental concern
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

### 5.1 Caries risk (AAPD CAT)

| Risk level | Criteria | Intervention tier | Fluoride frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low risk** | No caries experience; low dietary sugar; fluoride toothpaste; no plaque | §6.1 — standard prevention | 2×/year fluoride varnish |
| **Moderate risk** | 1–2 carious/restored teeth; moderate dietary sugar; mild plaque | §6.2 — enhanced prevention | 3×/year fluoride varnish |
| **High risk** | ≥ 3 carious/restored teeth OR active WSL OR bedtime bottle with sugar OR special healthcare needs OR heavy plaque | §6.3 — intensive prevention + §7.3 pedodontist for restoration | 4×/year fluoride varnish |

### 5.2 Caries disease severity

| Severity | Findings | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-cavitation (WSL)** | White-spot lesions; enamel intact | §6.2 — fluoride varnish + remineralisation + dietary mod. Reversible. |
| **Cavitated (limited)** | 1–2 teeth with cavitation; no pulp involvement | §7.3 pedodontist for restoration + §6.2/§6.3 prevention |
| **Cavitated (extensive / ECC)** | Multiple teeth with cavitation; pulp involvement; early childhood caries pattern | §7.3 pedodontist (may require GA for restoration); §6.3 intensive prevention |
| **Dental abscess** | Localised swelling, sinus tract, fluctuance | §7.2 pedodontist within 7 days + oral antibiotic (§6.3) |
| **Facial cellulitis** | Diffuse facial/periorbital swelling of dental origin | §7.1 ER + IV antibiotics + pedodontist/oral surgery |

### 5.3 Dental trauma severity

Per §4.5 triage table — classified by IADT injury type and tooth type (primary vs. permanent).

### 5.4 Boundary rules

- White-spot lesions = reversible with fluoride + dietary change. Do NOT refer to pedodontist unless cavitation develops.
- Cavitation = beyond B1-04 Tier 1 management for restoration; always refer pedodontist. B1-04 continues preventive management alongside.
- Dental abscess without systemic signs = §7.2 (urgent). Dental abscess with facial cellulitis or systemic signs (fever, malaise) = §7.1 (emergency).
- Avulsion of permanent tooth = absolute time-critical §7.1 emergency (within 30–60 minutes).
- Malocclusion screening at B1-04; orthodontic treatment at pedodontist — B1-04 does not prescribe appliances.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (low caries risk — standard prevention)

- **Fluoride varnish** 2×/year at A1 periodic visits or dedicated B1-04 visits.
  - Application: dry teeth with gauze; paint 5% NaF varnish on all tooth surfaces with microbrush; thin coat is sufficient.
  - Instructions: do not eat/drink for 30 minutes; do not brush until next day.
  - Safe from first tooth eruption; no age minimum.
- **Oral hygiene guidance:**
  - 0–3 years: parent-supervised brushing 2×/day with fluoridated toothpaste (rice-grain smear); soft infant toothbrush.
  - 3–6 years: parent-supervised brushing 2×/day with fluoridated toothpaste (pea-size); child-size toothbrush.
  - ≥ 6 years: child brushes independently 2×/day; parent checks; flossing when interproximal contacts present.
- **Dietary guidance:** limit sugar to mealtimes; no sugary drinks between meals; no bedtime bottle with milk/juice after 12 months; water as default drink.

### 6.2 Standard tier (moderate risk / WSL — enhanced prevention)

- **Fluoride varnish** 3×/year.
- **Dietary sugar audit** with SKIDS dietitian (cross B1-09/B1-10): identify and reduce sugar exposures per day; target ≤ 3 exposures/day.
- **Remineralisation protocol** for WSL: fluoride varnish + casein phosphopeptide-amorphous calcium phosphate (CPP-ACP, e.g., GC Tooth Mousse) applied at home after brushing × 3 months [NEEDS EXPANSION IN EDITORIAL PASS — confirm CPP-ACP product availability in India and SKIDS dispensary].
- **Enhanced oral hygiene:** re-demonstrate brushing technique; consider electric toothbrush for children with poor manual dexterity.

### 6.3 Intensive tier (high risk / ECC / dental abscess)

- **Fluoride varnish** 4×/year.
- **Intensive dietary modification:** mandatory B1-09/B1-10 dietitian cross-care; SSB elimination; ultra-processed snack reduction.
- **Dental abscess antibiotic:** amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day PO in 3 divided doses × 7 days (OR amoxicillin-clavulanate if mixed flora suspected). NOT a substitute for definitive dental treatment — pedodontist referral is mandatory alongside.
- **Referral to pedodontist for restoration.** B1-04 provides the preventive layer; pedodontist provides restorative.
- **Re-evaluation at 4 weeks** post-pedodontist treatment: confirm caries-risk modification strategies are in place.

### 6.4 Habit-cessation programme (thumb-sucking / pacifier ≥ 4 years)

- Positive reinforcement (praise, sticker chart, non-food reward for habit-free days).
- Awareness device (adhesive bandage on thumb as reminder, NOT punishment).
- Identify triggers (boredom, anxiety, tiredness) and offer substitutes.
- If persistent ≥ 6 months of behavioural approach: pedodontist for habit-breaking appliance consideration.
- If dental effects already present (open bite, crossbite): pedodontist for orthodontic evaluation alongside habit cessation.

### 6.5 Oral mucosal management

**Aphthous ulcers (minor):**
- Topical triamcinolone 0.1% in orabase paste, apply to ulcer TDS × 5 days.
- Oral rinse: chlorhexidine 0.12% or benzydamine mouthwash for pain relief.
- Dietary: avoid acidic/spicy foods during episode.

**Herpetic gingivostomatitis:**
- Supportive: cold fluids, popsicles, soft diet; paracetamol for pain.
- Aciclovir 20 mg/kg/dose (max 200 mg) PO 5×/day × 5 days — if presenting < 72h of onset and moderate-severe; otherwise supportive only.
- Hydration assessment: if unable to swallow → §7.2 for IV hydration.

**Oral thrush (candidiasis):**
- Nystatin 100,000 units/mL oral suspension: 1 mL to each side of mouth QDS × 7–14 days.
- Clean pacifiers/bottle nipples; breastfeeding mothers — treat nipples if symptomatic.
- If not responding to nystatin: oral fluconazole 3 mg/kg day 1, then 3 mg/kg/day × 13 days (rare in immunocompetent).
- Recurrent thrush in child > 6 months → screen for immunodeficiency; §7.3 if confirmed.

### 6.6 Dental-anxiety management

- Tell-Show-Do technique for children ≥ 3 years.
- Distraction (tablet/phone video during fluoride application).
- Gradual desensitisation (first visit = look only; second visit = count teeth; third visit = fluoride).
- Positive framing: "tooth paint" instead of "fluoride application."
- If severe phobia preventing any oral examination → B1-08 cross-care for anxiety management.

### 6.7 Dispensary stock

- 5% NaF fluoride varnish (e.g., Duraphat or equivalent; single-dose units).
- Microbrushes for varnish application.
- Toothbrushes (infant, child, adolescent sizes) for in-clinic demonstration.
- Fluoridated toothpaste samples (1000–1450 ppm fluoride).
- CPP-ACP paste (GC Tooth Mousse or equivalent) [NEEDS EXPANSION — confirm stocking].
- Amoxicillin suspension and capsules (shared with B1-03).
- Nystatin oral suspension.
- Aciclovir tablets/suspension.
- Triamcinolone 0.1% orabase paste.
- Chlorhexidine 0.12% mouthwash.

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral (same day)

- [ ] **Facial cellulitis of dental origin** — diffuse facial or periorbital swelling, fever, toxic appearance. → ER + pedodontist/oral surgery.
- [ ] **Ludwig's angina** — bilateral submandibular swelling, floor-of-mouth elevation, airway compromise. → ER immediately.
- [ ] **Avulsion of permanent tooth** — time-critical. → Pedodontist within 30–60 minutes; replant if possible en route.
- [ ] **Dental trauma with alveolar fracture** → ER + pedodontist/oral surgery.
- [ ] **Uncontrolled post-extraction bleeding** (from pedodontist procedure) → ER.
- [ ] **Enamel-dentin-pulp fracture (permanent tooth)** → Pedodontist ASAP for vital pulp therapy.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)

- [ ] **Dental abscess without systemic signs** — localised swelling, sinus tract. → Pedodontist for definitive treatment + oral antibiotic started at B1-04.
- [ ] **Enamel-dentin fracture (permanent tooth)** → Pedodontist for bonding/restoration.
- [ ] **Lateral luxation / extrusion of permanent tooth** → Pedodontist for splinting within 24h.
- [ ] **Herpetic gingivostomatitis with dehydration risk** (unable to swallow) → Pediatric ER/daycare for IV hydration.
- [ ] **Oral ulcer > 2 weeks not healing** → Pedodontist / oral surgery for biopsy (malignancy screen).

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)

- [ ] **Cavitated caries (any number)** → Pedodontist for restoration (B1-04 continues preventive management).
- [ ] **Early childhood caries (ECC) — extensive pattern** → Pedodontist (may require GA for restoration).
- [ ] **Malocclusion requiring orthodontic evaluation** (crossbite, overjet > 4 mm, severe crowding) → Pedodontist/orthodontist.
- [ ] **Persistent thumb-sucking ≥ 4 years with dental effects not responding to 6 months behavioural approach** → Pedodontist for habit-breaking appliance.
- [ ] **Mucocele / ranula** → Pedodontist / oral surgery.
- [ ] **Gingival hyperplasia (drug-induced)** → Pedodontist + medication-prescribing physician.
- [ ] **Delayed eruption with suspected systemic cause** → B2-01 Endocrine / genetics.
- [ ] **Natal/neonatal teeth with aspiration risk (extremely mobile)** → Pedodontist for extraction.
- [ ] **Recurrent oral thrush in child > 6 months** → Pediatric immunology for immunodeficiency screen.

### 7.4 Handoff communication

Standard packet:
1. Visual caries inspection findings (tooth chart with caries mapping).
2. AAPD CAT risk classification.
3. Dietary sugar assessment summary (from dietitian).
4. Fluoride varnish history (dates, frequency).
5. Dental trauma mechanism, time, first-aid given (if applicable).
6. Companion-generated referral summary.

## §8 Follow-up schedule

| Scenario | First follow-up | Subsequent |
|---|---|---|
| Low caries risk | Next A1 periodic visit (fluoride varnish 2×/year) | Per A1 cadence |
| Moderate caries risk | 4 months (fluoride varnish + WSL reassessment) | 4-monthly (3×/year) |
| High caries risk | 3 months | 3-monthly (4×/year) until risk downgrades |
| Post-pedodontist restoration | 4 weeks (confirm prevention in place) | Per caries-risk schedule |
| Dental trauma (minor, observed) | 1 week | 4 weeks; 3 months; 6 months (pulp vitality monitoring) |
| Habit-cessation programme | Monthly × 3 months | 3-monthly until cessation |
| Aphthous / herpetic / thrush | 1–2 weeks (resolution check) | As needed |

§8.2 What to recheck: caries status (every visit); CAT risk reassessment (annually); oral hygiene quality; dietary sugar frequency; fluoride varnish adherence; malocclusion progression.

§8.3 Success: caries-free status maintained or achieved; WSL remineralised; caries risk downgraded from high to moderate/low; habit cessation achieved; dietary sugar reduced to ≤ 3 exposures/day.

§8.7 Loss-to-follow-up: Companion alert to manager + parent for high-risk children who miss scheduled fluoride-varnish visits. Dental caries is the most prevalent chronic disease in children — prevention requires programme adherence.

## §9 Companion software workflow

### 9.1 Doctor Module screens

**Screen 1: Pre-consult summary** — A1 oral flag; prior caries-risk level; caries history (tooth chart); fluoride varnish dates; pedodontist referral status; dietary-sugar assessment (B1-09/B1-10 cross).

**Screen 2: Visual inspection** — interactive tooth chart (primary and permanent dentition selectable); mark each tooth: sound / WSL / cavitated / restored / missing / abscess. Auto-generates caries count.

**Screen 3: AAPD CAT** — structured risk-factor form; auto-calculated overall risk level.

**Screen 4: Examination** — gingival status; eruption status; malocclusion screening; mucosal findings; habit screening.

**Screen 5: Severity grading** — auto-populated from caries count + risk level.

**Screen 6: Therapeutic plan** — fluoride varnish application log; oral hygiene instruction checkbox; dietary counselling referral; pedodontist referral workflow; prescription (antibiotic for abscess, nystatin for thrush, etc.).

**Screen 7: §7 review** — referral trigger checklist; dental-trauma triage (if applicable).

**Screen 8: Follow-up** — auto-suggests per risk-based schedule; next fluoride varnish date.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

Fluoride varnish:

> "I'm going to paint a protective coating on [Aanya]'s teeth. It's called fluoride varnish — it strengthens the tooth enamel and helps prevent cavities. It takes 30 seconds, tastes a bit sticky, and [she] can eat and drink normally in half an hour. We do this [2–4 times a year] depending on [her] cavity risk."

White-spot lesion:

> "I can see some white spots on [Arjun]'s teeth. These are very early signs of cavity formation, but they haven't turned into actual cavities yet. The good news is they're reversible. We're going to apply fluoride, adjust the diet to reduce sugar frequency, and improve brushing. If we do this consistently, these spots can harden back up and [he] won't need fillings."

Cavitated caries:

> "[Aanya] has [number] teeth with actual cavities — holes in the enamel that have gone through to the softer layer underneath. These need to be fixed by a children's dentist. I'm referring [her] to our pedodontist partner. In the meantime, we'll strengthen the other teeth with fluoride and work on reducing the sugar in [her] diet so we don't get more cavities."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities

- *"Baby teeth don't matter — they'll fall out."* Address directly: primary teeth hold space for permanent teeth; untreated cavities cause pain, infection, and damage to developing permanent teeth underneath. Early caries also establishes the bacterial environment for permanent-tooth caries.
- *Sugar in Indian diets.* Traditional Indian sweets (mithai), jaggery, sugarcane juice, and chai with sugar are culturally significant. Do not ban; reduce frequency. Frame as: "The issue is how often [he] eats sweet things, not whether [he] ever eats them. If sweets are at mealtimes only, not as snacks, the teeth have time to recover between exposures."
- *Bottle at bedtime.* Common practice in Indian households. Counsel: after 12 months, bedtime bottle should contain water only. Milk or juice in a bedtime bottle bathes the teeth in sugar overnight — this is the single biggest risk factor for early childhood caries.
- *Fluoride fears.* Some parents fear fluoride (internet misinformation). Address: fluoride varnish is a topical application — a thin coat on the teeth, not swallowed. The amount of fluoride is less than what's in a cup of tea. The evidence for cavity prevention is very strong.
- *"Dentist is for when it hurts."* Reframe: the goal of B1-04 is to prevent pain. Regular fluoride and dietary management mean [child] is less likely to ever need a filling or an extraction.
- *Cost of pedodontist treatment.* Restorative dental work is expensive. Frame prevention as the cost-effective approach: "Fluoride and diet changes cost very little; fillings and crowns cost a lot more. We want to keep [her] teeth strong enough that she never needs them."

## §11 Manager handoff

§11.1 Manager Console: caries-risk level; caries count; fluoride varnish schedule adherence; pedodontist referral status; dietary-sugar assessment status.

§11.2 Tiering: fluoride varnish 2×/year at A1 = Basic; enhanced prevention + dietary counselling = Advanced; intensive prevention + multi-protocol cross-care = Premium.

§11.3 Top parent objections:

| Objection | Manager response |
|---|---|
| "Baby teeth will fall out — no need to worry." | "Baby teeth keep the space for adult teeth. Infections in baby teeth can damage the adult teeth growing underneath. Prevention now saves pain and cost later." |
| "Fluoride is harmful." | "The amount in the varnish is tiny — less than in a cup of tea. It's painted on, not swallowed. It's the single most effective way to prevent cavities." |
| "We brush every day — he shouldn't have cavities." | "Brushing is important, but frequency of sugar in the diet matters just as much. The dietitian can help identify hidden sugar sources. It's not about brushing harder — it's about reducing how often the teeth are exposed to sugar." |
| "Can't afford the dentist." | "That's exactly why prevention matters — fluoride and dietary changes are much less expensive than fillings. If [child] does need the dentist, we coordinate referral to our partner at the best available rate." |
| "He screams at the dentist." | "Many children are nervous. We start gently — just looking at the teeth first. The fluoride application is quick and painless. Over a few visits, most children become comfortable." |

## §12 Quality and audit

§12.1 Metrics:
- ≥ 90% of enrolled children receive age-appropriate fluoride varnish at each scheduled visit.
- Caries risk downgrade: ≥ 30% of high-risk children downgrade to moderate/low within 12 months.
- WSL remineralisation rate: ≥ 50% of WSL resolved at 6-month follow-up with fluoride + dietary intervention.
- Cavitated caries identified at B1-04 → 100% referred to pedodontist within 4 weeks.
- Dental trauma: 100% of permanent-tooth avulsions have documented replantation attempt or storage in appropriate medium within 60 minutes.
- Dietary sugar audit: ≥ 80% of moderate/high-risk children have dietitian sugar assessment completed.
- ≥ 95% of §7 triggers result in documented referral with complete handoff packet.
- Fluoride-varnish refusal rate: < 5% (quality target — measure engagement effectiveness).

§12.2 Quarterly: protocol owner reviews caries-risk distribution across B1-04 population, fluoride varnish utilisation rate, pedodontist referral completion, dental trauma outcomes, and a random sample of 30 B1-04 visits.

§12.3 Certification: every SKIDS pediatrician delivering B1-04 must complete fluoride-varnish application training (2 hours practical); AAPD CAT training (1 hour); dental-trauma first-aid training (2 hours); annual update (1 hour). Certification includes supervised varnish application on 10 patients.

§12.4 Fellowship: no SKIDS Fellowship pathway for oral health. Protocol governance sits with the named pedodontist partner. SKIDS pediatricians deliver prevention; pedodontist delivers treatment.
