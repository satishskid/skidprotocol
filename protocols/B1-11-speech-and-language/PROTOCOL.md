# SKIDS Speech & Language Clinic

> The SKIDS Speech & Language Clinic delivers structured speech-
> language assessment using REELS-3 and clinical milestones,
> hearing-first workup for language delay (mandatory B1-02 cross),
> SLP-led therapy initiation for articulation, expressive-language,
> receptive-language, and fluency disorders, and coordination with
> B1-23 Developmental Surveillance for broader developmental
> concerns. Oral-motor / swallowing disorders and voice disorders
> route per §7.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Assesses speech and language development across receptive, expressive, pragmatic, and articulatory domains using REELS-3 (Receptive-Expressive Emergent Language Scale, 3rd edition) and clinical milestone evaluation [AAP-DevScreen-2020].
- Mandates hearing assessment (B1-02 cross-care) before attributing language delay to a non-auditory cause — hearing loss is the most common treatable cause of language delay.
- Initiates speech-language therapy (SLP-led) for: expressive-language delay, receptive-language delay, mixed receptive-expressive delay, articulation disorder, phonological disorder, and childhood fluency disorder (stuttering).
- Differentiates developmental language delay from late-talker phenotype: late talkers (expressive delay with normal receptive, normal comprehension, normal play) are monitored; children with receptive delay or absent communicative intent are referred for B1-23/B1-08 assessment.
- Manages early stuttering (developmental dysfluency < 6 months duration in 2–5 years): parent counselling, environmental modification, Lidcombe or Palin PCI approach. Refers persistent stuttering (> 6 months, > 5 years, or with secondary behaviours) to §7.3.
- Screens for oral-motor dysfunction: drooling, feeding difficulty with oral-motor basis, tongue-tie impact on feeding/speech (cross B1-04 for tongue-tie assessment; pedodontist for frenotomy if indicated).
- Cross-cares with B1-23 Developmental Surveillance (language delay within broader developmental delay), B1-08 (language regression in autism, selective mutism), B1-02 (hearing-related language delay), and B1-04 (tongue thrust with speech impact).
- Provides parent-coaching in language-stimulation strategies (responsivity education, narrating, expanding, recasting) as a core component of therapy.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Perform formal audiological assessment — B1-02 performs hearing testing; B1-11 requires result before therapy initiation.
- Manage voice disorders (dysphonia, vocal-nodule management) — §7.3 to ENT + SLP specialist.
- Manage swallowing disorders (dysphagia, aspiration risk) — §7.3 to pediatric GI/ENT + SLP specialist.
- Perform formal autism diagnosis (language regression + social deficit → B1-08 + B1-23 + §7.3 for ADOS).
- Manage cleft-palate-related speech — §7.3 to cleft team.

**Age range covered:** 0 – 12 years (primary); 13–18 years for persistent speech-sound disorders, fluency disorders, and pragmatic-language concerns.

**SKIDS subscription tier(s):**
- **Basic ₹2,999** — A1 speech/language screening; first B1-11 visit on per-visit pricing.
- **Advanced ₹6,999** — up to 8 SLP sessions per year; structured speech-therapy programme; parent-coaching sessions.
- **Premium ₹12,999** — unlimited SLP sessions; multi-protocol cross-care (B1-11 + B1-02 + B1-23 + B1-08); priority specialist referral.

**SKIDS clinical tier:** Tier 1 with allied-staff dependency on the SKIDS speech-language pathologist (SLP). Without an SLP, B1-11 can screen and refer but cannot deliver therapy. Protocol governance with named SLP or DBP with speech expertise.

**Departures:**

1. *Hearing-first mandate.* Every child assessed at B1-11 must have hearing results from B1-02 before language delay is attributed to a non-auditory cause. Some centres initiate speech therapy without hearing assessment — SKIDS does not.

## §4 Diagnostic workflow

### 4.1 Hearing screen (mandatory first step)
- Confirm B1-02 hearing result available. If not → refer to B1-02 before proceeding with language-delay diagnosis.
- If hearing loss identified → B1-02 hearing management is primary; speech therapy is adjunctive to hearing intervention.

### 4.2 Speech-language history
1. Language milestones: cooing (3m), babbling (6m), first words (12m), two-word phrases (24m), sentences (36m).
2. Current language level: number of words, longest utterance, intelligibility to familiar/unfamiliar listeners.
3. Comprehension: follows commands? points to objects named? understands questions?
4. Communication intent: gestures, pointing, eye contact, social referencing (absent intent raises autism concern → B1-08/B1-23).
5. Multilingual environment: languages spoken at home; which is dominant; sequential vs. simultaneous bilingualism.
6. Family history: language delay, learning disability, stuttering.
7. Screen time: excessive screen time (> 2h/day) is associated with expressive-language delay in toddlers; assess and counsel.

### 4.3 Assessment tools

| Tool | Age | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **REELS-3** | 0–36 months | Receptive + expressive language quotients |
| **PPVT (Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test)** or Indian adaptation | ≥ 2.5 years | Receptive vocabulary |
| **Clinical milestone evaluation** | All ages | Domain-specific milestone achievement |
| **Goldman-Fristoe Test of Articulation** (or equivalent) | ≥ 2 years | Articulation assessment |
| **Stuttering Severity Instrument (SSI-4)** | ≥ 2 years | Fluency disorder grading |
| **Pragmatic language checklist** | ≥ 3 years | Social-communication assessment (ASD screen) |

### 4.4 Oral-motor assessment
- Lip closure, tongue mobility, palatal movement, jaw stability, drooling.
- Feeding history: choking, gagging, texture avoidance (oral-motor vs. sensory).
- If significant oral-motor dysfunction → §7.3 for feeding-team assessment (SLP + OT + pediatric GI).

### 4.5 Decision tree

```
Child with speech / language concern
        │
        ├── Hearing screen (B1-02) → hearing loss → B1-02 primary
        │
        ├── Language regression → §7.2 → B1-23 + B1-08
        │
        ├── Late talker (expressive delay only, normal receptive,
        │   normal play, good communicative intent) → §6.1 monitor
        │
        ├── Expressive delay + receptive delay → §6.2 therapy + B1-23
        ├── Articulation disorder → §6.2 articulation therapy
        ├── Fluency disorder → §6.3 fluency programme
        ├── Absent communicative intent → B1-08/B1-23 (autism concern)
        │
        ▼
§5 severity → §6 SLP therapy + parent coaching
```

## §5 Severity grading [SACRED SECTION]

| Severity | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **At risk (late talker)** | Expressive delay (< 50 words at 24m OR no 2-word phrases at 24m); normal receptive; normal communicative intent | §6.1 — monitor + parent coaching |
| **At risk** | REELS quotient 78–84 (approximately −1 to −1.5 SD); isolated expressive or mild articulation delay; intelligible to familiar listeners | §6.1 — monitor + parent coaching; initiate therapy if no progress at 3 months |
| **Mild delay** | REELS quotient 70–77 (approximately −1.5 to −2 SD); isolated expressive or articulation delay; intelligible to familiar listeners | §6.2 — SLP therapy 1×/week |
| **Moderate delay** | REELS quotient 55–69 (approximately −2 to −3 SD); mixed receptive-expressive delay; reduced intelligibility | §6.2 — SLP therapy 2×/week + parent coaching |
| **Severe delay** | REELS quotient < 55 (> −3 SD below mean); minimal expressive output; poor comprehension; unintelligible | §6.3 — intensive SLP + B1-23 cross + §7.3 formal assessment |

### Boundary rules
- Late talker with normal receptive = monitor at B1-11; most catch up by 3 years. If not improved by 30 months → initiate therapy.
- Receptive delay at any age = always investigate (hearing first; then B1-23 for developmental assessment).
- Language regression = §7.2 (autism or neurological); never "wait and see."
- Absent communicative intent (no pointing, no gestures, no eye contact) = autism concern → B1-08/B1-23 regardless of other language level.
- Bilingual children: do NOT diagnose language delay based on dominant-language-only assessment; assess in both languages; sequential bilinguals may have a temporary silent period.

## §6 Therapeutic options

### 6.1 Light tier (at-risk / late talker)
- Parent-coaching: responsivity education (follow the child's lead, narrate activities, expand child's utterances, read daily, reduce screen time, increase conversational turns).
- Monitor at 3 months: if word count increasing and phrases emerging → continue monitoring. If not → §6.2.

### 6.2 Standard tier (SLP therapy programme)
- **Expressive-language therapy:** vocabulary-building, sentence-expansion, story-telling, play-based language stimulation. 1–2×/week SLP sessions + daily parent carryover.
- **Articulation therapy:** sound-specific drills, minimal-pair contrast therapy, phonological-process remediation. 1×/week SLP.
- **Mixed receptive-expressive:** SLP therapy targeting comprehension (following commands, vocabulary mapping) + expressive output. 2×/week.
- **Parent-coaching sessions:** monthly parent-SLP session to model techniques and review home carryover.

### 6.3 Intensive tier (severe delay / fluency)
- Intensive SLP: 2–3×/week + daily structured home practice.
- Fluency management: Lidcombe Programme (parent-delivered, SLP-supervised; for children < 6 years) or Palin PCI (Parent-Child Interaction therapy). For older children/adolescents: cognitive-behavioural fluency therapy.
- B1-23 cross-care for developmental assessment.
- §7.3 referral for formal developmental/psychometric assessment if global delay suspected.

### 6.4 Contraindications

| Intervention | Precaution |
|---|---|
| Speech therapy without hearing result | Do NOT initiate; B1-02 first |
| Tongue-tie division for speech (frenotomy) | Evidence is limited for speech improvement from frenotomy in children with normal feeding; only with SLP + pedodontist joint assessment |
| "Wait and see" for receptive delay | Do NOT wait — investigate |

## §7 Hard referral triggers [SACRED SECTION]

### 7.1 Emergency referral: None specific to B1-11.

### 7.2 Urgent referral (within 7 days)
- [ ] **Language regression** (loss of words at any age) → B1-23 + B1-08 + pediatric neurology.

### 7.3 Routine referral (within 4 weeks)
- [ ] **Severe speech-language delay with suspected global developmental delay** → B1-23 for formal assessment.
- [ ] **Absent communicative intent** (no pointing, no gestures, no eye contact) → B1-08/B1-23 for autism evaluation.
- [ ] **Persistent stuttering** (> 6 months, > 5 years, or with secondary behaviours: eye blinking, head jerks, avoidance) → specialist SLP fluency centre.
- [ ] **Voice disorder** (chronic hoarseness, breathy voice, stridor) → ENT for laryngoscopy.
- [ ] **Dysphagia / aspiration concern** → pediatric GI / ENT + SLP specialist.
- [ ] **Cleft-palate-related speech** → cleft team.
- [ ] **Oral-motor dysfunction significant** (drooling, feeding difficulty, texture refusal with choking) → feeding-therapy team (SLP + OT).

### 7.4 Handoff communication
Standard: REELS scores, speech-sound inventory, language sample, hearing results (B1-02), B1-23 developmental scores (if available), therapy history.

|---|---|---|
| Late talker (monitoring) | 3 months | 3-monthly until 3 years; initiate therapy if not catching up |
| SLP therapy (any) | Monthly SLP progress review | 3-monthly physician review |
| Fluency programme | 2 weeks (Lidcombe parent check) | Monthly × 6 months |

§8.3 Success: age-appropriate language milestones achieved; intelligibility improved to ≥ 75% by unfamiliar listeners by age 4; fluency within normal limits; therapy goals met.

## §10 Parent-facing communication

### 10.1 Scripted explanations

> "[Arjun] is taking longer than expected to start talking. His understanding seems [good / also delayed]. The first thing we need to check is his hearing — we want to make sure he hears clearly, because hearing is the foundation for speech. Once we have the hearing result, we'll start a therapy programme where a speech therapist works with [him] — and with you — to build his language skills. What you do at home every day matters more than what happens in the therapy room."

### 10.5 Cultural sensitivities
- *"Boys talk late."* While mild expressive-language delay is more common in boys, it does not mean it should be ignored. The assessment tells us whether to wait or act.
- *Bilingualism confusion.* "Is speaking two languages causing the delay?" — No. Bilingual children may have a temporary silent period but reach the same milestones. Assess in both languages; do NOT advise dropping a language.
- *Screen time as babysitter.* Many Indian toddlers have excessive screen time (> 3h/day). Screen time does not teach language — it replaces the conversational interaction that builds language. Counsel reduction without shaming.
- *Therapy stigma.* "Does he need a doctor for talking?" — Frame SLP as a teacher, not a doctor. The SLP teaches the child (and the family) techniques to build language, just like a tutor helps with maths.
