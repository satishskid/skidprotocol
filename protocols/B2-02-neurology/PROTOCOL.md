# SKIDS Neurology Clinic

> The SKIDS Neurology Clinic is a Tier 2 service requiring a
> Fellowship-credentialed pediatric neurologist. It manages
> epilepsy, headache disorders, neurodevelopmental regression
> workup, movement disorders, neuromuscular disease, and
> complex neurological presentations referred from B1-23
> Developmental Surveillance, B1-08 Behavioural (tic disorders),
> and A1 §7.2 neurological emergencies.

---

## §1 Service definition

**What this clinic does:**
- Manages epilepsy: seizure classification (ILAE 2017), EEG interpretation, anti-seizure medication (ASM) initiation and titration (levetiracetam, valproate, carbamazepine, oxcarbazepine, lamotrigine, ethosuximide per seizure type), drug-level monitoring, breakthrough-seizure management, driving/activity counselling for adolescents.
- Evaluates and manages headache disorders: migraine (with/without aura) per ICHD-3 criteria, tension-type headache, secondary headache (red flags → neuroimaging), cluster headache (rare in children). Migraine management: lifestyle triggers, acute treatment (ibuprofen, sumatriptan for ≥ 12y), prophylaxis (propranolol, amitriptyline, topiramate).
- Investigates developmental regression: metabolic screening (TMS, urine organic acids, lactate, ammonia), neuroimaging (MRI brain + MRS), EEG (Landau-Kleffner syndrome, epileptic encephalopathy), genetic testing (whole-exome sequencing where indicated). Receives from B1-23 §7.2 bridge.
- Manages neuromuscular disease: Duchenne/Becker muscular dystrophy (from B1-23 CK screening), spinal muscular atrophy, myasthenia gravis, hereditary neuropathies. Coordinates with multidisciplinary team (physiotherapy, respiratory, cardiology, orthopaedics).
- Manages movement disorders: tic disorders (chronic motor/vocal tics, Tourette syndrome — from B1-08 §7.3), chorea, dystonia, tremor.
- Evaluates and manages cerebral palsy: classification (GMFCS), spasticity management (physiotherapy, botulinum toxin, baclofen), comorbidity surveillance (epilepsy, feeding, hip surveillance, cognitive), orthopaedic coordination.
- Manages febrile seizures: counselling (simple febrile seizure = benign; no ASM needed), complex febrile seizure (prolonged, focal, recurrent in 24h → EEG + follow-up), status epilepticus in febrile seizure (→ §7.1 ER).
- Evaluates neonatal seizures: referred from NICU / neonatal unit; classification, EEG, metabolic workup, neuroimaging.

**What this clinic does not do:**
- Operate without a Fellowship-credentialed pediatric neurologist.
- Manage acute status epilepticus beyond recognition and benzodiazepine first-aid (→ §7.1 ER).
- Perform neurosurgery (epilepsy surgery, VP shunt → §7.3 neurosurgery).
- Manage acute stroke (→ §7.1 ER + interventional neurology).

**Tier 2 credential requirement:** Pediatric neurologist with DM Pediatric Neurology or equivalent Fellowship.

**When B2-02 is NOT available:** A1 / B1-23 §7 triggers route to named external pediatric neurologist partner (Tier 3).

## §3–§12 (Tier 2 structure)

**§3:** Receives from: A1 §7.1 (acute neurological event), A1 §7.2 (developmental regression), B1-23 §7.2/§7.3 (developmental regression, seizures, neuromuscular), B1-08 §7.3 (tic disorders, encephalopathy post-pertussis vaccine cross B1-22), B1-20 §7.3 (spinal dysraphism with enuresis).

**§4:** Neurological examination (cranial nerves, tone, power, reflexes, coordination, gait, fundoscopy), EEG ordering + interpretation, MRI brain ordering + interpretation, nerve-conduction studies, metabolic screening panels, genetic testing coordination.

**§5 [SACRED]:** Epilepsy severity by seizure frequency and ASM response (well-controlled = seizure-free ≥ 12 months on ASM; poorly controlled = ≥ 1 seizure/month despite 2 appropriate ASM = drug-resistant → §7.3 for epilepsy-surgery evaluation). Headache severity by frequency/disability (MIDAS score). CP severity by GMFCS level (I–V).

**§6:** ASM prescribing (weight-based, seizure-type-matched), migraine acute + prophylactic treatment, botulinum toxin for spasticity, GH-therapy coordination for DMD (cross B2-01), physiotherapy prescriptions, orthopaedic device prescriptions (AFOs, standing frames).

**§7 [SACRED]:**
- §7.1: Status epilepticus (→ ER); acute neurological deterioration with raised ICP (→ ER + neurosurgery); acute stroke (→ ER).
- §7.2: New-onset seizures with focal features or abnormal neuroimaging → tertiary neurology centre.
- §7.3: Drug-resistant epilepsy (≥ 2 appropriate ASM failed) → epilepsy-surgery centre evaluation; neurodegenerative disease confirmed → tertiary metabolic/genetics centre; DMD/SMA → neuromuscular centre; VP shunt malfunction → neurosurgery.

**§8:** Epilepsy: every 3 months (seizure diary + ASM levels); headache: every 3 months (headache diary); CP: every 6 months (GMFCS, hip surveillance, growth, feeding).

**§9:** Companion: seizure diary (parent-logged with video upload), ASM log with drug-level tracking, headache diary (MIDAS score auto-calculated), EEG report integration, MRI report integration.

**§10:** Epilepsy diagnosis: "Epilepsy means the brain has a tendency to produce seizures. It is one of the most common brain conditions in children and is very treatable. Most children with epilepsy live completely normal lives with the right medicine. The medicine prevents seizures — it does not cure the underlying tendency, which is why the course is usually 2 years seizure-free before we consider stopping." Headache: "Migraine in children is common and is not 'just a headache.' It is a brain condition that causes intense pain, often with nausea and sensitivity to light. We can treat the attacks and, if they happen often, prevent them." Cultural: epilepsy stigma in India is severe; frame as a medical condition, not supernatural; no activity restrictions (except unsupervised swimming and heights without barriers) once controlled.

**§11:** B2-02 is Premium tier. Manager coordinates: ASM refill adherence, EEG scheduling, MRI scheduling, physiotherapy attendance for CP.

**§12:** Metrics: seizure-freedom at 12 months ≥ 60% of newly-diagnosed epilepsy; migraine-frequency reduction ≥ 50% at 3 months of prophylaxis; EEG obtained within 4 weeks of first seizure ≥ 90%; CP hip-surveillance compliance ≥ 90%. Fellowship: SKIDS Pediatric Neurology Fellowship IS the credential pathway.
