# Your Child's SKIDS Health Check-Up — For Parents

## What is the SKIDS health check-up?

The SKIDS health check-up is a thorough, age-appropriate wellness review for your child — from birth through to 18 years. At each visit, the doctor checks your child's growth, development, vision, hearing, teeth, behaviour, sleep, blood pressure (from age 3), and vaccination status, using a combination of observation, parent conversation, and screening tools. The purpose is to catch anything early — before it becomes a problem — and to make sure your child is on track across all areas. If everything is fine, you get reassurance and practical guidance for the months ahead. If something needs attention, the doctor connects you to the right SKIDS specialty clinic or specialist straight away.

## What does SKIDS do about it?

At every check-up, the SKIDS doctor:
- Measures and plots your child's growth to track their trajectory over time
- Screens vision, hearing, and development using age-appropriate tools
- Checks vaccination status and administers any doses that are due
- Reviews sleep, feeding, behaviour, and school performance with you
- Provides age-appropriate guidance — things like safe sleep practices, healthy screen-time limits, nutrition advice, and activity targets
- Prescribes routine supplements (vitamin D and iron) where recommended for your child's age
- For adolescents, conducts a private, confidential conversation about wellbeing, mood, and safety

If anything needs closer attention, the doctor either:
- Sets up a monitoring plan with a recheck visit in 3 months, or
- Books your child into the relevant SKIDS specialty clinic (such as the vision clinic, nutrition clinic, or behavioural clinic) for a deeper assessment

## What you can do at home

- Keep the parent app updated — log any concerns between visits so the doctor can see them at the next check-up
- Follow the age-appropriate guidance the doctor shares after each visit (sleep, screen time, nutrition, safety)
- Complete any home tracking the doctor activates (such as a sleep diary or food diary) before the recheck visit
- Make sure your child's vaccination card is up to date and bring it to every visit
- For adolescents: give your teenager the space to speak privately with the doctor — this is a normal part of adolescent health care

## When to call us

Call SKIDS immediately if your child:
- Has difficulty breathing, turns blue, or is breathing very fast
- Has a seizure or loses consciousness
- Cannot be woken or seems unusually drowsy
- Has a very high fever with a rash that looks like small bruises or dots that do not fade when you press on them
- Has sudden unexplained bruising in unusual places
- Talks about wanting to hurt themselves or not wanting to be alive (any age)

Call SKIDS within 24 hours if your child:
- Develops a new symptom that worries you between visits
- Has a fever that does not respond to paracetamol and lasts more than 48 hours
- Suddenly loses a skill they previously had (for example, stops walking or stops talking)

You do not need to wait for the next scheduled visit to contact us. Call the clinic any time something changes.

## Common questions

**Q: My child seems fine — do we really need regular check-ups?**
A: Many conditions in childhood — such as mild vision problems, iron deficiency, or early growth concerns — have no obvious symptoms at first. Regular check-ups catch these early, when they are easiest to address. Most visits confirm that everything is on track, and that is valuable information too.

**Q: My child co-sleeps with us. Will the doctor tell us to stop?**
A: No. Co-sleeping is common in Indian families. The doctor will share safe co-sleeping practices — such as using a firm surface and keeping soft bedding away — rather than asking you to stop. The goal is safety, not changing your family's sleeping arrangement.

**Q: Will the doctor label my child with a mental-health diagnosis at the screening visit?**
A: No. If the check-up raises a behavioural or emotional concern, the doctor uses general language ("we want to look at this more carefully") rather than diagnostic labels at the screening stage. If a more detailed assessment is needed, it happens at the behavioural clinic, where the doctor explains everything step by step and with your input.

**Q: We have concerns about vaccination. Will we be pressured?**
A: No. The doctor will share the evidence and answer your questions honestly. If you choose to delay or decline a vaccine, the doctor documents your decision and revisits the conversation at the next visit. Your child remains enrolled at SKIDS regardless of your vaccination decision.

**Q: My child's previous doctor had different records. How do you handle that?**
A: At the first visit, the SKIDS doctor reviews any prior records you bring — growth charts, vaccination cards, discharge summaries — and enters them into your child's SKIDS health record. Any gaps in the check-up schedule are filled at this visit.

## What happens next

Your child's next check-up is scheduled based on their age:
- **Newborns and infants** are seen frequently — every 1 to 3 months in the first year
- **Toddlers** are seen every 3 to 6 months
- **Preschool, school-age, and adolescent children** are seen once a year (or more often if something needs watching)

If the doctor asked you to come back sooner for a recheck (usually in 3 months), that appointment is booked before you leave the clinic.

The parent app will remind you of upcoming visits and give you access to the guidance and programmes activated after today's check-up. You can also see your child's health summary on the app at any time.
