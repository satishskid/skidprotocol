# Understanding Your Child's Development — For Parents

## What is developmental surveillance?

Every child develops at their own pace, but there are milestones — sitting, walking, talking, playing, solving problems — that most children reach within a predictable window. When our screening checks show that your child may be taking longer than expected in one or more areas, we do a detailed assessment to understand exactly where they are, what is going well, and where they might benefit from extra support. Some children catch up on their own. Others do better with early help — therapy, targeted activities, and guided support. The earlier we identify a gap and act on it, the better the outcome. Developmental surveillance is not about finding something wrong; it is about making sure your child gets the best possible start.

---

## What does SKIDS do about it?

Here is how we help your child:

- **Detailed assessment.** We use validated tools to measure your child's abilities across five areas: movement, hand skills, understanding language, speaking, and social interaction. These give us an objective picture — not guesswork.
- **Hearing and vision checks.** We make sure your child can hear and see properly, because these affect all other development. If there is a problem, we address it first.
- **Early-intervention services.** If your child needs support, we connect you with the right therapists — speech therapy for language, occupational therapy for hand skills and daily activities, physiotherapy for movement. We coordinate with the therapy team so you are not managing it alone.
- **Home activities.** We give you specific, age-appropriate activities to do at home every day — reading together, movement games, interactive play — that support the area where your child needs help.
- **Ongoing monitoring.** We track your child's progress over time. If things are improving, we celebrate. If progress is slower than expected, we adjust the plan.
- **Specialist referral when needed.** If the assessment suggests a need for more detailed testing (for example, to understand whether your child has autism, or to measure their learning ability), we arrange that with a specialist. We do not wait — we start therapy while the specialist assessment is being arranged.

## What you can do at home

- **Talk to your child constantly.** Narrate what you are doing: "Now we are washing the red cup." This builds language even before your child can respond. Reduce screen time and increase conversation time.
- **Read together every day.** Even 10 minutes of shared reading — pointing at pictures, naming objects, asking questions — builds language and thinking skills.
- **Play on the floor.** Get down to your child's level. Roll a ball, stack blocks, do puzzles together. These activities build hand skills, problem-solving, and social connection.
- **Follow the therapy programme.** If a therapist has given you exercises or activities, do them consistently. Short daily practice is more effective than long occasional sessions.
- **Celebrate small wins.** Every new skill your child masters is progress. Focus on what your child can do, not just what they cannot do yet.
- **Limit screen time.** For children under 2, no screens. For children 2-5, no more than one hour per day. Interactive play with a caregiver builds development; screens do not.

## When to call us

**Call SKIDS immediately if:**
- Your child has lost skills they previously had — stopped talking, stopped walking, or become unsteady when they were previously steady
- Your child has a seizure or unusual repetitive movements (head drops, body stiffening in clusters)
- Your child suddenly stops responding to you or seems to "switch off"

**Call the clinic within a few days if:**
- You feel your child's progress has stalled despite therapy
- Your child is not attending therapy sessions (we can help with alternatives)
- You are struggling with the home-activity programme and need guidance
- A teacher or caregiver has raised a new concern
- You have questions about government support, disability services, or therapy options

## Common questions

**Q: Does delayed development mean my child is not intelligent?**
A: No. Development and intelligence are not the same thing. Many children who are slower to reach milestones catch up fully with the right support. Even children who have lasting delays have strengths and abilities — our job is to help them reach their full potential.

**Q: My mother says he is just a late bloomer and we should wait.**
A: Some children are late bloomers who catch up on their own. The assessment tells us which group your child is in. If he is going to catch up, the test will show that — and you lose nothing by checking. If he needs support, starting early makes a real difference.

**Q: Will my child be labelled?**
A: The assessment is about understanding your child's needs, not about labelling. We describe things in practical terms — "needs help with language" or "movement skills are developing slowly." Formal diagnostic terms are used only when they help your child access services or school support, and always with your involvement.

**Q: Can therapy really help, or is development fixed?**
A: The young brain is remarkably adaptable. Early-intervention therapy — started in the first few years of life — takes advantage of this adaptability. Research consistently shows that children who receive early therapy make significantly more progress than those who wait. Therapy does not guarantee that every child will catch up completely, but it gives every child the best possible chance.

**Q: What about traditional remedies or special diets?**
A: We respect your family's choices. We ask only that evidence-based therapy continues alongside any traditional practices. There is no scientific evidence for special diets, supplements, or alternative therapies for developmental delay. What does work is structured therapy, consistent home activities, and regular monitoring.

**Q: My child was premature. Does that mean they will always be behind?**
A: Many premature babies catch up by age 2-3. We use "corrected age" (adjusting for how early they were born) when assessing their milestones, which gives a fairer picture. The developmental follow-up programme for premature babies is specifically designed to catch any areas that need support early.

## What happens next

Your next visit depends on your child's assessment:
- **At-risk (needs watching closely):** We see your child again in 3 months with the same assessment tools to check progress.
- **Mild delay (one area):** We start targeted therapy and review in 3 months.
- **Moderate delay (multiple areas):** We start therapy in several areas and review in 6 weeks.
- **Significant delay:** Monthly reviews for the first 3 months, then every 3 months. We also arrange specialist assessment for a full picture.
- **Known condition (Down syndrome, cerebral palsy, etc.):** Six-monthly monitoring visits, plus coordination with your therapy team.

Your SKIDS parent app will show your child's developmental status, therapy schedule, milestone tracker (where you can log new skills), and age-appropriate home activities.
