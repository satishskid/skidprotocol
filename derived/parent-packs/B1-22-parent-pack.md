# Understanding Vaccination — For Parents

## What is vaccination?

Vaccination protects your child against serious infectious diseases — such as measles, whooping cough, polio, hepatitis, pneumonia, and diarrhoea caused by rotavirus — by training the immune system to recognise and fight these infections before they happen. The Indian Academy of Pediatrics recommends a schedule of vaccines from birth through age 18, given at specific ages when they are most effective. Some vaccines need multiple doses to build full protection, and some need boosters as your child grows. SKIDS follows the nationally recommended schedule and also offers additional recommended vaccines — such as those for chickenpox, hepatitis A, typhoid, influenza, and HPV (which prevents certain cancers) — so your child has the most complete protection available.

## What does SKIDS do about it?

At SKIDS, vaccination is built into your child's regular health check-ups. At each visit, the doctor checks which vaccines are due, confirms there are no reasons to delay, and administers the doses your child needs. Here is how we help:

- We maintain a complete digital vaccination record for your child, updated at every visit
- If your child has missed any doses, we create a personalised catch-up plan to get them back on track safely
- We offer the HPV cancer-prevention vaccine for both boys and girls from age 9
- We provide travel vaccination advice and doses if your family is travelling internationally
- After every vaccination, your child stays in the clinic for a 30-minute observation period — this is standard safety practice
- We issue an updated digital and printable vaccination certificate after each visit

## What you can do at home

- Bring your child's vaccination card (paper or digital) to every SKIDS visit
- After vaccination, your child may have a mild fever or a sore spot where the injection was given — this is normal and usually lasts a day or two
- You can give paracetamol (at the dose the doctor recommends) if your child is uncomfortable after vaccination
- Apply a cool cloth to the injection site if it is sore
- Keep track of the next vaccination date on the parent app — it will send you a reminder

## When to call us

Call SKIDS immediately if, after vaccination, your child:
- Develops a widespread rash with swelling of the face, lips, or tongue
- Has difficulty breathing or wheezing
- Becomes unusually pale, floppy, or unresponsive
- Cries inconsolably for more than 3 hours
- Has a fever above 40 degrees Celsius that does not come down with paracetamol
- Develops swelling that spreads well beyond the injection site (past the nearest joint)

These reactions are rare but need prompt attention. For any other concerns after vaccination, call the clinic during working hours — we are happy to advise.

## Common questions

**Q: Is it safe to give multiple vaccines at one visit?**
A: Yes. Your child's immune system handles thousands of different substances every day. Vaccines add a very small number to that load. Giving multiple vaccines at one visit is safe and means fewer clinic trips for your family. If you prefer fewer injections per visit, we can spread them across visits — just let us know.

**Q: My child has missed several vaccines. Is it too late to catch up?**
A: It is almost never too late. We will make a catch-up plan with the right spacing between doses. Most children can be fully caught up within a few months. The important thing is to start.

**Q: Does the HPV vaccine encourage sexual activity?**
A: No. The HPV vaccine is given at age 9-14 because it works best before any exposure to the virus, which is very common. It protects against cancers that can develop decades later. There is no evidence that HPV vaccination changes behaviour.

**Q: We prefer natural immunity. Why vaccinate?**
A: Natural infection does produce immunity, but it also carries the risk of serious complications. For example, measles can cause brain inflammation in about 1 in 1,000 children. The vaccine achieves the same immunity without the disease risk.

**Q: We have religious or dietary concerns about vaccine ingredients.**
A: Some vaccines have specific ingredients that families ask about. Where alternative formulations exist, we offer them. The doctor can discuss the specifics and share statements from recognised religious authorities that address common concerns.

**Q: We cannot afford all the recommended vaccines.**
A: The government-programme vaccines (included in your Basic plan) cover the most essential diseases at no extra cost. We can start with those and discuss the additional recommended vaccines when your family is ready.

## What happens next

Your child's next vaccination visit depends on their age and schedule:
- **Infants** receive several vaccines in the first year, with visits roughly every 4-10 weeks
- **Toddlers and preschoolers** have booster doses at 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- **School-age and adolescent children** receive Tdap and HPV doses, plus annual influenza

If your child is on a catch-up plan, the next visit is usually in 2-4 weeks. The parent app shows your child's complete vaccination timeline — what has been given, what is coming next, and when.

After today's visit, you will receive:
- An updated vaccination certificate (digital and printable)
- Post-vaccination care guidance on the parent app
- A reminder for the next vaccination date
