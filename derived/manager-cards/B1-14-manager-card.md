# Pulmonology / Asthma Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Asthma Clinic diagnoses and manages childhood asthma, recurrent wheezing, and chronic cough. The doctor uses breathing tests, peak-flow monitoring, and structured follow-ups to keep asthma well controlled. Every child with asthma gets a written action plan (green-yellow-red traffic light) and spacer-technique training. The clinic works closely with the Allergy Clinic to identify and manage triggers like dust, pollen, and pets.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "He wheezes every time he gets a cold."
- "She coughs at night and can't sleep."
- "His asthma pump isn't working any more."
- "She's been coughing for 6 weeks."
- "He was in hospital for an asthma attack."
- "Asthma runs in the family — should we test?"
- "Her asthma is triggered by dust / pollen / animals."
- "He snores loudly and stops breathing at night."
- "She gets breathless during PE class."
- "He's breathing fast / struggling to breathe."
- "She has a wet cough that never goes away."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "It's just a cold — he doesn't have asthma." | "Many children wheeze with colds. The doctor's assessment tells us whether it is a pattern that needs a preventer. The preventer stops the colds from triggering bad episodes." |
| "Inhalers are addictive / for life." | "Inhalers are not addictive. Many children step down or stop as they grow. The preventer is used to keep the airways calm — like brushing teeth prevents cavities. The doctor reviews every 3 months whether he still needs it." |
| "We can't afford the inhaler and spacer every month." | "The preventer canister lasts 1–2 months and costs about ₹100–150. The spacer lasts years. The Advanced plan includes the spacer kit. An ER visit for an asthma attack costs much more." |
| "Can't he just use the reliever when he needs it?" | "Reliever-only treatment puts him at higher risk of bad attacks. The preventer stops attacks from happening. The doctor has tailored this plan to his needs." |
| "We want to try homoeopathy / ayurveda first." | "The doctor respects your choices. We ask that you continue the preventer alongside any complementary treatment so he is protected while you explore other options." |

| Recurrent wheeze in a child under 5 | 3 months | 2 weeks past due |
| Chronic cough under investigation | 4 weeks | 1 week past due |

**Seasonal pre-engagement:** Contact asthma families before winter and monsoon to reinforce preventer use during high-trigger periods.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage severe asthma that needs biologic injections (like omalizumab) — those cases go to a lung specialist.
- We do not manage structural lung conditions (e.g., cystic fibrosis, bronchiectasis) — those go to a lung specialist.
- We do not manage TB — if TB is suspected during a cough workup, the child is referred to the government TB programme.
- We do not perform bronchoscopy or CT scans.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (e.g., pediatric lung specialist, ER for acute attack)
- Contact details for the relevant partner
- **Priority note:** Asthma children who miss follow-ups are high-priority for re-engagement, because stopping the preventer increases the risk of attacks.
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the timeframe shown
