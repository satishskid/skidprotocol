# SKIDS Behavioural Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Behavioural Clinic helps children and teenagers with attention difficulties (ADHD), anxiety, low mood, disruptive behaviour, and school refusal. The doctor assesses the child using validated questionnaires completed by parents, teachers, and the child, then builds a plan that may include structured therapy sessions through our LISSUN partner, parent-training programmes, school-liaison letters, and medication where indicated. The clinic also screens for autism (and refers for formal diagnosis) and provides brief support for adjustment difficulties and adolescent psychosocial concerns. Adolescent visits include a private conversation with the teenager, protected by a clear confidentiality framework.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "We don't want to label our child / put 'mental health' on his record." | "Our doctor communicates findings in everyday terms — 'attention difficulty', 'low mood', 'anxiety affecting school' — at the first visit. Diagnostic labels become relevant only later, with your consent, and are useful primarily for school accommodations. The bundle covers the care without forcing any label." |
| "Going to a 'mental health' clinic feels stigmatising." | "The Behavioural Clinic is part of a children's clinic, not a separate facility. The visits are scheduled like any other children's visit. LISSUN sessions can be in-clinic or by video — whatever works for the family." |
| "Therapy is for problems we should handle in the family." | "Family is exactly what most of the work is about. The parent-training programme is designed for the parent to learn techniques; the child isn't being 'fixed' away from the family. Many sessions involve the parent more than the child." |
| "Medication for a child seems extreme." | "We start without medication where severity allows; we move to medication only when behavioural approaches haven't worked or when severity warrants it. The decision is jointly made with you; the bundle covers either pathway." |
| "We can pay per session at LISSUN — why bundle?" | "You can. For families on a 12-session therapy programme, the Premium bundle works out cheaper than per-session LISSUN rates plus our visits. We can pull up the comparison." |
| "Suicidality / hospitalisation is for severe cases — we don't think that applies." | "The reason we screen with a standard questionnaire is precisely so we don't make that decision based on appearance. If the screening doesn't surface concern, we don't escalate. The screening is the protective step." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Mild attention difficulty on behavioural-first approach | 12 weeks | 14 weeks |
| Moderate/severe attention difficulty on medication | Weekly for 4 weeks (titration), then 4 weeks, then 8-12 weeks | 7 days on medication (high-priority) |
| Mild anxiety/depression on therapy | 6 weeks | 8 weeks |
| Moderate anxiety/depression on medication + therapy | Weekly for 4 weeks, then 4 weeks, then 8 weeks | 7 days on medication (high-priority) |
| Disruptive behaviour on parent-training | 4 weeks (mid-programme), 12 weeks (end) | 2 weeks |
| Adjustment / brief therapy | 6 weeks (mid-programme), 12 weeks (end) | 8 weeks |
| Annual heart tracing (for children on stimulant medication) | 7 days before due date reminder | Day of due date |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child on stimulant medication or antidepressant who misses two consecutive follow-ups. Contact by manager, doctor, and LISSUN therapist.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not diagnose autism at this clinic — we screen and refer for specialist diagnostic assessment.
- We do not prescribe antipsychotic medicines or mood stabilisers — those require a psychiatrist.
- We do not manage severe depression, psychosis, severe eating disorders, or active substance dependence — those go to a specialist.
- We do not provide specialist trauma therapy — our LISSUN partners handle milder cases; severe cases go to a specialist therapist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (psychiatrist, neurologist, developmental specialist, emergency room)
- Contact details for the partner specialist or LISSUN crisis arm
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- **For same-day emergencies involving safety concerns:** the child does not leave the clinic unaccompanied. Coordinate with the doctor and caregiver for direct transfer.
- For adolescent visits with privacy in effect: the Manager Console shows only the parent-disclosable summary
