# SKIDS Bedwetting Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Bedwetting Clinic helps children aged 5 and older who wet the bed at night. About 1 in 10 children at age 7 still wets the bed — it is very common and is not the child's fault. The clinic determines whether the bedwetting is the simple type (just nighttime wetting, no daytime problems) or whether there are other factors like constipation, daytime accidents, or medical causes that need to be addressed first. The main treatment is a bedwetting alarm — a device that trains the brain to wake up when the bladder is full. The clinic loans the alarm, trains the family on how to use it, and monitors progress over a 12-16 week programme. For children who do not respond to the alarm, or for sleepovers and camps, a medicine (desmopressin) is available.

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Education + motivational support (mild) | 4 weeks | 6 weeks |
| Alarm programme | 4 weeks (technique check), then monthly for 4 months | 2 weeks |
| Desmopressin (medicine) | 2 weeks (dose response check) | 3 weeks |
| Medicine withdrawal attempt | Monthly during withdrawal phase | 2 weeks |
| Referred to specialist (refractory) | Per specialist guidance | B1-20 maintains contact |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child who drops out of the alarm programme. Alarm therapy only works with consistent use over 12-16 weeks. Programme dropout is common. Manager re-engages family within 2 weeks of a missed visit.

---

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage daytime wetting as the primary concern — that is investigated by the Kidney Clinic.
- We do not prescribe imipramine (an older medicine with heart-related risks) — it is not used at SKIDS.
- We do not manage bedwetting caused by nerve or spinal problems — those go to a specialist.
- We do not guarantee the alarm will work for every child — success rate is 65-70%, and some children need medicine or specialist referral.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (kidney specialist for bladder tests, endocrine specialist for excessive thirst/urination, nerve specialist for spinal concerns, emergency room for medicine-related seizure)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency for seizure related to the medicine — very rare; within 7 days for new excessive thirst/urination or blood in urine; within 4 weeks for bladder tests after alarm and medicine have not worked, or spinal abnormality on examination)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
