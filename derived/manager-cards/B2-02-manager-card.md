# SKIDS Neurology Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Neurology Clinic manages brain and nerve conditions in children: epilepsy (seizures), migraine headaches, cerebral palsy, neuromuscular conditions (such as muscular dystrophy), movement disorders (tics, Tourette syndrome), and investigation of children who have lost previously learned skills (developmental regression). The doctor prescribes anti-seizure medicines, headache treatments, physiotherapy, and specialist equipment. This is specialist care available only at SKIDS clinics where a credentialed children's neurologist is on staff. At clinics without this specialist, children are referred to our named neurology partner.

- "My child had a seizure."
- "She has been diagnosed with epilepsy — we need regular follow-up."
- "He gets terrible headaches with nausea and light sensitivity."
- "She has cerebral palsy and needs physiotherapy coordination."
- "He has tics he can't control — blinking, throat clearing."
- "She used to walk and talk but has been losing skills."
- "He had a febrile seizure — should we be worried?"
- "She was referred from the developmental clinic for investigation."
- "His muscles seem weak — he falls a lot and can't climb stairs."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "The medicine is controlling the seizures — why do we need to keep coming?" | "That is exactly the goal. The follow-up visits check that the medicine dose is still right as your child grows, monitor for side-effects with blood tests, and plan when we can start reducing the medicine. Skipping visits risks the dose becoming too low as your child gains weight, which can cause a breakthrough seizure." |
| "We can get the medicine from our local chemist — why use the SKIDS dispensary?" | "You absolutely can. The dispensary is a convenience, not a requirement. The clinical care and follow-up are the same either way. The subscription bundle includes the medicines, which is usually cheaper than buying them separately, but that is your choice." |
| "Our child has been seizure-free — do we really need an EEG?" | "The EEG helps us decide when it is safe to start reducing the medicine. Without it, we are guessing. It is a painless test — the child sits with some sensors on the head for about 30 minutes. We recommend it at the milestones the doctor has scheduled." |
| "Physiotherapy is expensive and my child does not like it." | "We understand. For children with cerebral palsy, physiotherapy is the single most important intervention — more important than any medicine. The subscription includes the sessions, and our physiotherapist is experienced with children who find it hard. We can adjust the session format to make it more engaging." |
| "We want to try Ayurvedic medicine for epilepsy." | "We respect that. The doctor's request is that your child continues the prescribed medicine alongside any other therapy you choose, because stopping suddenly is dangerous. As long as both continue, we can monitor safely." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Epilepsy — newly diagnosed, dose adjustment phase | Every 4 weeks | 7 days |
| Epilepsy — stable on medicine | Every 3 months | 14 days |
| Epilepsy — not responding to medicine | Every 4-6 weeks | 7 days |
| Migraine — on preventive medicine | Every 6-8 weeks during dose adjustment, then every 3 months | 30 days |
| Cerebral palsy | Every 6 months + annual hip X-ray (for those with more limited mobility) | 14 days |
| Febrile seizure (complex) | 3 months, then 6 months, then annual | 14 days |
| Neuromuscular disease (muscular dystrophy) | Every 3-4 months | 14 days |
| Movement disorders / tics | Every 3 months (step down to 6-monthly if stable for 12 months) | 30 days |
| Loss of skills — under investigation | Every 4-6 weeks | 7 days |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** Any child on anti-seizure medicine. Missing follow-ups risks running out of medicine, which can cause dangerous seizures. Manager phone call at 7 days; medicine refill check. Also check: has the child's seizure diary been updated in the app?

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not offer this service without a credentialed children's neurologist on staff. If the specialist is not available at your clinic, the child is referred to our neurology partner.
- We do not manage prolonged seizures (status epilepticus) in clinic — those go to the emergency room. We manage the ongoing care.
- We do not perform brain surgery — children who need epilepsy surgery are referred to a specialist surgery centre.
- We do not manage acute strokes — those go to the emergency room.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral or emergency, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the type of emergency or specialist needed (emergency room, epilepsy surgery centre, neuromuscular centre, neurosurgery)
- Contact details for the partner specialist or emergency room
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- **For same-day emergencies (prolonged seizure, sudden brain-related deterioration, stroke):** coordinate immediate transfer to the emergency room. Confirm by phone that the receiving team is expecting the child.
- **For medicine running out:** treat as urgent. A child stopping anti-seizure medicine suddenly risks a dangerous seizure. Coordinate refill or emergency prescription the same day.
