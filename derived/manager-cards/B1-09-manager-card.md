# SKIDS Nutrition & Growth Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Nutrition & Growth Clinic manages children who are not growing as expected, have nutritional deficiencies (iron, vitamin D, B12, zinc, vitamin A), or need help with feeding — from breastfeeding support and starting solids through to adolescent sports nutrition. The clinic uses our in-house dietitian to build individualised eating plans, prescribes supplements where blood tests show deficiency, and runs structured programmes for picky eaters, breastfeeding difficulties, and growth rehabilitation. For children with cow's-milk-protein allergy, the clinic works jointly with the Allergy Clinic to manage the elimination diet and ensure the child's nutrition stays adequate.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "He's not growing like his cousin / brother / classmates."
- "She doesn't eat anything we give her."
- "He's been pale and tired."
- "She's exclusively breastfed and I'm worried she isn't getting enough."

- "She's vegetarian and I worry about her nutrition."

- "He's adolescent, very active in sports, and tired all the time."
- "She's eating less, losing weight, and exercising a lot."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "We're already buying iron syrup at the chemist; why pay for visits?" | "Iron syrup at a chemist costs less per bottle, but the visits include the blood recheck, the dose adjustment, and the dietitian time. Most families on a 3-month iron course need three or four visits; the bundle covers those." |
| "The dietitian sessions seem unnecessary — we know how to feed our child." | "The dietitian's job isn't to tell you how to feed your child differently; it's to help work out where the calories and nutrients are slipping through the gaps. Most families finish the first session with one or two specific changes that fit your kitchen, your routine, and your child's preferences." |
| "Our grandmother manages the feeding; she'll handle this." | "That's actually exactly why we'd love to have her at the dietitian session — most family feeding decisions involve grandparents, and the most effective plans have the whole feeding team in the room." |
| "Why so many supplements? We don't want our child on chemicals." | "Each supplement is for a specific deficiency found on the blood test. We're not adding supplements speculatively. The doses are appropriate for children; the duration is time-limited; and we recheck levels to confirm they've normalised." |
| "Premium is expensive; we'd rather pay per visit." | "For families on a 12-week programme with eight visits and four dietitian sessions, the bundle is generally cheaper than the per-visit total. We can pull up the cost comparison for your specific case." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage severe malnutrition — children who are severely underweight or have swelling from malnutrition need hospital admission with specialised feeding.
- We do not manage severe anaemia (very low blood counts) — those need hospital-based treatment.
- We do not diagnose or treat hormonal causes of short stature — those go to an endocrine specialist.
- We do not manage coeliac disease, inflammatory bowel disease, or other conditions causing poor absorption — those go to a gastroenterology specialist.
- We do not manage eating disorders (anorexia, bulimia) — those go to a psychiatrist with our Behavioural and Adolescent clinics as support.

- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
