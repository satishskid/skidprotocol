# SKIDS Cardiac Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Cardiac Clinic evaluates heart-related concerns picked up at screening — murmurs, blood pressure readings, and sports fitness checks. The doctor listens carefully to the heart using a digital stethoscope, determines whether a murmur is harmless (most are) or needs further investigation, monitors blood pressure over multiple visits, and provides sports-clearance certificates for competitive athletes. For children with known heart conditions, the clinic coordinates follow-up with the heart specialist. The clinic also checks newborns whose critical heart-disease screen was missed at the hospital, and monitors the heart safety of children on ADHD medication.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "It's just a murmur — why do we need a check?" | "Most murmurs in children are innocent — and the check confirms that. It takes 15 minutes and gives you peace of mind." |
| "BP is for old people — why check in children?" | "Childhood high blood pressure is increasingly common, especially with rising obesity rates. Catching it early prevents heart and kidney problems later. The check is quick and painless." |
| "She fainted once — it's probably nothing." | "Most fainting in children is harmless. But the doctor checks carefully to make sure the heart is not the cause, because that small number of cases needs urgent attention." |
| "He's fine — why does he need a heart check for ADHD medicine?" | "The ADHD medicine can slightly raise heart rate and blood pressure. A quick check before starting and at each visit makes sure everything stays safe. It's routine and takes just a few minutes." |
| "Why can't you do the heart scan here?" | "The heart scan (echocardiogram) needs specialist equipment and a trained cardiologist to read it. We coordinate the appointment with our heart specialist partner so the family doesn't have to figure it out alone." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not perform heart scans (echocardiograms) at the clinic — those are done by our heart specialist partner.
- We do not manage heart failure, abnormal heart rhythms, or heart-muscle disease — those go directly to a heart specialist.
- We do not perform heart procedures or surgery — those are managed at specialist centres.
- We do not manage Kawasaki disease beyond recognising it and referring urgently to a heart specialist.
- We do not independently read ECG results — these are reviewed by or with a heart specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (heart specialist, kidney specialist, emergency room)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency for fainting during exercise, severe high blood pressure with symptoms, or a blue episode; within 7 days for confirmed high blood pressure or a failed newborn heart screen; within 4 weeks for a murmur needing a heart scan)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- For sports-clearance referrals: communicate to the family that clearance is on hold until the heart specialist reviews
