# SKIDS Constipation Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Constipation Clinic helps children who have hard, infrequent, or painful stools, and children who soil their underwear because of constipation. The doctor confirms the diagnosis, checks for any underlying medical causes (which are rare), and starts a structured programme: first clearing the blockage with a higher dose of medicine for a few days, then keeping the stool soft with a daily medicine (PEG) for 3-6 months while the stretched bowel recovers. The programme also includes dietary changes (more fibre, more water, less milk), toileting-behaviour training (regular toilet sits after meals), and follow-up to ensure the medicine is working and is gradually reduced at the right time. Constipation is also a common cause of bedwetting, so the clinic works closely with the Bedwetting Clinic.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "She'll become dependent on the medicine." | "The medicine (PEG) is safe long-term — it holds water in the stool to keep it soft. It is not habit-forming. We wean off gradually once the bowel recovers. Stopping too soon is the main reason constipation comes back." |
| "Can't we just change the diet?" | "Diet helps prevent constipation from returning, but once a blockage has built up, diet alone cannot clear it. The medicine clears the blockage; the diet prevents it from happening again." |
| "He's soiling on purpose — it's a behaviour problem." | "He is not doing it on purpose. When constipation builds up, the bowel stretches and loses sensation. Loose stool leaks around the blockage without the child knowing. Treating the constipation stops the soiling. Punishment makes it worse." |
| "We've tried everything — nothing works." | "Often what was tried before was stopped too soon. The medicine needs 3-6 months to let the bowel recover. The doctor will review what has been done and make sure the dose and duration are right this time." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Mild (medicine started) | 4 weeks | 6 weeks |
| After blockage clearance | 1-2 weeks (confirm clearance) | 1 week |
| Severe / soiling | 2 weeks | 1 week |
| Maintenance phase | Monthly for 3-6 months | 2 weeks |
| Weaning phase (reducing medicine dose) | Every 4 weeks | 2 weeks |
| After successful wean (off medicine) | 3 months (confirm no relapse) | 4 months |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child whose PEG medicine has not been refilled for more than 2 weeks (dispensary records). Premature medicine withdrawal is the most common cause of relapse. Manager contacts family promptly.

---

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage constipation caused by Hirschsprung disease (a condition where nerves are missing from part of the bowel) — that goes to a children's surgeon.
- We do not manage constipation that does not improve after 3 months of proper treatment — that escalates to the GI (stomach and bowel) specialist for further investigation.
- We do not perform invasive tests (biopsies, contrast scans) — those are done by a specialist.
- We do not use enemas routinely in children — the medicine is given by mouth. Enemas are only used by specialists when the oral approach has not worked.
- We do not promise a quick fix — the bowel needs 3-6 months to recover, and the programme requires patience and consistency.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (GI specialist for refractory constipation or suspected organic cause, children's surgeon for Hirschsprung suspicion, Behavioural Clinic for severe emotional impact of soiling, emergency room for suspected bowel obstruction)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency for bowel obstruction with vomiting and severe pain; within 7 days for infant with delayed first stool, failure to thrive, and abdominal distension; within 4 weeks for constipation not improving after 3 months of treatment, or suspected underlying medical cause)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
