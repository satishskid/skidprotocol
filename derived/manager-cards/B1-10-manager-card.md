# SKIDS Obesity & Metabolic Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Obesity & Metabolic Clinic helps children and teenagers whose weight is above the healthy range for their height and age. The clinic confirms the diagnosis using growth charts, runs blood tests to check for early metabolic changes (blood sugar, cholesterol, liver health, blood pressure), and delivers the SKIDS Healthy Weight Programme — a 12-week structured family-based programme combining dietitian-led dietary changes, physical activity planning, screen-time reduction, and motivational support. The clinic also manages pre-diabetes with lifestyle changes and monitors children whose cholesterol or liver markers are elevated. The dietitian is integral — the programme does not run without one.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "He's just big-boned / it runs in the family." | "Family history does play a role. That's exactly why the programme is important — children with a family history benefit the most from early lifestyle changes because they have higher metabolic risk." |
| "She'll grow out of it." | "Some children do lose excess weight as they grow taller. But without changes, the trend usually continues — and the blood tests show early changes we want to reverse now while they're still reversible." |
| "We can do this at home — don't need a programme." | "Many families find that helpful. The programme gives you a structured plan, a dietitian who knows children's nutrition, and regular check-ins so you're not guessing. If home changes work, we'll see it on the growth chart at the next visit." |
| "My child will be upset if I change the food." | "The programme works with the whole family — everyone eats the same way. It's not about taking food away; it's about everyone building healthier habits together." |
| "Can't you just give a medicine for it?" | "For children, lifestyle changes are both safer and more effective than medicines. Medicines are reserved for a very small number of cases where lifestyle alone isn't enough, and those are managed by a specialist." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Overweight, no risk factors (counselling only) | 3 months | 4 months |
| Healthy Weight Programme (12 weeks) | Week 6 (mid-programme), Week 12 (end) | 3 days after missed session |
| Severely overweight / metabolic concerns | 4 weeks, then monthly for 3 months | 2 weeks |
| Pre-diabetes monitoring | 3 months (repeat blood test) | 4 months |
| Cholesterol dietary trial | 3 months, then 6 months (decision point) | 4 months |
| Fatty liver lifestyle trial | 3 months (repeat liver test), 6 months (decision point) | 4 months |
| Post-programme maintenance | 3-monthly for 1 year, then 6-monthly | 4 months |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child who drops out of the Healthy Weight Programme. Manager contacts family within 3 days of a missed session. Programme dropout is a significant risk for continued weight gain.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not prescribe weight-loss medicines for children — those require a specialist and are used in very few cases.
- We do not manage confirmed diabetes — that goes to an endocrine specialist.
- We do not offer weight-loss surgery assessments — extremely rare in children and managed by specialist centres.
- We do not impose restrictive diets on growing children — the approach is calorie-appropriate (meeting growth needs without excess), not calorie-restrictive.
- We do not manage rare genetic causes of obesity (like Prader-Willi syndrome) — those go to a genetics or endocrine specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (endocrinologist for diabetes, cardiologist for blood pressure or cholesterol, gastroenterologist for liver concerns, orthopaedics for hip/knee pain in severely overweight adolescents)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency for diabetic crisis or severe high blood pressure, within 7 days for confirmed diabetes, or within 4 weeks for specialist assessment)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- For Healthy Weight Programme dropouts: re-engage family within 3 days; document reason for dropout
