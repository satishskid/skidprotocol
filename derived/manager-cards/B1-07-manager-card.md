# SKIDS Sleep Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Sleep Clinic helps children who struggle with falling asleep, staying asleep, or getting quality sleep. For babies and toddlers, the most common issue is that they need a parent to fall asleep and wake repeatedly at night — this is treated with a structured 4-6 week behavioural programme that the family chooses and our counsellor supports. For older children and teenagers, the clinic manages difficulty falling asleep, delayed body-clock patterns, and screens for snoring-related breathing problems during sleep. If snoring suggests a blocked airway, the clinic arranges an overnight oxygen recording and refers to an ENT specialist.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "She wakes up multiple times at night needing me to settle her."
- "He takes hours to fall asleep at bedtime."
- "She refuses to go to bed without a fight."
- "He snores loudly every night."
- "She gasps or stops breathing in sleep."
- "He has nightmares many nights a week."
- "She sleepwalks / talks in her sleep."
- "He wakes up screaming and inconsolable."
- "She can't stay awake in school."
- "He's a teenager who falls asleep at 3am and can't wake for school."
- "She has restless legs / kicks all night."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "Sleep training is cruel — we don't want our baby to cry." | "The programme offers three different approaches — including ones where the parent stays in the room the whole time. The family chooses the method. All evidence-based approaches involve some short-term adjustment; most families see real improvement within two weeks. Our counsellor supports you through the hardest nights." |
| "He'll grow out of the snoring." | "Some children do. But if the snoring is causing oxygen drops at night, it can affect growth, learning, and behaviour. The overnight oxygen recording is a simple finger-clip sensor at home for two nights. It tells us whether we need to act now or can safely watch." |
| "Melatonin is available over the counter — why not just use that?" | "Over-the-counter melatonin products vary widely in actual content and quality. We use melatonin only when specific criteria are met, with pharmaceutical-grade preparations, for a limited time, and only after behavioural approaches have been tried. The clinic visit ensures it is used safely and appropriately." |
| "It's just a phase — all teenagers stay up late." | "Some late sleeping is normal in teenagers. When it reaches 2-3am regularly with school impact, we can help shift the body clock using morning light, evening routines, and sometimes a short course of low-dose melatonin. The programme is 6 weeks." |
| "We co-sleep and that's our family's choice." | "We respect that completely. The programme works within whatever sleeping arrangement your family uses — we don't require separate sleeping. We focus on improving sleep quality and reducing night-waking within your chosen setup." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not confirm sleep apnoea — that requires an overnight sleep study at a specialist laboratory. We screen and refer.
- We do not prescribe sleeping pills for children. Melatonin is used only in narrow, specific situations for limited periods.
- We do not manage narcolepsy or other rare sleep disorders — those go to a sleep specialist.
- We do not manage complex sleepwalking patterns or unusual sleep behaviours that need specialist investigation.
