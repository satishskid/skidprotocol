# SKIDS Throat & Airway Clinic --- Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Throat & Airway Clinic manages sore throats (using a rapid strep test to decide whether antibiotics are needed), assesses tonsil size and recurrent tonsillitis to determine when surgery is indicated, treats croup in-clinic, manages early peritonsillar infections, administers rheumatic fever prevention injections, and routes to our ENT surgeon partner for tonsil/adenoid removal and airway procedures.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "She has a sore throat and fever."
- "White patches on the tonsils."
- "He gets tonsillitis every few months."
- "The tonsils are huge -- she can barely swallow."
- "He snores and breathes through his mouth."
- "Barking cough, noisy breathing at night."
- "Swelling on one side of the throat -- can't open mouth."
- "Chronic sore throat -- never fully goes away."
- "She had rheumatic fever -- needs the injection."
- "Post-tonsillectomy follow-up."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "Just remove the tonsils and be done with it." | "The doctor tracks every episode carefully. Surgery is recommended when the evidence shows it will help -- based on specific criteria. Right now, [he] hasn't reached that threshold, but we're monitoring closely." |
| "Antibiotics didn't work last time." | "The doctor will check whether this infection is the same type. If the rapid test is negative, it's a different kind of infection that antibiotics won't help. If positive, the doctor may choose a different antibiotic." |
| "Croup sounds terrible -- shouldn't he be in hospital?" | "The steroid medicine we gave works within hours. Most children with mild-to-moderate croup are safe at home. We'll call you tomorrow to check. If the noisy breathing returns at rest, go straight to hospital." |
| "He's had tonsillitis 3 times this year." | "We're tracking that. The specialist referral happens at [threshold]. We're at [N] documented episodes. Each one is logged in detail so the ENT surgeon has the full picture when the time comes." |
| "I don't want monthly injections for RF." | "I understand that's difficult. The injection protects [his] heart from further damage. Missing injections increases the risk of another episode of rheumatic fever. Let's set up reminders and make it as convenient as possible -- same nurse, same time each month." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Viral sore throat | No routine follow-up; return if not resolving in 5 days | N/A |
| Strep throat (treated) | No routine follow-up if improving; return at 48-72 hours if not improving | 24 hours past 72h checkpoint |
| Recurrent tonsillitis (monitoring) | At next episode; per routine screening schedule | Log each episode |
| Croup (mild, sent home) | Phone follow-up at 24 hours | Same day if not reached |
| Croup (moderate, observed in clinic) | Phone at 24 hours; in-person at 48-72 hours if symptoms persist | Same day if not reached |
| Post-tonsillectomy | Day 7-10 post-op; 4 weeks post-op | 48 hours past due |
| Rheumatic fever injection | Every 3 weeks | CRITICAL: re-engage within 3 days if missed |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not perform tonsil or adenoid surgery -- that goes to our ENT surgeon partner.
- We do not perform scope examinations of the throat or airway -- those go to the ENT surgeon.
- We do not manage deep neck infections (abscess behind the throat) beyond initial recognition and emergency referral.
- We do not manage airway abnormalities that need surgical intervention.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (ENT surgeon, emergency department)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner
- Urgency level: emergency (same day), urgent (within 7 days), or routine (within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe

**Critical notes:**
- Rheumatic fever prophylaxis families are the highest-priority re-engagement group. Missed injections increase the risk of heart damage. If the injection is overdue by 3 days, call immediately.
- Post-tonsillectomy days 5-10 are the highest-risk period for bleeding. Send the family a reminder via WhatsApp during this window.
- Any post-tonsillectomy bleeding, even minor, requires emergency department attendance.
