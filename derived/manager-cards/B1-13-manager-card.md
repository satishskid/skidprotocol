# Adolescent Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Adolescent Clinic provides structured health assessments for teenagers (ages 10–18), including a confidential psychosocial interview, puberty evaluation, menstrual-health management, contraception counselling, substance-use screening, sports-clearance evaluations, and coordination with the Behavioural Clinic for mental-health concerns. A core feature is the privacy framework: part of every visit is conducted privately with the teenager, without the parent present.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "Routine teen check-up."
- "Periods are very painful / heavy / irregular."
- "I haven't started periods yet (age 15+)."
- "I want to know about contraception." (private)
- "Acne is awful and I'm avoiding school."
- "I'm not sleeping / studying / eating well."
- "I want to talk about something private."
- "School fitness certificate."
- "Body image / weight worries."
- "Drinking with friends every weekend."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "Why does part of the visit happen without me?" | "We see every teenager this way — it's our standard practice, not specific to your child. The private part lets them talk freely about things that matter for their health. You'll receive a summary of the general medical findings. If the doctor learns something about safety, you will be informed." |
| "My child doesn't need a 'mental health' check." | "We don't single out any child for a mental-health check. The psychosocial interview is part of every adolescent visit, just like checking height and weight. It covers school, friends, sleep, diet, and safety — the whole picture." |
| "She's too young for these topics." | "We raise topics at the age they become relevant. The conversation is age-appropriate, respectful, and led by what your teenager brings up. Our goal is for them to have reliable information and a trusted adult to ask." |
| "I want to know everything the doctor discussed." | "The doctor shares all general medical information with you. The confidential part is protected unless there is a safety concern — then you are always informed. This is the same standard used in adolescent medicine worldwide." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not treat severe mental-health crises (active self-harm intent, severe substance dependence) at this clinic — those are managed urgently with hospital and specialist partners.
- We do not manage confirmed pregnancies beyond initial counselling and handoff to an obstetric or family-planning specialist.
- We do not start certain strong skin medicines (isotretinoin) — those go through the Skin Clinic specialist pathway.
- We do not manage complex hormonal conditions (PCOS workup completion, delayed or early puberty) — those go to the Endocrine Clinic.
