# SKIDS Oral Health Clinic --- Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Oral Health Clinic assesses cavity risk, applies fluoride varnish to strengthen teeth, counsels families on reducing sugar in the diet to prevent cavities, trains parents and children on proper brushing, screens for crooked teeth and thumb-sucking habits, provides first aid for dental injuries, and routes to our children's dentist (pedodontist) partner for fillings, extractions, braces, and complex dental procedures.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "Routine dental check."
- "Brown spots on her teeth."
- "His teeth are rotting."
- "She fell and knocked a tooth."
- "His teeth are crooked."
- "She still sucks her thumb (age 5+)."
- "Teething pain -- what can I give?"
- "White patches in the baby's mouth."
- "Mouth sores -- can't eat."
- "Bad breath."
- "He won't let anyone near his mouth."
- "The screening visit flagged teeth."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "Baby teeth will fall out -- no need to worry." | "Baby teeth keep the space for adult teeth. Infections in baby teeth can damage the adult teeth growing underneath. Prevention now saves pain and cost later." |
| "Fluoride is harmful." | "The amount in the varnish is tiny -- less than in a cup of tea. It's painted on, not swallowed. It's the single most effective way to prevent cavities." |
| "We brush every day -- he shouldn't have cavities." | "Brushing is important, but frequency of sugar in the diet matters just as much. The dietitian can help identify hidden sugar sources. It's not about brushing harder -- it's about reducing how often the teeth are exposed to sugar." |
| "Can't afford the dentist." | "That's exactly why prevention matters -- fluoride and dietary changes are much less expensive than fillings. If [child] does need the dentist, we coordinate referral to our partner at the best available rate." |
| "He screams at the dentist." | "Many children are nervous. We start gently -- just looking at the teeth first. The fluoride application is quick and painless. Over a few visits, most children become comfortable." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not fill cavities or perform any dental procedures (fillings, crowns, extractions) -- those go to our children's dentist partner.
- We do not provide braces or orthodontic treatment -- those go to the dentist/orthodontist partner.
- We do not take dental X-rays -- those are done at the dentist partner clinic.
- We do not manage dental abscesses with facial swelling -- those go to the emergency department.
- We do not manage complex dental injuries (knocked-out permanent tooth, jaw fracture) beyond initial first aid and immediate referral.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (children's dentist, orthodontist, emergency department, or oral surgery)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner
- Urgency level: emergency (same day), urgent (within 7 days), or routine (within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe

**Critical note:** A knocked-out permanent tooth is an absolute time-critical emergency. The tooth must reach the dentist within 30-60 minutes. If this happens at the clinic, do not delay -- call the dentist immediately and arrange transport.

**Prevention note:** High-risk children who miss scheduled fluoride varnish visits should be re-engaged promptly. Dental cavities are the most common chronic disease in children, and prevention requires consistent programme adherence.
