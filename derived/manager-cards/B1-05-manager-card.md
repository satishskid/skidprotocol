# SKIDS Skin Clinic --- Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Skin Clinic manages eczema (atopic dermatitis), acne, common childhood skin infections (scabies, impetigo, ringworm), allergic skin reactions, and monitors birthmarks and moles. Treatment includes moisturisers, medicated creams, and short courses of oral medicines. Children with severe or treatment-resistant skin conditions are referred to our children's skin specialist (dermatologist) partner.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "She has dry, itchy patches that come and go."
- "His face / cheeks are red and rough since infancy."
- "She scratches all night and her sleep is broken."
- "His teenage acne is getting worse / leaving marks."
- "Itchy bumps between fingers / the whole family is itching."
- "His scalp has a circular bald patch with broken hairs."
- "She has yellow crusted spots on her face that are spreading."
- "Hives that come and go for weeks."
- "Birthmark she's had since birth -- should we worry?"
- "Many flat brown patches all over."
- "Diaper rash that won't clear."
- "Patches losing pigment."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "We'll just buy the cream from a chemist whenever it flares." | "That works for very mild eczema. For moderate eczema like [child]'s, the regimen has more parts -- the moisturiser quantity matters, the technique matters, and the follow-up visits to step the medication down are how we avoid the flares getting bigger. Buying a tube at a time tends to cost more than the bundle and misses the maintenance step." |
| "The doctor said this is forever -- why pay every year?" | "Eczema management changes as the child grows. The first 2-3 years on the programme need more visits; by school age, most children are on a maintenance regimen with one or two visits a year, and the bundle lets you step down to Basic at that point." |
| "Acne goes away on its own; why treat it?" | "Mild acne sometimes does. Moderate acne with inflammation tends not to, and untreated moderate acne is what leaves scars that last decades. The 12-week course gets the inflammation under control before it scars." |
| "Antibiotics for three months sounds like too much." | "It's a real concern. The 3-month cap is exactly to limit antibiotic resistance. We use the topical creams together with the antibiotic to reduce the duration we need the antibiotic for. We won't extend beyond 3 months without reassessing -- the programme blocks it." |
| "Why does the moisturiser cost so much per month?" | "Moderate eczema needs about 250-500 grams a month for a school-age child to actually work. Less than that, and the steroid course doesn't hold. The bundle includes the supply at a lower cost than buying individually." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage severe skin reactions (widespread blistering, mouth/eye involvement) -- those are emergencies that go to the hospital.
- We do not prescribe isotretinoin (strong acne medicine) or biologic medicines -- those are started by the skin specialist.
- We do not perform skin biopsies or surgical removal of skin lesions -- those go to our specialist partner.
- We do not manage genetic skin conditions or autoimmune skin diseases beyond initial assessment and referral.
- We do not manage vitiligo treatment -- that goes to the skin specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (children's skin specialist, emergency department, neurology/genetics)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner
- Urgency level: emergency (same day), urgent (within 7 days), or routine (within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe

**Important notes:**
- Children on oral antibiotics for acne or scalp ringworm who miss follow-up visits are a priority for re-engagement. Active antibiotic courses without follow-up are flagged in our quality review.
- The application-technique video reminder should go out on Day 3 after the first visit -- proper application technique is the single biggest factor in treatment success for eczema.
- A self-photo upload prompt via the parent app at Day 14 (eczema) or Week 4 (acne) helps the doctor assess progress between visits.
