# Gastrointestinal Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The GI Clinic evaluates and manages children with recurring tummy pain, reflux (acid coming up), chronic diarrhoea, and suspected coeliac disease (gluten sensitivity). The doctor uses structured assessments to determine whether the problem is functional (the gut is sensitive but not damaged) or organic (there is an underlying disease). The clinic also tests for H. pylori (a stomach bug) and co-manages fatty liver with the Obesity Clinic. Children who need endoscopy or who have inflammatory bowel disease are referred to a pediatric gastroenterologist.

**Priority re-engagement:** Coeliac families (gluten-free diet needs ongoing support) and children on acid-reducing medicine (to prevent indefinite unsupervised use).

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not perform endoscopy (camera into the stomach or bowel) — that is done by our pediatric GI specialist partner.
- We do not manage inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's or colitis) — those are managed by the GI specialist after diagnosis.
- We do not manage liver disease beyond fatty-liver screening — liver conditions go to a liver specialist.
- We do not manage surgical conditions (appendicitis, bowel obstruction) — those are emergencies managed at hospital.
- We do not manage neonatal jaundice — that follows a separate pathway.
