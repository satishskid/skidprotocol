# SKIDS Hearing & Ear Clinic --- Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Hearing & Ear Clinic performs hearing screening using OAE devices at routine visits, manages ear infections (acute and fluid buildup behind the eardrum), removes ear wax safely, identifies children with hearing loss and coordinates hearing-aid or cochlear-implant referrals, and routes to our ENT surgeon partner for grommets, adenoidectomy, and cochlear-implant surgery.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "Newborn hearing screen was a refer."
- "He doesn't turn to sounds."
- "She's not talking yet (18 months+)."
- "He always says 'what?' / turns up the TV."
- "Her ear hurts / she's pulling at her ear."
- "Ear discharge for weeks."
- "Doctor said there's fluid behind the eardrum."
- "Wax buildup -- can you clean it?"
- "He snores and has ear infections often."
- "She uses earphones all day -- worried about hearing."
- "RBSK flagged hearing."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "The hearing screen was just a refer -- it's probably nothing." | "You may be right -- many refers turn out to be nothing. But the repeat test tells us for sure. We don't want to miss something when catching it early makes all the difference." |
| "Ear infections are normal -- why does he need a specialist?" | "One or two ear infections a year are common. [He] has had [number] in [timeframe], which puts [him] at risk for fluid buildup and hearing problems. The ENT specialist will check if a small tube in the ear would prevent future infections." |
| "Hearing aids are too expensive." | "I understand. There are government schemes that can help cover the cost. I can help you with the application. Early amplification makes a huge difference for [child's name]'s speech and learning." |
| "She hears fine at home." | "Children with mild hearing loss often cope well at home where it's quiet. The challenge is in noisy classrooms. The hearing test shows exactly how much she hears at different pitches." |
| "We'll wait and see if the fluid clears." | "That's exactly what the doctor recommends -- 3 months of watching. We have the next hearing check scheduled at [date] to make sure it's resolving." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Ear infection (treated) | 48-72 hours (phone or in-person if not improving); then 4 weeks | 3 days past 48-72h check |
| Ear infection (watchful waiting) | 48-72 hours mandatory | 24 hours past due |
| Fluid behind eardrum (monitoring) | 3 months (hearing test + tympanometry) | 14 days past due |
| Mild hearing loss (monitoring) | 6 months (repeat hearing test) | 14 days past due |
| Newborn hearing screen refer | Within 7 days for repeat/diagnostic test | CRITICAL: re-engage within 24 hours if missed |
| Risk-indicator child (delayed-onset risk) | 6-monthly through age 3 | 14 days past due |
| Noise-exposure counselling (adolescents) | Annual at routine screening visit | Per screening cadence |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not perform specialised hearing tests (ABR/ASSR) -- those go to our audiology partner.
- We do not insert grommets (ear tubes) -- that goes to our ENT surgeon partner.
- We do not perform tonsil or adenoid surgery -- ENT surgeon partner.
- We do not manage complicated ear infections (mastoiditis, cholesteatoma) -- those go to the ENT surgeon or emergency department.
- We do not perform cochlear-implant surgery or programming -- that goes to the ENT surgeon and cochlear-implant centre.
- We do not manage ongoing hearing loss beyond initial diagnosis and referral.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (ENT surgeon, audiology partner, emergency department)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner
- Urgency level: emergency (same day), urgent (within 7 days), or routine (within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe

**Critical note:** Newborn hearing screen refers are the highest-priority cases. The national guideline requires diagnosis by 3 months and intervention by 6 months. If a newborn-refer family misses their follow-up, escalate immediately -- do not wait for the standard re-engagement window.
