# SKIDS Endocrine Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Endocrine Clinic manages hormonal conditions in children: type 1 diabetes (insulin management, blood-sugar monitoring, diet guidance), type 2 diabetes, thyroid problems (underactive and overactive), growth-hormone treatment for children who are not growing properly, early or late puberty, PCOS in teenagers, and adrenal conditions. This is specialist care available only at SKIDS clinics where a credentialed children's endocrinologist is on staff. At clinics without this specialist, children are referred to our named endocrinology partner.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "Insulin injections — is there really no tablet?" | "For type 1 diabetes, insulin is the only treatment — the body has stopped making its own. It is not a sign of severity; it is specific to the type of diabetes your child has. Most children adjust to the injections within a week or two. We also offer continuous glucose monitors that reduce the number of finger-pricks." |
| "Growth hormone injections every day seem like a lot." | "It is a very small injection at bedtime — most children get used to it quickly. We expect to see significant growth improvement in the first year. We monitor every three months and adjust as needed. The alternative is that your child does not reach their growth potential." |
| "We want to try Ayurvedic medicine for thyroid / diabetes." | "We respect your interest. For thyroid conditions, the tablet replaces a hormone the body is not making — it is not optional. For diabetes, insulin is life-sustaining. We ask that prescribed medicines continue alongside any other approach." |
| "The Premium subscription is expensive." | "We understand. For a child with diabetes, the subscription covers quarterly visits, blood tests (HbA1c), insulin supply, glucose-monitor sensors, and the annual comprehensive review. Paying separately for these typically costs more. The clinic manager can show you the comparison." |
| "Our child's diabetes is controlled — do we still need to come every 3 months?" | "That is exactly the goal. The quarterly visits check that the medicine dose is still right as your child grows, monitor for long-term effects, and adjust the plan. Children grow fast, and doses need regular updating." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Type 1 diabetes — newly diagnosed | 1 week after starting insulin | 3 days |
| Type 1 diabetes — stable | Every 3 months (HbA1c + review) | 7 days |
| Type 1 diabetes — annual comprehensive review | 12 months (eyes, kidneys, heart, thyroid) | 14 days |
| Type 2 diabetes | 4 weeks after starting medicine, then every 3 months | 7 days |
| Underactive thyroid — new | 2 weeks, then every 4-6 weeks until stable | 7 days |
| Underactive thyroid — stable | Every 3-6 months | 14 days |
| Overactive thyroid (Graves) | 2 weeks (blood count check), then every 4-6 weeks | 3 days (blood count is safety-critical) |
| Growth-hormone treatment | Every 3-6 months | 14 days |
| Early puberty on treatment | Every 3-6 months | 14 days |
| Adrenal condition (CAH) | Every 3 months | 7 days |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** Any child on insulin. Missed follow-ups increase the risk of a dangerous blood-sugar crisis. Manager phone call at 3 days, endocrinologist review at 7 days.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not offer this service without a credentialed children's endocrinologist on staff. If the specialist is not available at your clinic, the child is referred to our endocrinology partner.
- We do not manage acute diabetic emergencies (blood-sugar crises) in clinic — those go to the emergency room. We manage the long-term care after the emergency.
- We do not prescribe growth-hormone treatment based on parental height expectations alone — it is prescribed only for confirmed growth-hormone deficiency or other approved conditions.
- We do not perform surgeries (thyroid surgery, adrenal surgery) — those are referred to a surgical specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral or emergency, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the type of emergency or specialist needed
- Contact details for the partner emergency room or specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- **For same-day emergencies (blood-sugar crisis, thyroid storm, adrenal crisis):** coordinate immediate transfer to the emergency room. Confirm by phone that the receiving team is expecting the child.
- Supply reminders (insulin refill, glucose-monitor sensor change) are manager-driven via WhatsApp at 7 days before projected stock-out.
