# SKIDS Developmental Surveillance — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Developmental Surveillance clinic monitors how children are developing across movement, language, thinking, and social skills. When a child is flagged at a screening visit, the doctor does a detailed assessment to find out whether the child is on track, slightly behind, or significantly behind — and in which areas. Based on the findings, the clinic starts early-intervention services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, physiotherapy, special education) and coordinates with therapy partners. The clinic also provides ongoing monitoring for children with known conditions such as Down syndrome or cerebral palsy. It does not diagnose autism or intellectual disability — those require specialist assessment, which the doctor arranges.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "He's just a late bloomer — he'll catch up." | "You may be right. The assessment helps us know for sure. If he is going to catch up, the test will show that. If he needs a little extra support, starting early is the best thing we can do." |
| "Therapy is too expensive / too far." | "I can help you apply for government support under the disability act. There are also centres closer to home — let me find one. We can also start with a home programme of activities you do with him every day." |
| "We don't want a label for our child." | "This assessment is not about labelling — it's about understanding what she needs. The information stays in her medical record and helps us plan the right support. You decide what to share with others." |
| "He talks fine at home." | "It's common for children to perform differently in different settings. The formal assessment gives us an objective picture across several areas of development — not just language." |
| "We're already doing everything at home." | "That's great — and we can build on that. The therapist can add specific techniques that target exactly where he needs support. What you're doing at home is the foundation." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| At-risk (enhanced surveillance) | 3 months | 7 days |
| Mild delay (single area) | 3 months (after therapy starts) | 7 days |
| Moderate delay (multiple areas) | 6 weeks (after therapy starts) | 7 days |
| Severe / global delay | Monthly for 3 months, then every 3 months | 7 days |
| Known condition monitoring (Down syndrome, cerebral palsy, etc.) | 6 months | 14 days |
| Premature baby follow-up | Corrected-age milestones at 6, 9, 12, 18, 24, 36 months | 7 days |
| Loss of skills (regression — post-specialist referral) | 2 weeks (bridge visit) | Same day |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** NICU graduates and children who have lost previously learned skills. These families get manager phone call at 7 days, plus outreach from the doctor.

---

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not diagnose autism at this clinic — we assess development and refer for specialist diagnostic assessment.
- We do not do formal IQ or intelligence testing — that requires a clinical psychologist, which we arrange.
- We do not manage cerebral palsy rehabilitation directly — we monitor progress and coordinate with the therapy team.
- We do not manage genetic conditions beyond developmental monitoring — genetic diagnosis goes to a specialist.
- We do not manage seizures or brain-related diseases — those go to a children's neurologist.
- We do not prescribe unproven developmental supplements or therapies (no nootropics, no stem-cell therapy).

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (developmental-behavioural specialist, neurologist, clinical psychologist, geneticist)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- **For loss of previously learned skills (regression):** this is urgent. The neurologist appointment must happen within 7 days. Follow up the same day to confirm the appointment is booked.
- **For NICU-graduate and premature-baby families:** these are highest-priority engagements. Missing developmental follow-up has real consequences — pursue actively.
