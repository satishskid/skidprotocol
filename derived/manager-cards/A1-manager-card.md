# SKIDS Screening — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

SKIDS Screening is the front door of every SKIDS clinic. Every child who enrols at SKIDS receives a full age-appropriate health check-up — covering growth, development, vision, hearing, oral health, behaviour, sleep, blood pressure, and vaccination status. The doctor grades each area as "no concern," "something to watch," "needs a specialty follow-up," or "needs immediate attention," and the family is guided to the right next step.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

**"We don't need a subscription. We'll just come when something is wrong."**
"That's fine — visits are individually priced too. Most families find that with the periodic check-up schedule and the typical specialty visits in childhood, the Basic bundle costs less than paying per visit. Would you like me to walk through the cost comparison for [child's name]'s expected next 12 months?"

**"Why is the Premium tier so much more than Basic?"**
"Premium is bundled for families with active multi-clinic follow-up — for example, [child's name] is heading to our nutrition clinic and our behavioural clinic over the next few months. Premium covers those visits in the bundle. If the situation simplifies later, the family can step down to Basic at the renewal."

**"We're not sure SKIDS is right for our family long-term."**
"Subscriptions are 12-month and refundable pro-rata if you switch clinics. There's no lock-in. The discount on the bundle is the trade-off for committing 12 months upfront."

**"Other clinics don't charge a subscription. Why does SKIDS?"**
"Most pediatric clinics charge per visit only. The SKIDS model bundles routine and predictable care. For families whose child uses the routine schedule — vaccinations, well-checks, allied staff follow-ups — the bundle is the simpler and cheaper structure. For families whose child is mostly seen sporadically, per-visit pricing is also available."

**"Is the bundle a discount on the clinical care, or are we paying extra?"**
"The bundle includes some things that aren't priced into per-visit fees — for example, the parent app programmes, the dietitian sessions, the longitudinal health record access. If you want a line-item comparison, I can pull it up. There's no extra charge for the clinical work; the bundling is pure operational saving."

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Child's age band | Next routine check-up | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Newborn (0-28 days) | 3-5 days, 2 weeks, 1 month | 14 days past due |
| Infant (1-12 months) | 2, 4, 6, 9, 12 months | 30 days past due |
| Toddler (12-36 months) | 15, 18, 24, 30, 36 months | 30 days past due |
| Preschool (3-6 years) | Annual (4, 5, 6 years) | 60 days past due |
| School-age (6-12 years) | At ages 7, 8, 10, 12 | 60 days past due |
| Adolescent (12-18 years) | At ages 13, 15, 17 | 60 days past due |
| "Something to watch" recheck | 3 months (or 6 months if the doctor specified) | 14 days past due |
| Specialty clinic booking | Within 2-6 weeks (depending on the area) | 7 days past due |

**Re-engagement sequence:**
- 30 days overdue: WhatsApp reminder (auto-generated).
- 60 days overdue: Phone call from clinic manager.
- 90 days overdue: Phone call from the child's named doctor.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not treat conditions at the screening visit. Treatment happens at the relevant specialty clinic.
- We do not replace emergency or sick-visit care. A child arriving with a fever or breathing difficulty is seen under the acute-visit process first.
- We do not provide the same level of assessment as a subspecialist. If the doctor identifies something that needs a specialist, the child is referred.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the type of specialist needed (e.g., "heart specialist," "kidney specialist")
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner specialist or hospital
- Urgency level: "same-day transfer," "within 7 days," or "within 4 weeks"
- Your job: coordinate the appointment, confirm it is booked, and follow up to make sure the family attended
- For same-day transfers: the doctor will have already called the receiving hospital. Your role is logistics support (transport, paperwork, parent communication).
