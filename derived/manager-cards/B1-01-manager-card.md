# SKIDS Vision Clinic --- Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Vision Clinic confirms eyesight problems flagged at the screening visit, dispenses glasses in-clinic, runs the myopia-control programme for children whose eyesight is getting worse each year, manages mild eye allergies and infections, and sends children with eye-alignment or structural problems to our children's eye specialist partner.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "She's squinting at the TV / can't see the board."
- "He holds books very close to his face."
- "Her eyes look crossed sometimes."
- "We failed the school vision screening."
- "The screening device at our last visit said refer."
- "She rubs her eyes constantly during pollen season."
- "His eye has been red and sticky for two days."
- "He says his eyes hurt after using the tablet."
- "Her glasses are getting stronger every year."
- "He has a white spot in the photo flash of his eye."
- "His eyelid has a painful swelling."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "We can buy glasses cheaper at an optical shop." | "You can. The optical shop fits to the prescription we issue. The Advanced subscription includes one frame-and-lens replacement per year and our quality is the SKIDS pediatric impact-resistant catalogue. Most families find the bundled cost is comparable to the optical-shop cost, and the prescription-update visits are included. The Rx is yours; you can fill it where you want." |
| "My child won't wear the glasses anyway." | "That's a real risk. The reason we dispense in-clinic is so we can fit the frames to the child's face today, and so the child sees the difference glasses make immediately. Adaptation in the first two weeks is normal; we follow up at six weeks specifically to address wear concerns. If wear remains a problem, we work with you on it." |
| "Atropine drops every night for years sounds extreme." | "Low-dose atropine has been studied for over a decade now in pediatric myopia; the side-effect profile at 0.01% is minimal. The alternative -- uncorrected progression -- has lifelong visual implications. The drops slow progression by about half in the typical case. If it's not working at four months, we step up; if step-up doesn't work, we route to the children's eye specialist for other options. We're not committing to forever; we're committing to four months at a time." |
| "Why can't the school doctor just do this?" | "School screening is screening -- it identifies children who need a fuller exam. A B1-01 visit is the fuller exam: cycloplegic refraction, examination, and dispensing if needed. The school identifies the question; we provide the answer." |
| "Glasses make myopia worse -- better not to wear them." | "There's a folk version of this; the evidence does not support it. Uncorrected myopia in school-age children is associated with school performance loss and adaptive squinting that adds eye strain. Wearing the right prescription does not accelerate progression -- what slows progression is the myopia-control programme. The two are separate." |

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|

| Glasses issued (first time) | 6 weeks (adaptation check) | 2 weeks past due |
| Stable glasses wearer | 12 months | 30 days past due |
| High prescription | 6 months | 14 days past due |
| Myopia-control programme (drops) | Every 4 months | 14 days past due (active drops -- high priority) |
| Eye allergy (mild/moderate) | 4 weeks if symptomatic | 14 days past due |
| Eye infection | Day 7 (phone or photo OK) | 3 days past due |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not treat crossed eyes (strabismus) or lazy eye (amblyopia) -- those go to our children's eye specialist partner.
- We do not perform any surgery or laser treatment.
- We do not fit contact lenses -- that goes to a specialist optometrist partner.
- We do not manage eye injuries -- those go to the emergency department.
- We do not manage conditions inside the eye (cataracts, retinal problems, glaucoma) -- those go to our specialist partner.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (children's eye specialist, emergency department, or neurology)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner
- Urgency level: emergency (same day), urgent (within 7 days), or routine (within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- For emergency referrals: confirm the receiving clinic has been reached by phone before the family leaves

**Special note:** A child on the myopia-control programme who misses two scheduled 4-monthly follow-ups must be contacted immediately -- the Manager Console will alert you. Active eye-drop therapy without follow-up is a clinical concern.
