# SKIDS Vaccination Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The SKIDS Vaccination Clinic delivers all recommended childhood vaccines per the IAP (Indian Academy of Pediatrics) schedule from birth through age 18. It also provides catch-up vaccination for children who have missed doses, the HPV cancer-prevention vaccine programme for boys and girls, travel vaccination, and management of any reactions after vaccination. Every vaccination visit includes a 30-minute observation period in the clinic.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "Routine vaccination visit."
- "She's behind on her vaccines."

- "We're travelling to [destination] next month."
- "He had a reaction after the last vaccine."
- "We don't want to vaccinate."
- "We want only some vaccines."
- "She's on chemotherapy — can she get vaccines?"
- "He was born premature — is the schedule different?"
- "Flu vaccine for this season."
- "School requires a vaccination certificate."
- "Booster due — which ones?"

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

**"Government vaccines are enough."**
"The government programme covers essential vaccines. The additional vaccines in your plan protect against rotavirus diarrhoea, pneumonia, chickenpox, and hepatitis A — diseases your child can still catch. Your doctor can explain which ones matter most for [child's name]."

**"Too many injections at one visit."**
"We can give up to 4 at a time safely. If you prefer fewer, we can split across visits — the catch-up calendar will show you the plan."

**"HPV vaccine — my child is too young."**
"The vaccine works best before age 15 — that is why the medical community recommends it at 9-14. It protects against cancers that can develop decades later."

**"We can't afford all of these."**
"Your Basic plan covers the government-programme vaccines at no extra cost. We can arrange those first and discuss the additional ones when you are ready."

**"I've heard vaccines cause [X]."**
"I understand your concern. Dr. [name] can discuss this with you at the next visit — they can explain what the research shows for [child's name] specifically."

**Catch-up families:** schedule additional manager follow-up at each catch-up visit to reduce dropout.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not administer unapproved or investigational vaccines.
- We do not manage severe vaccine reactions that require hospitalisation — after initial treatment in the clinic, the child is transferred to the hospital.
- We do not force families to vaccinate. If a family declines, we document the decision and continue to engage respectfully at future visits. The child remains enrolled at SKIDS.
- We do not stock BCG (the tuberculosis birth-dose vaccine) — this is given at the birthing facility. If it was missed, we arrange it through a government immunisation centre.
- We do not investigate suspected immune-system disorders — those cases are referred to a specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the type of specialist needed (e.g., "allergy specialist" for a severe vaccine-component allergy, "immune-system specialist" for a suspected immune deficiency)
- Contact details for the SKIDS partner specialist
- Urgency level: "same-day transfer" (severe allergic reaction during observation), "within 7 days," or "within 4 weeks"
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up
- For anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction) during the observation period: the doctor and nurse will manage it immediately in the clinic and arrange hospital transfer. Your role is to support logistics and parent communication.
