# SKIDS Kidney & Urology Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Kidney & Urology Clinic investigates why a child's blood pressure is high (checking whether the kidneys are the cause), diagnoses and treats urine infections, evaluates protein or blood in the urine, and manages nephrotic syndrome — a condition where the kidneys leak protein, causing puffiness around the eyes and legs. The clinic also follows up on kidney abnormalities detected on scans before birth, and provides bladder-function assessment for children referred from the Bedwetting Clinic. For serious kidney conditions or surgical problems, the clinic coordinates referral to a kidney or urology specialist.

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "It's just a urine infection — why does she need an ultrasound?" | "For children under 2 with a fever and urine infection, the ultrasound checks that the kidneys and tubes are normal. It is a painless scan — no needles, no radiation. The doctor follows national guidelines for this." |
| "Steroids for 3 months — isn't that too long?" | "The 3-month course is the standard treatment used worldwide for this condition. Stopping early makes the condition come back. The doctor tapers the dose gradually so the body adjusts. Side effects are temporary." |
| "His BP is high — does he need medicine for life?" | "In children, we first look for the cause. If the cause is treatable, the BP often normalises. Even when lifestyle changes are needed, children respond well. Medicine is only added if lifestyle alone doesn't work after 6 months." |
| "We want a second opinion from a kidney specialist." | "Absolutely — and the doctor may recommend that too. The workup we have done gives the specialist all the information they need to advise quickly." |

## Follow-up schedule (for scheduling)

| Condition | Next visit | Re-engage if missed by |
|---|---|---|
| Urine infection (on antibiotics) | 48-72 hours if not improving; urine test at 4 weeks | 1 week after treatment ends |
| Recurrent urine infections (on preventive medicine) | 4 weeks | 6 weeks |
| Nephrotic syndrome (medicine course — first 6 weeks) | Weekly (weight + urine check) | 3 days |
| Nephrotic syndrome (tapering phase) | Monthly | 2 weeks |
| Nephrotic syndrome (after medicine finished) | Monthly for 3 months, then every 3 months | 4 weeks |
| Blood pressure monitoring (lifestyle changes) | Monthly for 3 months | 6 weeks |
| Blood or protein in urine (monitoring) | 3 months | 4 months |
| Kidney abnormality from birth scan | Per ultrasound schedule | 2 weeks after scheduled scan |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child on the nephrotic syndrome medicine course. Stopping the medicine early causes the condition to return. Manager contacts family within 3 days of a missed visit. Also high-priority: children on preventive antibiotics for recurrent urine infections.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not manage advanced kidney disease or dialysis — those go to a kidney specialist centre.
- We do not perform kidney biopsies — those are done by a specialist.
- We do not manage nephrotic syndrome that does not respond to the standard medicine — that goes to a kidney specialist.
- We do not perform surgery (circumcision, testicular surgery, kidney surgery) — those go to a children's urological surgeon.
- We do not manage sudden kidney failure — that goes to the emergency room and a kidney specialist.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (kidney specialist, urological surgeon, emergency room)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency for kidney failure or severe blood pressure with symptoms; within 7 days for kidney inflammation or high blood pressure without symptoms; within 4 weeks for surgery referral, ongoing kidney issues, or bladder tests)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- For nephrotic syndrome families: proactive re-engagement during the tapering phase to ensure the medicine course is completed and any return of symptoms is caught early
