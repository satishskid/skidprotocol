# Learning Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Learning Clinic evaluates school-age children (5 years and older) who are struggling academically — with reading, writing, or maths. The doctor screens for specific learning disabilities (like dyslexia), separates learning difficulty from attention problems or emotional causes, arranges formal testing with a psychologist, and helps set up school accommodations and remedial education. The clinic does not provide tutoring or formal testing directly.

## What parents say when they arrive

- "He can't read properly for his age."
- "She reverses letters and numbers."
- "He hates school and homework."
- "Teacher says she's behind the class."
- "He understands but can't write it down."
- "Maths is impossible for her."
- "He was fine until 3rd class — now struggling."
- "ADHD diagnosed but still failing."
- "He has a lisp." (routes to Speech & Language Clinic)

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "He just needs more tuition." | "The type of teaching matters more than the amount. A remedial educator uses specific techniques designed for the way his brain processes information. That's different from regular tuition." |
| "He's just lazy / not trying hard enough." | "Children with learning disabilities often work harder than their classmates for poorer results. The assessment tells us if there's a brain-wiring difference — not a character flaw — that we can work with." |
| "We don't want a 'disability' label." | "The certification provides legal rights to accommodations — extra time in exams, a scribe — that make a real difference in board results. It levels the playing field." |
| "Will he ever be normal?" | "Many children with learning disabilities develop strong workaround strategies and do very well in school and careers. Early identification and the right support are the key." |
| "Can't the school handle this?" | "Schools do their best, but a specific learning disability needs specific teaching methods. The school letter we provide helps teachers understand what your child needs. The remedial educator adds the targeted support." |

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not perform IQ testing or formal psychometric assessments — those are done by a clinical psychologist or educational psychologist, and we coordinate the referral.
- We do not provide remedial education or tutoring — we connect your family with certified remedial educators.
- We do not treat attention-deficit concerns with medicine — that is managed at the Behavioural Clinic.
- We do not make school-placement decisions — our role is advisory.
- We do not recommend vision therapy, coloured lenses, or eye exercises for learning problems — there is no evidence these work.
