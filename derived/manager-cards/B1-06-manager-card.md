# SKIDS Allergy Clinic — Manager Card

## What this clinic does

The Allergy Clinic identifies what a child is allergic to (food, dust, pollen, insect venom, drugs) using skin-prick tests and blood tests, then builds a clear avoidance plan and an emergency action plan. For children with food allergy or a history of severe reactions, the clinic dispenses adrenaline auto-injectors and trains the family to use them. For children with persistent nasal allergy not controlled by sprays and tablets, the clinic offers a long-term desensitisation programme (sublingual immunotherapy). The clinic also co-manages eczema-with-food-triggers (with Skin Clinic) and cow's-milk-protein allergy (with Nutrition Clinic).

## What parents say when they arrive

- "She has a runny nose and sneezing for months without a cold."
- "His eyes are itchy and watery, especially during pollen season."
- "She got hives and swelling after eating peanuts / cashews / egg."
- "Every time he drinks milk, he gets stomach pain / diarrhoea / blood in stools."
- "She has eczema that flares up after certain foods."
- "He has hives that come and go for weeks."
- "She had a reaction to amoxicillin last year."
- "He was stung by a bee and had swelling all over."
- "He had anaphylaxis a year ago and we don't have an action plan."
- "She wants to outgrow her egg / milk allergy — can we test?"
- "His school nurse needs an action plan on file."
- "She was prescribed an EpiPen but we don't know how to use it."

## Top parent objections (use these scripts)

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| "We can buy the antihistamine over the counter; why come here?" | "For mild seasonal symptoms, that works. The clinic visit adds the dust-avoidance plan, the trigger-identification testing if needed, and the cross-checks with skin and lung clinics if relevant. Most families come once or twice and don't need ongoing visits — that fits Basic per-visit pricing." |
| "Auto-injectors are expensive — we'll just be careful." | "Careful avoidance prevents most reactions; it doesn't prevent all. The reason we dispense two injectors is that careful families still face accidental exposures — the injector is the bridge between exposure and hospital. The bundle covers replacement annually." |
| "Skin tests on a small child — is that necessary?" | "Skin tests give us specific information about which allergen, which we can't confirm by history alone. The procedure takes 20 minutes, hurts about as much as a sticker peel, and we always have the resuscitation kit ready. The alternative is broad avoidance based on guesswork — usually too restrictive." |
| "Desensitisation for three years feels long." | "It is. At three years of daily dosing, two-thirds of children get significant improvement that often persists after the course ends. For families committed to the daily routine, the long-term benefit is worth it. If you're not sure, we don't start." |
| "Premium is for severe cases — we're not severe enough." | "It's not about severity — it's about how often you'd use the bundle. Families with severe-reaction-risk children benefit from unlimited visits because action-plan rehearsal, annual testing, and any acute concern are bundled. If you'd see the clinic less than four times a year, Advanced is enough." |

| Mild hives (acute) | 2 weeks if persistent | 3 weeks |
| Chronic hives (active) | 4 weeks | 6 weeks |
| Desensitisation programme | 1 week (first check), then every 3 months | 2 weeks after first; 4 months after subsequent |
| Auto-injector expiry approaching | 30 days before expiry | 7 days before expiry (escalate to phone) |

**High-priority loss-to-follow-up:** A child with severe-reaction-risk allergy who misses the annual auto-injector renewal visit. Auto-injector expiry without replacement leaves the family unprotected. Contact within 30 days of due date; phone follow-up if no response.

## What we do NOT promise

- We do not treat severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) happening in the moment — those go directly to the emergency room.
- We do not offer oral immunotherapy (a programme to build tolerance to food allergens by eating increasing amounts) — that requires a specialist centre.
- We do not offer injection-based desensitisation (subcutaneous immunotherapy) — that requires specialist infrastructure.
- We do not manage rare conditions like hereditary angioedema or mast-cell disease — those go to a specialist.
- We do not manage severe chronic hives that don't respond to standard treatment — those need specialist medicines.

## Escalation

If the doctor flags a specialist referral, the Manager Console will show:
- Child's name and the specialist type needed (allergy specialist, ENT, emergency room, or gastroenterologist)
- Contact details for the partner specialist
- Urgency level (same-day emergency, within 7 days, or within 4 weeks)
- Your job: coordinate the appointment and follow up within the stated timeframe
- For auto-injector expiry alerts: ensure replacement is scheduled before the device expires
